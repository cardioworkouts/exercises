How To Workout Core With Weights A Beginner s Guide

Written by Frank Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for ways to challenge your core muscles during your workout routine? Adding weights to your core exercises is a great way to increase intensity and build strength. In this guide, we'll explore the benefits of core workouts with weights, how to perform the exercises correctly, and some tips for beginners.

Why should you workout core with weights?

Core muscles are responsible for stabilizing your body and supporting your spine. A strong core can improve your posture, balance, and overall athletic performance. While bodyweight exercises like planks and sit-ups are effective for building core strength, adding weights can increase the resistance and make the exercises more challenging.

How to workout core with weights?

Before starting any workout routine, it's important to warm up your muscles and stretch to prevent injuries. Once you're ready, here are some effective core exercises with weights:

1. Weighted Russian Twist

Start by sitting on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground. Hold a dumbbell or a medicine ball with both hands and lift it to your chest. Lean back slightly and twist your torso to the right, then to the left. Repeat for 10-12 reps. This exercise targets the obliques and improves rotational strength.

2. Weighted Plank

Assume a plank position with your forearms on the ground and your body in a straight line. Place a weight plate on your upper back and hold it with your hands. Hold the position for 30-60 seconds, then rest and repeat for 3 sets. This exercise strengthens the entire core and improves stability.

3. Weighted Bicycle Crunch

Lie on your back with your knees bent and your hands behind your head. Lift your shoulders off the ground and bring your right knee towards your chest while extending your left leg. Twist your torso to the right and touch your left elbow to your right knee. Switch sides and repeat for 10-12 reps. This exercise targets the rectus abdominis and obliques.

4. Weighted Side Plank

Lie on your side with your forearm on the ground and your feet stacked on top of each other. Hold a dumbbell or a kettlebell in your top hand and lift your hips off the ground. Hold the position for 30-60 seconds, then switch sides and repeat for 3 sets. This exercise targets the obliques and improves lateral stability.

Tips for beginners

If you're new to working out with weights, start with light weights and focus on proper form. Gradually increase the weight as you get stronger, but don't compromise your technique. Also, don't forget to engage your core muscles during the exercises and breathe deeply. Lastly, it's important to rest and recover between workouts to prevent overtraining.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do these exercises at home without equipment?

A: Yes, you can modify these exercises by using household items like water bottles or cans as weights. You can also do bodyweight versions of these exercises, like regular planks or bicycle crunches.

Q: How often should I workout my core?

A: It's recommended to do core workouts 2-3 times a week, with at least one day of rest in between. Make sure to incorporate other types of exercises, like cardio and strength training, in your routine as well.

Q: Will working out my core help me lose belly fat?

A: While core workouts can strengthen your abdominal muscles, they won't specifically target belly fat. To lose fat, you need to maintain a calorie deficit through a balanced diet and regular exercise.

Q: Can I do these exercises if I have back pain?

A: It's best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any exercise routine if you have back pain. Some of these exercises may exacerbate your symptoms, so it's important to modify them or avoid them altogether.

Conclusion of how to workout core with weights

Working out your core with weights is a great way to challenge your muscles and improve your overall fitness. By incorporating these exercises into your routine, you can increase your core strength, stability, and balance. Just remember to start slowly, focus on proper form, and listen to your body. Happy lifting!
