How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training

Written by Aprilia Oct 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly to keep yourself healthy and in shape? If yes, then you must be aware of the importance of cardio and weight training in your fitness routine. However, do you know how much cardio you should do when weight training? If not, don't worry, you're not alone.

Pain Points Related to How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training

It can be challenging to determine how much cardio you should do when weight training, especially if you're new to fitness. Doing too much cardio can hamper your muscle-building efforts, while doing too little can hinder your weight loss goals. Additionally, your fitness goals and the duration and intensity of your weight training sessions also play a crucial role in determining how much cardio you need.

Answer to How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training

The general rule of thumb is to do 150 minutes of moderate cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio per week, along with two to three strength training sessions. However, this is a generic recommendation and may not be suitable for everyone. It's best to consult a fitness professional who can assess your fitness level and guide you on how much cardio you need to do when weight training.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, determining how much cardio you should do when weight training depends on various factors, including your fitness goals, weight training routine, and fitness level. A fitness professional can guide you on the appropriate amount of cardio you need to do to achieve your fitness goals.

How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training: Personal Experience

As someone who loves to lift weights and do cardio, I struggled to find the right balance between the two. Initially, I did a lot of cardio, thinking it would help me lose weight faster. However, I soon realized that I was losing muscle mass and not achieving the results I desired. I consulted a fitness professional who suggested reducing my cardio and increasing my strength training sessions. This helped me achieve my fitness goals and maintain my weight and muscle mass.

It's essential to understand that everyone's fitness journey is different, and there's no one-size-fits-all approach. It's best to experiment, listen to your body, and seek professional guidance to determine how much cardio you need to do when weight training.

Factors That Determine How Much Cardio You Should Do When Weight Training

Several factors determine how much cardio you should do when weight training. These include:

1. Fitness Goals

Your fitness goals play a crucial role in determining how much cardio you need to do when weight training. If you're looking to lose weight, you may need to do more cardio than someone who wants to gain muscle mass.

2. Duration and Intensity of Your Weight Training Sessions

The duration and intensity of your weight training sessions also impact how much cardio you should do. If you're doing high-intensity weight training sessions, you may need to do less cardio than someone who's doing low-intensity weight training sessions.

3. Fitness Level

Your fitness level also plays a crucial role in determining how much cardio you should do when weight training. Someone who's new to fitness may need to do less cardio than someone who's been training for a while.

How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training: Tips to Remember

Here are some tips to keep in mind when determining how much cardio you need to do when weight training:

  • Consult a fitness professional to assess your fitness level and guide you on the appropriate amount of cardio you need to do.
  • Experiment with different amounts of cardio to determine what works best for you.
  • Listen to your body and adjust your cardio accordingly.
  • Remember that your fitness journey is unique, and there's no one-size-fits-all approach.

Question and Answer

Q: Can doing too much cardio hinder my muscle-building efforts?

A: Yes, doing too much cardio can hamper your muscle-building efforts, as it can lead to muscle loss and hinder your recovery after weight training sessions.

Q: How much cardio should I do if I'm looking to lose weight?

A: If you're looking to lose weight, you may need to do more cardio than someone who wants to gain muscle mass. However, the amount of cardio you need to do depends on several factors, including your fitness level, weight training routine, and fitness goals. It's best to consult a fitness professional to guide you on the appropriate amount of cardio you need to do.

Q: Can doing too little cardio hinder my weight loss goals?

A: Yes, doing too little cardio can hinder your weight loss goals, as cardio helps you burn calories and lose weight. However, it's essential to strike a balance between cardio and weight training to achieve your fitness goals.

Q: How often should I do cardio when weight training?

A: The general recommendation is to do 150 minutes of moderate cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio per week, along with two to three strength training sessions. However, the amount of cardio you need to do depends on several factors, including your fitness level, weight training routine, and fitness goals. It's best to consult a fitness professional to guide you on the appropriate amount of cardio you need to do.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training

Determining how much cardio you should do when weight training can be challenging, as it depends on several factors. However, by consulting a fitness professional, experimenting, and listening to your body, you can determine the appropriate amount of cardio you need to achieve your fitness goals. Remember, there's no one-size-fits-all approach, and it's essential to find a balance between cardio and weight training to maintain a healthy and fit lifestyle.
