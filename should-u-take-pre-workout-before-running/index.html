Should You Take Pre Workout Before Running A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a runner looking to boost your performance? Have you ever considered taking pre-workout before hitting the road or trail? While many people associate pre-workout supplements with weight lifting and bodybuilding, they can also be beneficial for endurance athletes like runners. However, before you start chugging down a pre-workout drink, it's important to understand the potential benefits and drawbacks.

Potential Pain Points

If you've ever taken pre-workout before, you may be familiar with some of the potential side effects. These can include jitters, racing heart, and difficulty sleeping. For runners, these side effects could lead to decreased performance, as well as potential safety concerns if you feel dizzy or lightheaded while running. Additionally, pre-workout supplements can be expensive and may not be worth the cost if you don't see significant improvements in your running.

The Answer to Should You Take Pre-Workout Before Running

The answer to whether or not you should take pre-workout before running depends on your individual goals and preferences. If you're looking to boost your energy and focus during a long run or race, pre-workout supplements could be beneficial. However, it's important to start with a low dose and pay attention to how your body reacts. If you experience negative side effects, it may not be worth the potential benefits.

Additionally, it's important to choose a pre-workout supplement designed for endurance athletes, rather than weight lifters. These supplements will typically have ingredients like caffeine and beta-alanine, which can boost endurance and delay muscle fatigue, without the heavy stimulants that could be detrimental to runners.

Personal Experience and Explanation

As a long-distance runner, I've tried taking pre-workout supplements before some of my runs and races. I've found that they can be helpful in giving me an extra boost of energy and focus when I need it most. However, I've also experienced negative side effects like jitters and heart palpitations, which have made me hesitant to take pre-workout on a regular basis.

When choosing a pre-workout supplement, I look for ones that are specifically designed for endurance athletes and have lower doses of stimulants. I also make sure to start with a half dose and gradually increase as needed.

Benefits of Taking Pre-Workout Before Running

Some of the potential benefits of taking pre-workout before running include increased energy and focus, improved endurance, and delayed muscle fatigue. Additionally, pre-workout supplements can help you push through mental barriers and achieve a new personal best.

Ingredients to Look for in Pre-Workout Supplements for Runners

When choosing a pre-workout supplement for running, look for ingredients like caffeine, beta-alanine, and citrulline malate. These ingredients have been shown to improve endurance, delay muscle fatigue, and increase blood flow to the muscles. Avoid supplements with heavy stimulants like yohimbine or synephrine, which can be dangerous for runners.

How to Take Pre-Workout for Running

If you decide to take pre-workout before running, it's important to start with a low dose and pay attention to how your body reacts. Take the supplement about 20-30 minutes before your run, and make sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated. Avoid taking pre-workout late in the day, as it could interfere with your sleep.

Personal Experience and Explanation

As someone who has experienced negative side effects from pre-workout supplements, I would caution others to start with a low dose and pay attention to their body's reactions. It's also important to consider the cost and potential risks before investing in a pre-workout supplement. While they can be beneficial for some runners, they may not be worth the expense or potential side effects for everyone.

Question and Answer

Q: Can pre-workout supplements help me run faster?

A: Pre-workout supplements can help improve endurance and delay muscle fatigue, which could result in faster overall times. However, they may not have an immediate impact on your speed.

Q: Are there any risks to taking pre-workout before running?

A: Some potential risks include negative side effects like jitters or heart palpitations, as well as potential safety concerns if you become dizzy or lightheaded while running. It's important to start with a low dose and pay attention to how your body reacts.

Q: Can I drink coffee instead of pre-workout before running?

A: Yes, coffee can be a natural alternative to pre-workout supplements. However, be mindful of the amount of caffeine you consume to avoid negative side effects.

Q: Should I take pre-workout before every run?

A: It's not necessary to take pre-workout before every run. Reserve it for longer runs or races when you need an extra boost of energy and focus.

Conclusion of Should You Take Pre-Workout Before Running

Pre-workout supplements can be a helpful tool for runners looking to boost their endurance and performance. However, it's important to start with a low dose and pay attention to how your body reacts. Look for supplements designed specifically for endurance athletes, and avoid heavy stimulants that could be detrimental to your running. Ultimately, the decision to take pre-workout before running is a personal one that depends on your individual goals and preferences.
