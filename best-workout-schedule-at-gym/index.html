60 Tips Best Workout Schedule At Gym For Everyday

Written by Robby Nov 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to figure out the best workout schedule at the gym? Do you find yourself wandering aimlessly and not making progress towards your fitness goals? Look no further, because this article will provide you with the ultimate guide to crafting a workout schedule that works for you.

Working out can be overwhelming, especially when you’re not sure where to start. It’s easy to fall into the trap of hopping from machine to machine or doing random exercises without a clear plan in mind. This can lead to frustration and lack of progress.

The key to a successful workout schedule is to have a clear understanding of your fitness goals and then tailor your workouts to meet those goals. Whether you’re looking to lose weight, build muscle, or just improve your overall fitness level, there’s a workout schedule that will work for you.

In this article, we’ll go over the best workout schedule at the gym and provide tips and strategies for creating a schedule that will help you achieve your fitness goals.

Full Body Workout Schedule

A full body workout schedule is a great option for beginners or those who are short on time. This type of workout involves targeting all major muscle groups in one session, usually two to three times a week. It’s a great way to get a full body workout without spending hours in the gym.

For example, your full body workout schedule might include squats, lunges, bench press, pull-ups, and planks. You would perform each exercise for a certain number of reps or time and then move on to the next exercise. This type of workout can be done with or without weights, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Split Workout Schedule

A split workout schedule involves targeting specific muscle groups on different days of the week. This allows for more focused workouts and can be especially beneficial for those looking to build muscle mass.

For example, your split workout schedule might include chest and triceps on Monday, back and biceps on Wednesday, and legs and shoulders on Friday. This allows you to really focus on each muscle group and give them the attention they need to grow and develop.

Circuit Training Workout Schedule

Circuit training is a type of workout that involves performing a series of exercises back-to-back with little to no rest in between. This type of workout is great for those looking to improve their cardiovascular fitness and burn a lot of calories in a short amount of time.

For example, your circuit training workout schedule might include jumping jacks, push-ups, squats, and burpees. You would perform each exercise for a certain number of reps or time and then move on to the next exercise without resting. This type of workout can be done with or without weights, depending on your fitness level and goals.

HIIT Workout Schedule

HIIT, or high-intensity interval training, is a type of workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of workout is great for those looking to improve their cardiovascular fitness and burn a lot of calories in a short amount of time.

For example, your HIIT workout schedule might include sprinting for 30 seconds followed by 30 seconds of rest, jumping jacks for 30 seconds followed by 30 seconds of rest, and burpees for 30 seconds followed by 30 seconds of rest. You would repeat this cycle for a certain amount of time or number of rounds.

Question and Answer

Q: How many days a week should I work out?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. Generally, it’s recommended to work out at least three to four days a week for optimal results.

Q: Should I do cardio or strength training first?

A: It’s a matter of personal preference, but it’s generally recommended to do strength training first and then cardio. This allows you to have more energy for your strength training exercises.

Q: How long should my workouts be?

A: Again, it depends on your fitness goals and schedule. Generally, workouts should be at least 30 minutes to an hour long.

Q: Can I do the same workout every day?

A: It’s not recommended to do the same workout every day, as your body needs time to rest and recover. It’s important to switch up your workouts and target different muscle groups.

Conclusion of Best Workout Schedule at the Gym

Creating a workout schedule can be overwhelming, but it’s essential for achieving your fitness goals. Whether you choose a full body workout schedule, split workout schedule, circuit training workout schedule, or HIIT workout schedule, the key is to have a clear understanding of your fitness goals and tailor your workouts to meet those goals. Remember to switch up your workouts and give your body time to rest and recover. With dedication and consistency, you can achieve your fitness goals and live a healthier, happier life.
