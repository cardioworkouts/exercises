Does Aerobic Exercise Build Muscle

Written by Aprilia Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who enjoys running, cycling, or other forms of aerobic exercise but also wants to build muscle? Many people have the misconception that aerobic exercise only helps with cardiovascular health and endurance, but not muscle building. However, this is not entirely true. In this article, we will explore the question: does aerobic exercise build muscle?

When it comes to building muscle, many people think of weightlifting and resistance training. While these forms of exercise are certainly effective for muscle building, aerobic exercise can also play a role in building muscle. The key is to understand the different types of muscle fibers and how they respond to different types of exercise.

So, does aerobic exercise build muscle? The answer is yes, but not in the same way that weightlifting does. Aerobic exercise primarily works the slow-twitch muscle fibers, which are responsible for endurance and stamina. These fibers have a high capacity for energy production and can sustain activity for long periods of time. While slow-twitch fibers don't hypertrophy (grow in size) as much as fast-twitch fibers, they can still increase in size with consistent aerobic exercise.

In summary, while aerobic exercise may not be the most efficient way to build muscle, it can still contribute to muscle growth, especially in slow-twitch muscle fibers.

My Personal Experience with Aerobic Exercise and Muscle Building

As a long-distance runner, I have always prioritized aerobic exercise over weightlifting. However, I have still seen an increase in muscle mass, particularly in my legs. This is likely due to the consistent high-intensity running that I do, which works my slow-twitch muscle fibers. While my muscles may not be as big as someone who lifts weights regularly, I have still noticed a difference in my muscle tone and definition.

The Role of Nutrition in Aerobic Exercise and Muscle Building

When it comes to building muscle, nutrition plays a crucial role. This is true for both weightlifting and aerobic exercise. In order to build muscle, your body needs an adequate amount of protein to repair and rebuild muscle tissue. It's also important to consume enough calories to support muscle growth. Additionally, carbohydrates are important for providing energy during aerobic exercise and can help prevent muscle breakdown.

The Benefits of Combining Aerobic Exercise and Weightlifting

While aerobic exercise can contribute to muscle growth, combining it with weightlifting can be even more effective. This is because weightlifting primarily works the fast-twitch muscle fibers, which have a greater potential for hypertrophy. By incorporating both types of exercise into your routine, you can target both types of muscle fibers and see greater overall muscle growth.

Tips for Incorporating Aerobic Exercise into Your Muscle Building Routine

If you want to incorporate aerobic exercise into your muscle building routine, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, prioritize weightlifting and resistance training, but don't neglect aerobic exercise completely. Aim for 2-3 days of aerobic exercise per week, such as running or cycling. Additionally, make sure you are consuming enough protein and calories to support muscle growth. Finally, consider incorporating high-intensity interval training (HIIT) into your routine, which can be a more efficient way to work your slow-twitch muscle fibers and improve cardiovascular health.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you build muscle with just aerobic exercise?

A: While aerobic exercise can contribute to muscle growth, it is not the most efficient way to build muscle. Combining aerobic exercise with weightlifting and resistance training is the most effective way to build muscle.

Q: How often should I do aerobic exercise if I want to build muscle?

A: Aim for 2-3 days of aerobic exercise per week, in addition to weightlifting and resistance training.

Q: Can I build muscle with low-intensity aerobic exercise, such as walking?

A: While low-intensity aerobic exercise can be beneficial for overall health, it is not as effective for building muscle as higher-intensity exercise.

Q: Is nutrition important for building muscle with aerobic exercise?

A: Yes, nutrition plays a crucial role in building muscle with aerobic exercise. Make sure you are consuming enough protein and calories to support muscle growth.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while aerobic exercise may not be the most efficient way to build muscle, it can still play a role in muscle growth, particularly in slow-twitch muscle fibers. By combining aerobic exercise with weightlifting and resistance training, and prioritizing proper nutrition, you can achieve your muscle building goals and improve overall health and fitness.
