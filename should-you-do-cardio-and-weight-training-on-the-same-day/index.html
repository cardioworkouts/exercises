Should You Do Cardio And Weight Training On The Same Day

Written by William Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who struggles to find time for both cardio and weight training in your busy schedule? Or are you just looking to maximize the benefits of your workouts? Whatever the reason, you may be wondering if it's a good idea to do cardio and weight training on the same day. In this post, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of this approach, and help you decide whether it's right for you.

Why You Might Be Asking This Question

If you're considering doing cardio and weight training on the same day, chances are you're looking to improve your fitness level, lose weight, or build muscle. Both cardio and weight training can help you achieve these goals, but they each target different parts of your body and use different energy systems. This means that doing both types of exercise in the same workout can be challenging, and may not be the most effective approach for everyone.

The Answer: It Depends

The short answer to whether you should do cardio and weight training on the same day is that it depends on your goals, your fitness level, and your schedule. There are benefits and drawbacks to both approaches, and ultimately the best choice is the one that fits your needs and lifestyle.

Benefits and Drawbacks of Doing Cardio and Weight Training on the Same Day

One of the main benefits of combining cardio and weight training in the same workout is that you can save time. If you only have a limited amount of time to exercise each day, doing both types of exercise in the same session can allow you to get a complete workout without spending extra time at the gym. Additionally, combining cardio and weight training can help you burn more calories during your workout, which can be beneficial if you're trying to lose weight.

However, there are also some drawbacks to doing cardio and weight training on the same day. For one thing, it can be difficult to do both types of exercise at a high intensity in the same workout. This is because weight training requires a lot of energy and focus, which can be hard to maintain if you're also trying to do intense cardio. Additionally, combining cardio and weight training can increase your risk of injury, particularly if you're not used to doing both types of exercise in the same workout.

My Personal Experience

As a personal trainer, I often get asked whether clients should do cardio and weight training on the same day. In my experience, the answer really does depend on the individual. For some people, doing both types of exercise in the same workout can be a great way to save time and maximize results. For others, it can be too challenging and may lead to burnout or injury. The key is to listen to your body and do what feels right for you.

Tips for Combining Cardio and Weight Training

If you do decide to do cardio and weight training on the same day, there are some tips to keep in mind. Firstly, make sure you warm up properly before starting your workout, and don't skip your cool down either. Secondly, try to alternate between cardio and weight training exercises, rather than doing them all at once. This can help you maintain your energy and focus throughout your workout. Finally, be sure to stay hydrated and fuel your body with the right nutrients before and after your workout.

The Science Behind Cardio and Weight Training

Cardio and weight training use different energy systems in the body, which is why combining the two can be challenging. Cardio exercise primarily uses the aerobic energy system, which relies on oxygen to produce energy. Weight training, on the other hand, uses the anaerobic energy system, which relies on stored energy in the muscles. By doing both types of exercise in the same workout, you're asking your body to switch back and forth between these two energy systems, which can be difficult.

How to Decide Whether to Do Cardio and Weight Training on the Same Day

If you're still unsure whether you should do cardio and weight training on the same day, ask yourself these questions:

  1. What are my fitness goals?
  2. How much time do I have to exercise?
  3. Am I already comfortable doing both types of exercise?
  4. What is my current fitness level?

By answering these questions, you'll be able to determine whether doing cardio and weight training on the same day is the right choice for you.

Conclusion

In the end, the decision of whether to do cardio and weight training on the same day comes down to your individual needs and goals. While there are benefits and drawbacks to both approaches, the most important thing is to listen to your body and do what feels right for you. Whether you choose to do cardio and weight training separately or together, the most important thing is to stay consistent and keep pushing yourself towards your fitness goals.
