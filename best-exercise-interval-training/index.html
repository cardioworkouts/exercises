Best Exercise Interval Training A Beginner s Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a way to burn fat, build muscle, and get in shape, then exercise interval training might be the answer. Interval training is a type of workout that alternates between high-intensity and low-intensity exercises, which can help you get the most out of your workout in a shorter amount of time.

Are you tired of spending long hours at the gym without seeing significant results? Do you feel like you've hit a plateau in your fitness journey? If so, then exercise interval training might be the solution you've been looking for. Interval training is designed to push your body to its limits, helping you to burn more calories and build more muscle in less time.

The target of best exercise interval training is to increase your heart rate and metabolism, allowing your body to burn more calories during and after your workout. This type of workout is also great for improving cardiovascular health, endurance, and overall fitness. Some of the best exercises for interval training include running, cycling, jumping jacks, and burpees.

In summary, best exercise interval training is a type of workout that alternates between high-intensity and low-intensity exercises, designed to help you burn more calories and build more muscle in less time. It's a great way to push your body to its limits and achieve your fitness goals.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is one of the most popular forms of exercise interval training. It involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise, followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. HIIT can be done with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, and weightlifting.

Personally, I love doing HIIT workouts because they are challenging and effective. I usually start with a warm-up of jumping jacks or jogging in place, followed by 30 seconds of high-intensity exercise (such as burpees or mountain climbers), followed by 30 seconds of rest or low-intensity exercise (such as jogging or walking). I repeat this cycle for 20-30 minutes, and I always feel accomplished at the end of my workout.

Tabata Training

Tabata Training is another form of exercise interval training that involves 20 seconds of high-intensity exercise, followed by 10 seconds of rest, repeated for a total of 8 rounds (4 minutes). Tabata can be done with a variety of exercises, including squats, push-ups, and jumping jacks.

Personally, I find Tabata to be a great way to challenge myself and improve my endurance. I usually choose 2-3 exercises and do 4 rounds of each, with a 1-minute rest in between. By the end of my workout, I'm usually exhausted but proud of myself for pushing through.

Circuit Training

Circuit Training is another form of exercise interval training that involves a series of exercises done in a specific order, with little or no rest in between. Circuit training can be done with a variety of exercises, including bodyweight exercises, weightlifting, and cardio.

Circuit training is one of my favorite forms of exercise interval training because it allows me to work on different muscle groups while keeping my heart rate up. I usually choose 5-6 exercises and do 2-3 rounds of each, with a 1-minute rest in between rounds. By the end of my workout, I feel like I've worked out my entire body and challenged myself in new ways.

Tips for Best Exercise Interval Training

1. Start slow: If you're new to exercise interval training, start with shorter intervals and lower intensity exercises. As you get stronger and more comfortable, you can gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

2. Mix it up: Don't do the same workout every day. Mix up your exercises and intervals to keep your body challenged and prevent boredom.

3. Listen to your body: If you feel pain or discomfort during your workout, stop and rest. Don't push yourself too hard, too fast.

4. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated and prevent injury.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do exercise interval training?

A: It's recommended to do exercise interval training 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts.

Q: Can exercise interval training help me lose weight?

A: Yes, exercise interval training can help you burn more calories and lose weight, especially if combined with a healthy diet.

Q: What are some benefits of exercise interval training?

A: Exercise interval training can help improve cardiovascular health, build muscle, increase endurance, and burn more calories in less time.

Q: Is exercise interval training suitable for beginners?

A: Yes, exercise interval training can be modified for beginners, and it's a great way to challenge yourself and improve your fitness level.

Conclusion of Best Exercise Interval Training

Best exercise interval training is a great way to challenge yourself, improve your fitness level, and achieve your fitness goals. Whether you prefer high-intensity interval training, Tabata training, or circuit training, there are plenty of options to choose from. Remember to start slow, mix it up, listen to your body, and stay hydrated. With a little consistency and dedication, you can achieve the results you've been dreaming of.
