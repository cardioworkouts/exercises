Fundamental Exercise Examples A Beginner s Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you new to fitness and looking for some guidance on where to start with exercise? Or maybe you've been working out for a while but feel like you're not seeing the results you want. Whatever your situation, incorporating fundamental exercise examples into your routine can help you build a strong foundation for your fitness journey.

Fundamental exercises are movements that work multiple muscle groups and improve overall strength and stability. They are essential for building a strong base and preventing injuries. If you're not doing fundamental exercises, you may be missing out on some key benefits.

In this article, we will cover some fundamental exercise examples and why they are important for your fitness routine.

Squats

Squats are a classic fundamental exercise that targets your lower body, including your glutes, quads, and hamstrings. They also work your core and improve your balance and stability. To perform a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing slightly outward. Lower down as if you're sitting in a chair, keeping your knees in line with your toes. Push back up through your heels to return to standing.

When I first started working out, I struggled with squats because I didn't have the proper form. But once I learned how to engage my core and push through my heels, I noticed a huge improvement in my overall strength and stability.

Push-ups

Push-ups are another classic fundamental exercise that targets your chest, shoulders, triceps, and core. They also improve your posture and help prevent shoulder injuries. To perform a push-up, start in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower down until your chest touches the ground, keeping your elbows close to your body. Push back up to the starting position.

When I first started doing push-ups, I could barely do one. But by practicing consistently and modifying the exercise when necessary, I was able to build up my strength and eventually do multiple sets of push-ups.

Planks

Planks are a great fundamental exercise for targeting your core muscles. They also improve your posture and stability. To perform a plank, start in a push-up position and lower down onto your forearms. Keep your body in a straight line from your head to your heels, engaging your core muscles. Hold for as long as you can without breaking form.

Planks were a challenging exercise for me at first because I had weak core muscles. But by practicing consistently and gradually increasing my hold time, I was able to build up my core strength and improve my overall form.

Lunges

Lunges are a fundamental exercise that targets your lower body, including your glutes, quads, and hamstrings. They also improve your balance and stability. To perform a lunge, step forward with one foot and lower down until your back knee is almost touching the ground. Keep your front knee in line with your toes. Push back up to standing and repeat on the other side.

When I first started doing lunges, I struggled with balance and keeping my form. But by practicing consistently and focusing on my form, I was able to improve my overall strength and stability.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do fundamental exercises?

A: It's recommended to incorporate fundamental exercises into your routine at least two to three times per week.

Q: Can I modify fundamental exercises if I can't do them with proper form?

A: Yes, it's better to modify the exercise than to do it with poor form and risk injury. For example, you can do push-ups on your knees or use a resistance band for squats.

Q: Will I see results from doing fundamental exercises?

A: Yes, incorporating fundamental exercises into your routine can improve your overall strength, stability, and posture. You may also notice improvements in your balance and coordination.

Q: Can I do fundamental exercises at home without equipment?

A: Yes, most fundamental exercises can be done at home without equipment. For example, you can do bodyweight squats, lunges, push-ups, and planks.

Conclusion of Fundamental Exercise Examples

Incorporating fundamental exercise examples into your routine can help you build a strong foundation for your fitness journey. By targeting multiple muscle groups and improving overall strength and stability, fundamental exercises can help prevent injuries and improve your posture and coordination. Start with the exercises outlined in this article and gradually increase the difficulty as you get stronger.
