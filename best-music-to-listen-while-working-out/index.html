Best Music To Listen While Working Out A Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 02, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of feeling unmotivated during your workouts? Do you find yourself struggling to push through the last few reps? The solution may be simpler than you think. Choosing the right music to listen to while working out can make all the difference in your energy and motivation levels.

Many people don't realize the impact that music can have on their workout. It can help you focus, increase your endurance, and even distract you from the physical discomfort of exercising. But with so many options out there, how do you choose the best music to listen to while working out?

Target: Best Music for Cardio Workouts

When it comes to cardio workouts, the best music to listen to is something with a fast tempo and a strong beat. This will help you keep pace with your workout and maintain a steady rhythm. Personally, I love listening to upbeat pop or EDM music while doing cardio. Some great options include:

  • "Blinding Lights" by The Weeknd
  • "Don't Start Now" by Dua Lipa
  • "Levels" by Avicii
  • "Can't Stop" by Red Hot Chili Peppers

These songs have a BPM (beats per minute) of around 120-140, which is perfect for running, cycling, or any other high-intensity cardio workout.

Target: Best Music for Strength Training

When it comes to strength training, the best music to listen to is something with a slower tempo and a heavier beat. This will help you maintain a controlled pace and focus on your form. Personally, I love listening to rock or hip-hop music while lifting weights. Some great options include:

  • "Enter Sandman" by Metallica
  • "Lose Yourself" by Eminem
  • "Thunderstruck" by AC/DC
  • "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

These songs have a BPM of around 80-100, which is perfect for slower, controlled movements like lifting weights or doing bodyweight exercises.

Best Music for Yoga and Pilates

For yoga and Pilates, the best music to listen to is something calming and relaxing. This will help you focus on your breath and movements, without getting distracted by an upbeat tempo. Personally, I love listening to instrumental music or nature sounds while doing yoga or Pilates. Some great options include:

  • "Weightless" by Marconi Union
  • "Moonlight Sonata" by Beethoven
  • "Ocean Waves" by Relaxing White Noise
  • "Ave Maria" by Schubert

These songs have a slow tempo and minimal lyrics, allowing you to fully relax and focus on your movements.

How to Choose the Best Music to Listen While Working Out

The best way to choose the right music for your workout is to experiment with different genres and tempos. Start by creating a playlist of your favorite songs, and then try them out during different types of workouts. Pay attention to how the music makes you feel and how it affects your energy levels.

Another option is to use a workout app or website that provides pre-made playlists for different types of workouts. This can be a great way to discover new music that you might not have considered before.

Target: Best Music for a Cool-Down

After finishing a workout, it's important to take a few minutes to cool down and stretch. This is a great time to listen to some calming music that will help you relax and recover. Personally, I love listening to classical music or instrumental covers of popular songs during my cool-down. Some great options include:

  • "Clair de Lune" by Debussy
  • "Canon in D" by Pachelbel
  • "All of Me" by The Piano Guys
  • "Hallelujah" by Vitamin String Quartet

These songs have a slow tempo and minimal lyrics, making them perfect for winding down after a workout.

Question and Answer:

Q: How important is the tempo of the music?

A: The tempo of the music can have a big impact on your workout. Faster tempos are great for high-intensity cardio workouts, while slower tempos are better for strength training or yoga. However, it's important to choose music that you enjoy listening to, regardless of the tempo.

Q: Can listening to music during a workout improve performance?

A: Yes, listening to music during a workout can improve performance by increasing motivation, reducing fatigue, and improving focus. It can also help distract you from physical discomfort or boredom.

Q: Is it better to listen to familiar music or new music during a workout?

A: It's a matter of personal preference, but some studies suggest that listening to familiar music can be more effective during a workout. This is because familiar music can provide a sense of comfort and familiarity, which can increase motivation and reduce anxiety.

Q: Can the volume of the music affect the effectiveness of a workout?

A: Yes, the volume of the music can affect the effectiveness of a workout. Studies have shown that moderate volume levels can improve performance and motivation, while high volume levels can be distracting and increase fatigue.

Conclusion of Best Music to Listen While Working Out

Choosing the right music to listen to while working out can make a big difference in your energy, motivation, and overall performance. Experiment with different genres and tempos to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to try new things. With the right music, you can take your workouts to the next level.
