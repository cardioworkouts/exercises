What Are Interval Training Workouts

Written by Bardi Aug 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Do you want to burn fat, improve your cardiovascular health, and increase your endurance? If yes, then interval training workouts might be the right fit for you. Interval training workouts are a type of exercise that alternates between high-intensity and low-intensity periods, making it an effective way to maximize your workout.

For some people, traditional workouts can become monotonous and boring. Going to the gym and running on the treadmill for hours can become a tedious task. Moreover, some people find that they hit a plateau and can't seem to lose any more weight or increase their endurance. That's where interval training workouts come in.

What are Interval Training Workouts?

Interval training workouts consist of two types of intervals: high-intensity and low-intensity. High-intensity intervals are where you push yourself to your maximum heart rate for a short period, while low-intensity intervals allow you to recover.

For example, if you're running, you can alternate between sprinting at maximum effort for 30 seconds, then walking or jogging for 60 seconds. The high-intensity intervals push your body to its limits, while the low-intensity intervals allow your body to recover and prepare for the next high-intensity interval.

The Benefits of Interval Training Workouts

I personally have experienced the benefits of interval training workouts. I used to run for hours on the treadmill, but I never saw any significant changes in my body. However, when I started incorporating interval training workouts into my routine, I noticed that my body was changing, and my endurance was increasing.

Interval training workouts have numerous benefits, including:

  • Burns more calories in a shorter amount of time
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Increases endurance
  • Boosts metabolism
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and diabetes

Types of Interval Training Workouts

There are many types of interval training workouts that you can incorporate into your routine. Some popular ones include:

  • Tabata
  • HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)
  • Pyramid Interval Training
  • Little Method (Maximal Aerobic Speed Training)

How to Incorporate Interval Training Workouts into Your Routine

If you're interested in trying interval training workouts, it's essential to start slow and gradually increase your intensity. Here are some tips on how to incorporate interval training workouts into your routine:

  • Start with a warm-up of 5-10 minutes of low-intensity exercise
  • Alternate between high-intensity and low-intensity intervals
  • Gradually increase the intensity of your high-intensity intervals
  • End with a cool-down of 5-10 minutes of low-intensity exercise

FAQs about Interval Training Workouts

What are the best exercises for interval training workouts?

You can do interval training workouts with any exercise, including running, cycling, swimming, or strength training.

Can beginners do interval training workouts?

Yes, beginners can do interval training workouts. It's essential to start slow and gradually increase your intensity.

How often should I do interval training workouts?

You can do interval training workouts 2-3 times a week, depending on your fitness level and goals.

How long should an interval training workout be?

An interval training workout can last anywhere from 20-45 minutes, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of What are Interval Training Workouts

Interval training workouts are an effective way to burn fat, improve your cardiovascular health, and increase your endurance. By incorporating high-intensity and low-intensity intervals into your routine, you can push your body to its limits and see significant changes in your body. Remember to start slow, gradually increase your intensity, and listen to your body. Happy exercising!
