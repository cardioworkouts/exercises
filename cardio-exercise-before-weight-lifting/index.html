Why You Should Do Cardio Exercise Before Weight Lifting

Written by Christine Oct 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to improve your fitness routine and wondering if you should do cardio before weight lifting? Have you heard mixed opinions about the best order of exercises and want to know the benefits of doing cardio before lifting weights? Let's dive in and explore the advantages of incorporating cardio exercise before weight lifting.

Cardio exercise before weight lifting can help you warm up your muscles and prepare your body for the more intense strength training exercises. It also increases your heart rate and blood flow, which can help you have a more effective workout and reduce the risk of injury during weight lifting. However, there are still some misconceptions about the effectiveness of doing cardio before lifting weights.

The primary target of cardio exercise before weight lifting is to increase your heart rate and blood flow, which can help you perform better during strength training exercises. It can also help you lose weight and improve your overall fitness level. Doing cardio before lifting weights can also help you burn more calories, which is beneficial if you are trying to lose weight.

In summary, cardio exercise before weight lifting is an effective way to improve your fitness routine and prepare your body for strength training exercises. It can also help you lose weight and improve your overall fitness level. However, there are still some misconceptions about the effectiveness of doing cardio before lifting weights.

Benefits of Cardio Exercise Before Weight Lifting

Personally, I have found that doing cardio before lifting weights has helped me to have a more effective workout and reduce the risk of injury. It also helps me to mentally prepare for the more intense strength training exercises. Some of the benefits of doing cardio before lifting weights include:

  • Increased heart rate and blood flow
  • Better warm-up for muscles
  • Improved performance during strength training exercises
  • Burning more calories

Common Misconceptions About Cardio Exercise Before Weight Lifting

One common misconception about doing cardio before lifting weights is that it can reduce your strength and muscle gains. However, studies have shown that this is not necessarily true. Another misconception is that doing cardio before lifting weights can increase your risk of injury. However, if you warm up properly and perform the exercises with correct form, the risk of injury is minimal.

The Best Types of Cardio Exercise Before Weight Lifting

The best types of cardio exercise before weight lifting are those that increase your heart rate and blood flow. Some examples include running, cycling, rowing, or using the elliptical machine. It is important to choose a type of cardio that you enjoy and can stick to in the long term.

Tips for Incorporating Cardio Exercise Before Weight Lifting

If you are new to incorporating cardio before weight lifting, start with a shorter warm-up period of 5-10 minutes and gradually increase the time as your fitness level improves. It is also important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. Finally, make sure to stretch properly before and after your workout to reduce the risk of injury.

Final Thoughts

Incorporating cardio exercise before weight lifting can be an effective way to improve your fitness routine and prepare your body for strength training exercises. It can also help you lose weight and improve your overall fitness level. Remember to choose a type of cardio exercise that you enjoy and listen to your body to avoid injury.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio after weight lifting?

A: Yes, you can do cardio after weight lifting. However, it is generally recommended to do cardio before weight lifting in order to warm up your muscles and reduce the risk of injury.

Q: How long should I do cardio before lifting weights?

A: Start with a shorter warm-up period of 5-10 minutes and gradually increase the time as your fitness level improves.

Q: Will doing cardio before lifting weights reduce my strength and muscle gains?

A: Studies have shown that doing cardio before lifting weights does not necessarily reduce your strength and muscle gains.

Q: What is the best type of cardio exercise before weight lifting?

A: The best types of cardio exercise before weight lifting are those that increase your heart rate and blood flow. Some examples include running, cycling, rowing, or using the elliptical machine.

Conclusion of Cardio Exercise Before Weight Lifting

Cardio exercise before weight lifting can be an effective way to improve your fitness routine and prepare your body for strength training exercises. It can also help you lose weight and improve your overall fitness level. Remember to choose a type of cardio exercise that you enjoy and listen to your body to avoid injury.
