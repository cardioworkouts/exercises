Is It Better To Do Cardio Before Or After Strength Training A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to working out, there are many questions that come to mind. One of the most common questions is whether it is better to do cardio before or after strength training. The answer is not as simple as a straightforward yes or no. It depends on several factors, including your fitness goals, fitness level, and personal preferences. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the pros and cons of doing cardio before or after strength training and help you make the right decision for your fitness routine.

The Pain Points of Doing Cardio Before or After Strength Training

One of the main pain points of doing cardio before strength training is that it can deplete your energy levels, making it harder to lift heavy weights or perform strength exercises at your full potential. On the other hand, if you do strength training before cardio, you may not have enough energy to perform cardio exercises at your full potential, which can compromise your cardiovascular fitness goals.

The Answer to "Is it Better to Do Cardio Before or After Strength Training?"

The answer to whether you should do cardio before or after strength training depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness, it is better to do cardio before strength training. On the other hand, if your primary goal is to build muscle and strength, it is better to do strength training before cardio.

However, if you are looking to achieve both goals, you can alternate between cardio and strength training on different days or do a combination of both in one workout. Ultimately, the best approach is the one that works best for your body and fits your schedule and lifestyle.

Personal Experience: Doing Cardio Before Strength Training

I prefer to do cardio before strength training as it helps me warm up my muscles and gets my heart rate up, making me feel energized for the rest of my workout. I usually start with a 10-15 minute cardio warm-up, followed by 45 minutes of strength training. This routine has helped me achieve my fitness goals and has become a part of my daily routine.

Personal Experience: Doing Strength Training Before Cardio

Some people prefer to do strength training before cardio as it helps them lift heavier weights and perform strength exercises at their full potential. They also feel more energized for the rest of their workout after they have completed their strength training routine. However, this approach may not work for everyone, and it is essential to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio Before Strength Training

The main pros of doing cardio before strength training are that it helps warm up your muscles, improves your cardiovascular fitness, and burns more calories. The cons are that it can deplete your energy levels, making it harder to lift heavy weights or perform strength exercises at your full potential.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Strength Training Before Cardio

The main pros of doing strength training before cardio are that it helps you lift heavier weights, perform strength exercises at your full potential, and build muscle and strength. The cons are that you may not have enough energy to perform cardio exercises at your full potential, which can compromise your cardiovascular fitness goals.

Personal Experience: Combining Cardio and Strength Training

Combining cardio and strength training in one workout has become popular in recent years as it helps you achieve both fitness goals in one session. For example, you can start with a 10-15 minute cardio warm-up, followed by 30-45 minutes of strength training, and finish with 10-15 minutes of cardio cool-down. This approach saves time and can be more effective for weight loss and building muscle.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and strength training on the same day, but it is essential to plan your workout routine carefully to avoid overtraining and injury.

Q: How long should I wait after cardio to do strength training?

A: You should wait at least 30 minutes after completing cardio before starting your strength training routine to allow your muscles to recover and prevent injury.

Q: How long should I wait after strength training to do cardio?

A: You should wait at least 30 minutes after completing strength training before doing cardio to allow your muscles to recover and prevent injury.

Q: Which is better for weight loss, cardio, or strength training?

A: Both cardio and strength training are essential for weight loss, but a combination of both is more effective. Cardio helps burn calories and improve cardiovascular fitness, while strength training builds muscle and increases metabolism, leading to long-term weight loss.

Conclusion of Is it Better to Do Cardio Before or After Strength Training?

The answer to whether you should do cardio before or after strength training depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness, it is better to do cardio before strength training. On the other hand, if your primary goal is to build muscle and strength, it is better to do strength training before cardio. However, you can alternate between cardio and strength training on different days or do a combination of both in one workout. Ultimately, the best approach is the one that works best for your body and fits your schedule and lifestyle.
