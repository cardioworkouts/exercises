How To Create A Beginner Workout Schedule

Written by Frank Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Starting a workout routine can be intimidating, especially if you're new to exercising. You may have questions like, "what exercises should I do?" or "how often should I work out?" Creating a workout schedule can help you answer these questions and get started on the right foot.

Many beginners struggle with finding the right workout routine that suits their needs. It can be challenging to know where to start, and often people quit before they see any results. This is where a workout beginner schedule comes in.

A workout beginner schedule is a plan that outlines your exercise routine for the week. It includes the types of exercises you'll do, the number of sets and reps, and how many days per week you'll work out. The goal of a workout beginner schedule is to help you stay organized, motivated, and on track with your fitness goals.

In this article, we'll discuss the basics of creating a workout beginner schedule that works for you. We'll cover the importance of setting realistic goals, choosing the right exercises, and finding a workout routine that fits your schedule.

Setting Realistic Goals

The first step in creating a workout beginner schedule is setting realistic goals. Your goals should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound. This is also known as SMART goal setting. For example, a SMART goal for a beginner might be to do 30 minutes of cardio three times per week for the next two months.

When setting goals, it's important to be realistic. Don't expect to see results overnight, and don't push yourself too hard too fast. Start with small, achievable goals and work your way up. This will help you build confidence, stay motivated, and avoid injury.

Choosing the Right Exercises

Choosing the right exercises is crucial to the success of your workout beginner schedule. As a beginner, it's essential to start with exercises that are easy to perform and don't require too much equipment. Bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, and lunges are great for beginners.

You can also incorporate cardio exercises like jogging, cycling, or jumping jacks to get your heart rate up. Strength training exercises like dumbbell curls, bench press, and deadlifts can help you build muscle and improve your overall fitness.

Designing Your Workout Schedule

Once you've set your goals and chosen the right exercises, it's time to design your workout schedule. A good workout beginner schedule should include a combination of strength training and cardio exercises. You should aim to work out at least three days per week, with rest days in between.

When designing your workout schedule, it's important to listen to your body. Don't push yourself too hard, and take breaks when you need them. Your workout beginner schedule should be challenging but not overwhelming.

How to Stick to Your Workout Beginner Schedule

Sticking to a workout beginner schedule can be challenging, especially if you're new to exercising. Here are some tips to help you stay on track:

  • Find a workout buddy to hold you accountable
  • Set reminders on your phone or calendar
  • Track your progress and celebrate your successes
  • Remember why you started and stay motivated

FAQs About Workout Beginner Schedule

Q: How long should a beginner workout be?

A: A beginner workout should be around 30-45 minutes long. This gives you enough time to warm up, complete your exercises, and cool down.

Q: How often should I work out as a beginner?

A: As a beginner, you should aim to work out at least three days per week. This will give your body time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: What are some beginner-friendly exercises?

A: Some beginner-friendly exercises include squats, lunges, push-ups, jumping jacks, and jogging.

Q: How do I know if I'm pushing myself too hard?

A: If you experience pain, dizziness, or extreme fatigue during your workout, you may be pushing yourself too hard. Listen to your body and take breaks when you need them.

Conclusion of Workout Beginner Schedule

Creating a workout beginner schedule can help you stay organized, motivated, and on track with your fitness goals. Remember to set realistic goals, choose the right exercises, and design a workout schedule that works for you. By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to a healthier, fitter you.
