How Much High Intensity Cardio Per Week A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you feeling confused about how much high intensity cardio per week you need to do to achieve your fitness goals? You are not alone! Many people struggle with this question, particularly those who are new to exercise or are trying to increase the intensity of their workouts. In this blog post, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide to help you understand how much high intensity cardio per week you need to do to achieve your desired results.

When it comes to high intensity cardio, there are a few pain points that people commonly face. These include not knowing how much cardio to do, fearing that too much cardio will lead to burnout, and not understanding how to balance cardio with strength training.

The target for how much high intensity cardio per week you need to do largely depends on your fitness goals. If you are trying to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health, you may need to do more cardio than someone who is simply trying to maintain their current fitness level. In general, most experts recommend that you aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week.

In summary, how much high intensity cardio per week you need to do depends on your fitness goals. Most experts recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week.

The Importance of High Intensity Cardio

As someone who has been exercising for several years, I can attest to the importance of high intensity cardio. I used to be someone who only did low-intensity cardio, such as walking or cycling at a moderate pace. However, I found that I wasn't seeing the results I wanted. When I started incorporating high-intensity cardio into my routine, I saw a significant improvement in my fitness level.

High-intensity cardio is beneficial because it helps you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time. It can also help you build endurance and improve your cardiovascular health. However, it is important to note that high-intensity cardio can be taxing on your body, so it is important to give yourself adequate rest and recovery time.

The Best Types of High Intensity Cardio

There are many types of high-intensity cardio that you can incorporate into your routine. Some popular options include running, cycling, swimming, and HIIT (high-intensity interval training). When choosing a type of high-intensity cardio, it is important to choose something that you enjoy so that you will be more likely to stick with it.

Personally, I enjoy doing HIIT workouts because they are challenging and keep me motivated. I also like that they can be done in a relatively short amount of time, which is helpful when I am short on time.

Tips for Incorporating High Intensity Cardio into Your Routine

If you are new to high-intensity cardio, it is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. You can also try incorporating high-intensity intervals into your existing cardio routine. For example, you could try sprinting for 30 seconds followed by a minute of rest, and repeat for several rounds.

How to Avoid Burnout with High Intensity Cardio

One common concern with high-intensity cardio is that it can lead to burnout. To avoid burnout, it is important to give yourself adequate rest and recovery time. You should also make sure that you are fueling your body with the right nutrients and getting enough sleep. Finally, it is important to listen to your body and adjust your workouts as needed.

How Much High Intensity Cardio per Week: Final Thoughts

In conclusion, how much high intensity cardio per week you need to do largely depends on your fitness goals. Most experts recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week. It is important to choose a type of high-intensity cardio that you enjoy and to start slowly if you are new to this type of exercise. With dedication and consistency, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health.

Question and Answer

Q: Is high-intensity cardio better than low-intensity cardio?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. High-intensity cardio is beneficial for burning more calories and improving cardiovascular health, but low-intensity cardio can also be beneficial for improving endurance and recovery.

Q: Can you do high-intensity cardio every day?

A: It is not recommended to do high-intensity cardio every day. Your body needs time to rest and recover between workouts, so it is important to incorporate rest days into your routine.

Q: How long should a high-intensity cardio workout be?

A: A high-intensity cardio workout should be at least 20-30 minutes long, but no more than 60 minutes. It is important to give yourself adequate time to warm up and cool down before and after your workout.

Q: How can I make high-intensity cardio more enjoyable?

A: You can make high-intensity cardio more enjoyable by choosing a type of exercise that you enjoy, incorporating music into your workout, and working out with a friend or group.

Conclusion of How Much High Intensity Cardio per Week

Now that you have a better understanding of how much high intensity cardio per week you need to do to achieve your fitness goals, it is time to put this knowledge into practice. Remember to start slowly, choose a type of high-intensity cardio that you enjoy, and give yourself adequate rest and recovery time. With consistency and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health.
