Should I Do Cardio And Hiit On The Same Day A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering whether you should do cardio and HIIT on the same day? This is a common question among fitness enthusiasts, and the answer is not as simple as a yes or no. Many factors come into play when deciding whether to combine the two workouts, such as your fitness level, goals, and schedule.

The thought of performing two intense workouts in a single day can seem daunting, but it's important to understand the benefits and drawbacks of doing so. Many people make the mistake of assuming that more is better, but in reality, overtraining can be counterproductive and lead to injury.

So, should you do cardio and HIIT on the same day? The answer is that it depends on your fitness goals, current fitness level, and the amount of time you have available to work out.

Before we dive deeper into the topic, let's first understand what cardio and HIIT are and how they differ from each other.

What is Cardio?

Cardio, short for cardiovascular exercise, is any form of exercise that raises your heart rate and increases oxygen flow throughout the body. Examples of cardio include running, cycling, swimming, and brisk walking.

The primary goal of cardio is to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and promote weight loss. It also helps to reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

What is HIIT?

HIIT, short for high-intensity interval training, is a form of cardio that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. Examples of HIIT include sprinting, jumping jacks, and burpees.

The primary goal of HIIT is to improve your cardiovascular fitness, burn calories, and boost your metabolism. It also helps to build lean muscle mass, improve endurance, and reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio and HIIT on the Same Day

Now that we have a basic understanding of cardio and HIIT, let's dive into the pros and cons of combining them in a single workout session.

Pros

1. Time-Saving: Doing cardio and HIIT on the same day can save you time as you can get a full-body workout in a shorter amount of time.

2. Increased Calorie Burn: Combining cardio and HIIT can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster.

3. Improved Endurance: Doing both types of workouts can help improve your overall endurance, allowing you to work out longer and harder.

Cons

1. Overtraining: Doing too much intense exercise can lead to overtraining, increasing the risk of injury and negatively affecting your performance.

2. Exhaustion: Combining cardio and HIIT can leave you feeling exhausted, making it difficult to perform at your best during both workouts.

3. Plateau: Doing the same type of workout repeatedly can lead to a plateau in your results, making it harder to see progress.

My Personal Experience

As a fitness enthusiast, I have tried both cardio and HIIT on the same day, and the results have been mixed. When I first started combining the two workouts, I felt energized and saw a significant increase in my endurance and calorie burn.

However, after a few weeks, I started feeling exhausted and experienced a plateau in my results. I realized that I was overtraining and not giving my body enough time to recover between workouts.

Now, I alternate between cardio and HIIT sessions throughout the week and give my body ample time to rest and recover. This has helped me avoid overtraining and see better results in the long run.

Tips for Combining Cardio and HIIT

If you decide to combine cardio and HIIT, here are some tips to help you get the most out of your workouts:

1. Start Slow:

Don't jump into intense workouts right away. Start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

2. Listen to Your Body:

Pay attention to your body and how it responds to the workouts. If you feel exhausted or experience pain, take a break and rest.

3. Alternate Workouts:

Alternate between cardio and HIIT sessions throughout the week to avoid overtraining and see better results.

4. Fuel Your Body:

Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to fuel your body for intense workouts.

Question and Answer

Q1. Is it okay to do cardio and HIIT on the same day?

A1. It depends on your fitness level, goals, and schedule. If you are an experienced athlete and have enough time to recover between workouts, it may be beneficial to combine the two. However, if you are a beginner or have a busy schedule, it may be better to alternate between cardio and HIIT sessions.

Q2. How often should I do cardio and HIIT?

A2. The frequency of cardio and HIIT workouts depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, aim for 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week, along with two to three HIIT sessions.

Q3. Can combining cardio and HIIT help me lose weight faster?

A3. Yes, combining cardio and HIIT can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster. However, it's important to maintain a balanced diet and stay hydrated to see the best results.

Q4. What are the best cardio and HIIT exercises to combine?

A4. The best cardio and HIIT exercises to combine are those that work multiple muscle groups and elevate your heart rate. Examples include jumping jacks, burpees, mountain climbers, and high knees.

Conclusion

Whether you should do cardio and HIIT on the same day depends on various factors such as your fitness level, goals, and schedule. While combining the two workouts can save you time and help you burn more calories, it's important to avoid overtraining and give your body ample time to rest and recover. By following the tips mentioned above and listening to your body, you can reap the benefits of both types of workouts and achieve your fitness goals.
