The Best Aerobic Exercise Music To Keep You Moving

Written by Bardi Sep 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you ever find yourself struggling to stay motivated during your workout? Do you feel like you could use an extra push to get through those tough cardio sessions? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to find the right motivation to keep them going during aerobic exercise. Luckily, there's a solution that can help: the right music.

When it comes to aerobic exercise, having the right music can make all the difference. It can help you stay focused, keep you motivated, and even make your workout more enjoyable. But with so many options out there, it can be tough to know where to start. That's why we're here to help.

If you're looking for the best aerobic exercise music to help you power through your workouts, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll explore some of the top options out there and explain why they work so well. So, let's get started!

The Benefits of Listening to Music During Your Workout

Before we dive into the best aerobic exercise music options, let's take a moment to explore why music can be so beneficial during your workout. There are a few key reasons:

1. It can help you stay focused. When you're listening to music, it's easier to tune out distractions and focus on your workout.

2. It can improve your mood. Listening to upbeat music can help boost your mood and make your workout more enjoyable.

3. It can increase your endurance. Studies have shown that listening to music can help increase endurance during aerobic exercise, allowing you to push yourself harder and longer.

Top Aerobic Exercise Music Options

Now that we've covered why music can be so beneficial during your workout, let's take a look at some of the top options out there:

1. Pop Music

Pop music is a great option for aerobic exercise because it's typically upbeat and has a fast tempo. This type of music can help keep you energized and motivated throughout your workout. Some popular pop songs to consider include:

- "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake

- "Shut Up and Dance" by Walk the Moon

- "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars

2. Electronic Dance Music (EDM)

EDM is another great option for aerobic exercise because it's typically high-energy and has a fast beat. This type of music can help keep you moving and motivated throughout your workout. Some popular EDM songs to consider include:

- "Levels" by Avicii

- "Wake Me Up" by Avicii

- "Don't You Worry Child" by Swedish House Mafia

3. Hip-Hop

Hip-hop is a great option for aerobic exercise because it's typically high-energy and has a strong beat. This type of music can help you stay motivated and focused throughout your workout. Some popular hip-hop songs to consider include:

- "Lose Yourself" by Eminem

- "Jumpman" by Drake and Future

- "All the Way Up" by Fat Joe and Remy Ma

4. Rock Music

Rock music is a great option for aerobic exercise because it's typically high-energy and has a strong beat. This type of music can help you stay motivated and focused throughout your workout. Some popular rock songs to consider include:

- "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

- "Livin' on a Prayer" by Bon Jovi

- "Sweet Child O' Mine" by Guns N' Roses

FAQs About Best Aerobic Exercise Music

Q: How do I know what type of music is best for my workout?

A: The best way to determine what type of music is best for your workout is to experiment. Try listening to different genres and see what works best for you. Some people prefer high-energy music, while others prefer more mellow options. Find what works best for you and stick with it!

Q: Can I listen to any type of music during my workout?

A: In general, you can listen to any type of music during your workout as long as it helps keep you motivated and focused. However, some types of music may be more beneficial than others. For example, music with a fast tempo and strong beat is typically better for aerobic exercise than slow, mellow music.

Q: How loud should I listen to my music during my workout?

A: It's important to be mindful of your surroundings when listening to music during your workout. You don't want to be so loud that you disturb those around you. However, you also want to make sure that you can hear the music over any background noise. A good rule of thumb is to keep the volume at a level where you can hear the music but can also hear any important instructions or cues.

Q: Can listening to music really improve my workout?

A: Yes! Studies have shown that listening to music can help improve your mood, increase your endurance, and help you stay focused during your workout. So, if you're struggling to stay motivated during your aerobic exercise, try adding some music to your routine!

Conclusion of Best Aerobic Exercise Music

When it comes to aerobic exercise, having the right music can make all the difference. Whether you prefer pop, EDM, hip-hop, or rock music, there's an option out there that can help keep you motivated and focused. So, the next time you're heading to the gym or going for a run, be sure to bring along your favorite workout playlist. Your body (and mind) will thank you!
