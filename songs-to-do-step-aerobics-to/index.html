Songs To Do Step Aerobics To Get Your Heart Pumping And Your Body Moving

Are you tired of the same old workout routine? Do you find yourself struggling to stay motivated during your exercise sessions? If so, it might be time to switch things up and try step aerobics. This high-energy, low-impact workout is perfect for people of all fitness levels, and it can help you burn calories, build endurance, and tone your muscles. However, finding the right songs to do step aerobics to can be a challenge. After all, you need music that's upbeat, motivating, and easy to move to. In this blog post, we'll share some of our favorite songs to do step aerobics to, along with tips for creating the perfect playlist.

If you're new to step aerobics, you might be feeling a bit overwhelmed. After all, there's a lot to learn, from the basic steps to the more advanced moves. However, don't let that intimidate you. With a bit of practice, you'll be stepping like a pro in no time. One of the best ways to stay motivated during your step aerobics workout is to choose music that inspires you. That's why we've put together this list of songs to do step aerobics to.

Our list of songs to do step aerobics to includes a mix of genres, from pop to hip hop to electronic dance music. We've chosen songs that are upbeat, with a tempo of around 120-140 beats per minute. This tempo is perfect for step aerobics, as it allows you to move quickly without feeling rushed. Some of our favorite songs to do step aerobics to include "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake, "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars, "Shut Up and Dance" by Walk the Moon, and "Don't Start Now" by Dua Lipa.

In summary, if you're looking for a fun and challenging workout, step aerobics might be just what you need. To get the most out of your workout, be sure to choose music that inspires you and keeps you moving. Our list of songs to do step aerobics to is a great place to start, but don't be afraid to experiment and find the perfect playlist for you.

Songs to Do Step Aerobics to: Pop Hits

One of the best things about step aerobics is that it can be done to just about any type of music. However, if you're looking for something that's easy to move to and will keep you pumped up throughout your workout, pop hits are a great choice. Some of our favorite songs to do step aerobics to in this genre include "Sorry" by Justin Bieber, "Shape of You" by Ed Sheeran, and "Side to Side" by Ariana Grande ft. Nicki Minaj.

Personally, I love doing step aerobics to pop hits because they're fun and uplifting. They have a great beat that makes it easy to keep up with the steps, and they often have catchy lyrics that help me stay motivated. One of my favorite songs to do step aerobics to is "Shake It Off" by Taylor Swift. This song has a great beat, and the lyrics are all about not letting other people bring you down. It's the perfect song to listen to when you're feeling tired or discouraged.

Songs to Do Step Aerobics to: Hip Hop and R&B

If you're looking for something with a bit more edge, hip hop and R&B are great genres to try. These songs often have a slower tempo than pop hits, which can make them perfect for more challenging step aerobics routines. Some of our favorite songs to do step aerobics to in this genre include "No Limit" by G-Eazy ft. A$AP Rocky and Cardi B, "In My Feelings" by Drake, and "Work" by Rihanna ft. Drake.

When I'm in the mood for something with a bit more attitude, I love doing step aerobics to hip hop and R&B. These songs have a great beat that's perfect for keeping me motivated, and the lyrics often have a message of empowerment that helps me push through the tough parts of my workout. One of my favorite songs to do step aerobics to in this genre is "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott ft. Ciara and Fatman Scoop. This song has a great beat that makes it easy to keep up with the steps, and the lyrics are all about letting go and having fun.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect Songs to Do Step Aerobics To

Now that you've got some ideas for songs to do step aerobics to, how can you create the perfect playlist? Here are a few tips:

  • Choose music that you love. If you're not a fan of a particular genre or artist, don't force yourself to listen to them during your workout. Instead, choose music that makes you feel good and keeps you motivated.
  • Look for songs with a beat that matches the tempo of your workout. As we mentioned earlier, a tempo of around 120-140 beats per minute is perfect for step aerobics. Use a website or app that calculates the BPM of your songs to make sure they're the right speed.
  • Consider the lyrics. While the beat is important, the lyrics can also have a big impact on your motivation. Look for songs with lyrics that inspire you and keep you focused on your goals.

How to Create the Perfect Step Aerobics Playlist

Now that you've got some tips for choosing the perfect songs to do step aerobics to, how can you create the perfect playlist? Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by choosing a few of your favorite songs. These can be from any genre, as long as they have a beat that matches the tempo of your workout.
  2. Use a website or app that calculates BPM to find similar songs. This will help you create a playlist that flows well and keeps you motivated throughout your workout.
  3. Include a mix of genres. This will keep things interesting and prevent you from getting bored during your workout.
  4. Consider the length of your workout. If you're doing a longer workout, you'll want a longer playlist. Aim for at least 30-45 minutes of music.
  5. Test out your playlist during your workout. If you find that certain songs aren't working well, swap them out for something else.

Songs to Do Step Aerobics to: Electronic Dance Music

Finally, if you're looking for something with a bit more energy, electronic dance music (EDM) is a great genre to try. These songs often have a faster tempo than pop hits or hip hop, which can make them perfect for more intense step aerobics routines. Some of our favorite songs to do step aerobics to in this genre include "Levels" by Avicii, "Wake Me Up" by Avicii, and "This Is What You Came For" by Calvin Harris ft. Rihanna.

Personally, I love doing step aerobics to EDM because the beat is so strong and consistent. It makes it easy to keep up with the steps and stay motivated throughout my workout. One of my favorite songs to do step aerobics to in this genre is "Titanium" by David Guetta ft. Sia. This song has a great beat that never lets up, and the lyrics are all about being strong and resilient.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do step aerobics if I have bad knees?

A: Step aerobics can be a great low-impact workout, but if you have bad knees, you should talk to your doctor before starting any new exercise program. They can help you determine if step aerobics is safe for you, and provide you with modifications that will protect your knees.

Q: How many calories can I burn during a step aerobics workout?

A: The number of calories you burn during a step aerobics workout will depend on a variety of factors, including your weight, the intensity of your workout, and the length of your workout. However, on average, you can expect to burn between 400-600 calories per hour.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to do step aerobics?

A: To do step aerobics, you'll need a step platform. These are typically available at your local gym or fitness center, but you can also purchase them online. Make sure to choose a platform that's sturdy and has a non-slip surface.

Q: Can I do step aerobics at home?

A: Yes, you can definitely do step aerobics at home. All you need is a step platform and some music. You can find step aerobics routines on YouTube or purchase DVDs that will guide you through the steps.
