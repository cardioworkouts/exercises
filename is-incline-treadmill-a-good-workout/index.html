Is Incline Treadmill A Good Workout

Written by Christine Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a workout that will help you burn calories and build muscles? Have you heard about the incline treadmill but are not sure if it is a good workout? In this article, we will explore the benefits and drawbacks of the incline treadmill and help you decide if it is the right workout for you.

What is Incline Treadmill?

The incline treadmill is a type of treadmill that allows you to adjust the angle of the running surface. This means that you can simulate running uphill, which makes your workout more challenging and burns more calories. In addition, because you are running on a moving surface, your workout is low-impact, which is easier on your joints than running on hard surfaces like concrete or asphalt.

My Experience with Incline Treadmill

As a fitness enthusiast, I have used the incline treadmill many times. I find that it is an excellent way to burn calories and build leg muscles. When I run on an incline, I feel my heart rate increase, and I sweat more than when I run on a flat surface. I also feel the burn in my quads and calves, which tells me that I am working my muscles harder.

Benefits of Incline Treadmill Workout

The incline treadmill has several benefits, including:

1. Burns More Calories

When you run on an incline, you burn more calories than when you run on a flat surface. This is because you are working against gravity, and your body has to work harder to move forward. According to a study by the American Council on Exercise, running on a 1% incline can increase your calorie burn by up to 5%.

2. Builds Leg Muscles

Running on an incline is an excellent way to build leg muscles. When you run uphill, you engage your quads, hamstrings, and calves more than when you run on a flat surface. This helps to tone and strengthen your legs.

3. Low-Impact Workout

Running on a treadmill is generally considered a low-impact workout because the surface is cushioned, which reduces the impact on your joints. Running on an incline treadmill is even more low-impact than running on a flat surface because the incline reduces the force of impact on your joints.

4. Variety

Running on a treadmill can get boring after a while, but with an incline treadmill, you can mix things up by adjusting the angle of the running surface. This can help to keep your workouts interesting and challenging.

Drawbacks of Incline Treadmill Workout

While the incline treadmill has several benefits, there are also some drawbacks to consider:

1. Limited Upper Body Workout

Running on an incline treadmill primarily works your lower body. If you want to get a full-body workout, you will need to incorporate other exercises into your routine.

2. Expensive

Incline treadmills are generally more expensive than traditional treadmills, so they may not be affordable for everyone.

3. Requires More Energy

Running on an incline requires more energy than running on a flat surface, so it may not be suitable for beginners or those with certain health conditions.

4. Can Be Intimidating

Running on an incline can be intimidating, especially if you are not used to it. You may need to start with a lower incline and gradually increase it as you build up your strength and endurance.

Question and Answer

Q1. Is incline treadmill good for weight loss?

A1. Yes, running on an incline treadmill can help you burn more calories and lose weight.

Q2. Does incline treadmill work abs?

A2. Running on an incline treadmill primarily works your lower body, but it can also engage your core muscles to some extent.

Q3. How much incline should I use on a treadmill?

A3. The ideal incline for your treadmill workout depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, a 1% incline is a good place to start.

Q4. How long should I run on an incline treadmill?

A4. The length of your workout depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio exercise per day.

Conclusion of Is Incline Treadmill a Good Workout

The incline treadmill is an excellent workout for burning calories and building leg muscles. However, it may not be suitable for everyone, especially beginners or those with certain health conditions. If you are thinking about trying the incline treadmill, start with a lower incline and gradually increase it as you build up your strength and endurance.
