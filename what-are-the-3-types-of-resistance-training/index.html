What Are The 3 Types Of Resistance Training

Written by Luffy Sep 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Resistance training is an essential part of any fitness routine. It's a type of exercise that involves using resistance to build muscular strength and endurance. But did you know that there are different types of resistance training? In this post, we'll explore the 3 types of resistance training and their benefits.

Many people struggle with finding the right type of resistance training for their goals. Some may find that traditional weightlifting is too intimidating, while others may not enjoy the monotony of machine exercises. Others may be looking for a more challenging workout that targets specific muscle groups. Whatever your reason for wanting to learn more about resistance training, this post is for you.

What Are the 3 Types of Resistance Training?

The 3 types of resistance training are bodyweight exercises, free weights, and resistance bands. Each type has its own unique benefits and can be tailored to your specific fitness goals.

Bodyweight Exercises

Bodyweight exercises are exercises that use your own body weight as resistance. Examples include push-ups, squats, and lunges. Bodyweight exercises are great for beginners because they don't require any equipment and can be done anywhere. They also help improve flexibility and balance.

How to Do Bodyweight Exercises

To perform bodyweight exercises, start by choosing the exercises that target the muscle groups you want to work on. Then, perform each exercise for a set number of reps or time. Rest for a short period between each set, and repeat until you've completed the desired number of sets.

Free Weights

Free weights are exercises that use dumbbells, barbells, or kettlebells as resistance. Free weights are great for building strength and muscle mass because they allow you to lift heavier weights than bodyweight exercises. They also help improve bone density.

How to Do Free Weight Exercises

To perform free weight exercises, start by choosing the exercises that target the muscle groups you want to work on. Then, choose the appropriate weight and perform each exercise for a set number of reps. Rest for a short period between each set, and repeat until you've completed the desired number of sets.

Resistance Bands

Resistance bands are elastic bands that provide resistance when stretched. They come in different levels of resistance and can be used to target specific muscle groups. Resistance bands are great for beginners because they're easy to use and can be used anywhere. They're also great for rehabilitation exercises.

How to Use Resistance Bands

To use resistance bands, start by choosing the resistance band that provides the appropriate level of resistance. Then, choose the exercises that target the muscle groups you want to work on. Perform each exercise for a set number of reps or time. Rest for a short period between each set, and repeat until you've completed the desired number of sets.

Question and Answer About What Are the 3 Types of Resistance Training

Q: What's the best type of resistance training for beginners?

A: Bodyweight exercises and resistance bands are great for beginners because they don't require any equipment and can be done anywhere.

Q: Can resistance training help with weight loss?

A: Yes, resistance training can help with weight loss because it helps build muscle mass, which increases your metabolism.

Q: Can resistance training be done at home?

A: Yes, all 3 types of resistance training can be done at home with minimal equipment.

Q: How often should I do resistance training?

A: It's recommended to do resistance training at least twice a week, with a day of rest in between workouts.

Conclusion of What Are the 3 Types of Resistance Training

Resistance training is an important part of any fitness routine, and there are different types of resistance training to suit everyone's needs. Bodyweight exercises, free weights, and resistance bands all provide unique benefits and can be tailored to your specific fitness goals. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced lifter, incorporating resistance training into your routine can help you achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being.
