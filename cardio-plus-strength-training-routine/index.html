Cardio Plus Strength Training Routine The Ultimate Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a workout routine that will help you lose weight, build muscle, and improve your overall health? Look no further than a cardio plus strength training routine! In this guide, we'll explore the benefits of combining cardio and strength training, share tips for creating an effective routine, and answer some common questions about this type of workout.

If you've ever struggled to find a workout routine that works for you, you're not alone. Many people find it challenging to balance cardio and strength training or struggle to stick to a routine long-term. But with a little guidance, anyone can create a workout routine that fits their needs and goals.

What is a Cardio Plus Strength Training Routine?

A cardio plus strength training routine is a type of workout that combines cardiovascular exercise (like running, cycling, or swimming) with strength training exercises (like weight lifting or bodyweight exercises). The goal of this type of workout is to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and build lean muscle mass.

There are many benefits to this type of workout, including:

  • Increased calorie burn: Combining cardio and strength training can help you burn more calories during and after your workout.
  • Better cardiovascular health: Cardiovascular exercise can help improve your heart health, while strength training can improve your overall fitness and prevent injury.
  • Muscle building: Strength training exercises can help you build and maintain lean muscle mass, which can help you burn more calories even at rest.
  • Improved flexibility and mobility: Many strength training exercises can improve your flexibility and range of motion, which can help prevent injury and improve overall fitness.

My Personal Experience with Cardio Plus Strength Training

For years, I struggled to find a workout routine that worked for me. I tried running, weight lifting, and even yoga, but I could never seem to stick with any one thing for very long. But then I discovered cardio plus strength training, and everything changed.

By combining cardio and strength training, I was able to create a workout routine that was challenging, fun, and effective. I started to see results almost immediately, both in terms of weight loss and muscle gain. And because I was doing a variety of exercises, I never got bored or felt like I was stuck in a rut.

Today, I'm in the best shape of my life, and I owe it all to my cardio plus strength training routine.

Tips for Creating an Effective Cardio Plus Strength Training Routine

If you're interested in creating your own cardio plus strength training routine, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start slow: If you're new to exercise or haven't worked out in a while, don't try to do too much too soon. Start with a few days a week and gradually increase your workouts over time.
  • Find exercises you enjoy: The key to sticking with any workout routine is to find exercises you enjoy. Experiment with different types of cardio and strength training exercises until you find something that feels fun and challenging.
  • Mix it up: Doing the same workout every day can get boring quickly. Mix up your routine by trying new exercises or changing the order of your workouts.
  • Set realistic goals: Don't expect to see results overnight. Set realistic, achievable goals for yourself and celebrate your progress along the way.

The Best Cardio Exercises for a Cardio Plus Strength Training Routine

When it comes to cardio exercises, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are a few of the best exercises to include in your cardio plus strength training routine:

  • Running
  • Cycling
  • Swimming
  • Rowing
  • Jumping rope
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)

The Best Strength Training Exercises for a Cardio Plus Strength Training Routine

Strength training exercises are an essential part of any cardio plus strength training routine. Here are a few of the best exercises to include in your routine:

  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Push-ups
  • Planks
  • Deadlifts
  • Dumbbell or barbell rows

Common Questions About Cardio Plus Strength Training

What's the best time of day to do cardio plus strength training?

The best time of day to do cardio plus strength training is whenever you have the most energy and motivation. Some people prefer to workout in the morning, while others find they have more energy in the evening. Experiment with different times of day to find what works best for you.

How often should I do cardio plus strength training?

It's generally recommended to do cardio plus strength training at least three to four times a week. However, the exact frequency will depend on your goals, fitness level, and schedule.

Can I do cardio plus strength training if I have a health condition?

If you have a health condition or any concerns about starting a new exercise routine, it's important to talk to your doctor before getting started. They can help you determine what exercises are safe for you and provide guidance on how to modify your routine if needed.

What should I eat before and after a cardio plus strength training workout?

Before a workout, it's best to eat a balanced meal that includes protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats. After a workout, it's important to refuel with a meal or snack that includes protein and carbohydrates to help repair and build muscle. Some good options include a protein shake, a turkey sandwich on whole grain bread, or a Greek yogurt with fruit.

Conclusion of Cardio Plus Strength Training Routine

A cardio plus strength training routine can be an excellent way to improve your overall health, build muscle, and burn calories. By combining cardio and strength training exercises, you can create a workout routine that is challenging, fun, and effective. Just remember to start slow, find exercises you enjoy, and set realistic goals for yourself. With time and consistency, you'll be amazed at what you can achieve!
