Resistance Training Exercises For Soccer Players

Written by Daniel Jul 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a soccer player looking to improve your strength and power on the field? Resistance training exercises may be just what you need to take your game to the next level. In this post, weâll discuss the benefits of resistance training for soccer players and provide some examples of exercises to incorporate into your training regimen.

As a soccer player, you may have experienced challenges with endurance, speed, or agility on the field. Resistance training can help address these pain points by improving your overall muscular strength and power. By increasing your strength, you may be able to run faster, jump higher, and maintain your energy levels for longer periods of time.

The primary target of resistance training exercises for soccer players is to improve muscular strength, power, and endurance. Some examples of resistance training exercises for soccer players include squats, lunges, deadlifts, and plyometric exercises such as jump squats and box jumps. These exercises target the major muscle groups used in soccer, including the legs, core, and upper body.

Squats

One of the most effective resistance training exercises for soccer players is the squat. Squats target the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core muscles. To perform a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing forward. Lower your body down as if sitting in a chair, keeping your knees in line with your toes. As you lower your body, push your hips back and keep your chest lifted. Return to standing position and repeat.

Lunges

Lunges are another great resistance training exercise for soccer players. Lunges target the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. To perform a lunge, step forward with one foot and lower your body down until your knee is at a 90-degree angle. Keep your front knee in line with your toes and your back knee hovering just above the ground. Push back up to standing position and repeat on the other leg.

Deadlifts

Deadlifts are a more advanced resistance training exercise for soccer players, but they can be incredibly effective in building overall strength and power. Deadlifts target the entire posterior chain, including the glutes, hamstrings, and lower back. To perform a deadlift, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing forward. Keep your back straight and hinge at the hips, lowering down to grab a weight or barbell. Drive through your heels to lift the weight back up to standing position.

Plyometric Exercises

Plyometric exercises such as jump squats and box jumps are great for building explosive power and improving agility. To perform a jump squat, start in a squat position and jump as high as you can, landing softly back in the squat position. To perform a box jump, start with a box or step in front of you. Jump up onto the box, landing softly with both feet. Step down and repeat.

Benefits of Resistance Training for Soccer Players

Overall, resistance training can provide a wide range of benefits for soccer players. By improving muscular strength and power, you may be able to run faster, jump higher, and maintain your energy levels for longer periods of time. Resistance training can also help prevent injuries on the field by strengthening the muscles and joints.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should soccer players incorporate resistance training exercises into their training regimen?

A: Soccer players should aim to incorporate resistance training exercises into their training regimen at least 2-3 times per week, focusing on different muscle groups each session.

Q: Can resistance training exercises help improve endurance on the field?

A: Yes, resistance training can help improve muscular endurance on the field by increasing overall strength and power.

Q: Are there any specific resistance training exercises that can help prevent common soccer injuries?

A: Yes, exercises such as single-leg deadlifts and lateral lunges can help strengthen the muscles and joints that are commonly injured in soccer, such as the ankles and knees.

Q: Should soccer players use weights when performing resistance training exercises?

A: Yes, using weights can help increase the resistance and intensity of the exercises, leading to greater gains in muscular strength and power.

Conclusion of Resistance Training Exercises for Soccer Players

Resistance training exercises can be incredibly effective in improving muscular strength, power, and endurance for soccer players. By incorporating exercises such as squats, lunges, deadlifts, and plyometric exercises into your training regimen, you may be able to take your game to the next level and prevent injuries on the field.
