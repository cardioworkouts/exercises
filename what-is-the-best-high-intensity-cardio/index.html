Best High Intensity Cardio A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Sep 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking for a way to get in shape quickly and efficiently? High intensity cardio might be just what you need. However, with so many different types of cardio out there, it can be difficult to know which one is the best for you. In this article, we'll take a closer look at what high intensity cardio is, its benefits, and the best types of high intensity cardio to help you achieve your fitness goals.

High intensity cardio is a type of exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest. This type of workout can help you burn more calories in less time than traditional cardio exercises like running or cycling. High intensity cardio has been shown to improve cardiovascular health, increase metabolism, and help you lose weight.

The best type of high intensity cardio for you will depend on your fitness level, goals, and preferences. Some popular types of high intensity cardio include sprints, jump rope, plyometrics, and HIIT (high intensity interval training).

So, what is the best high intensity cardio for you? Let's take a closer look at some of the most popular types of high intensity cardio and their benefits.

Sprints

I have personally found sprints to be the most effective form of high-intensity cardio. Sprints are simple: sprint as fast as you can for a set distance or time, then rest and repeat. Sprints are an excellent way to increase cardiovascular fitness, burn fat, and build lean muscle.

Jump Rope

Jump rope is another great option for high intensity cardio. It's easy to do, requires minimal equipment, and can be done anywhere. Jump rope is an excellent way to improve cardiovascular fitness, coordination, and agility.

Plyometrics

Plyometrics involves explosive movements like jump squats, box jumps, and burpees. Plyometrics is a great way to build explosive power, speed, and agility. It's also an excellent way to burn fat and build lean muscle.

HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training)

HIIT involves short bursts of high intensity exercise followed by periods of rest. HIIT is an excellent way to improve cardiovascular fitness, burn fat, and build lean muscle. It's also a great way to increase metabolism and improve overall health.

Question and Answer

Q: Is high intensity cardio safe for beginners?

A: Yes, but it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio?

A: It's recommended to do high intensity cardio 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts.

Q: What should I eat before and after high intensity cardio?

A: It's important to eat a balanced meal containing carbohydrates and protein before your workout. After your workout, you should eat a meal containing protein and carbohydrates to help your muscles recover.

Q: Can high intensity cardio help me lose weight?

A: Yes, high intensity cardio can help you lose weight by burning more calories in less time than traditional cardio exercises.

Conclusion of Best High Intensity Cardio

High intensity cardio is an excellent way to improve cardiovascular fitness, burn fat, and build lean muscle. The best type of high intensity cardio for you will depend on your fitness level, goals, and preferences. It's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. By incorporating high intensity cardio into your fitness routine, you can achieve your fitness goals more quickly and efficiently.
