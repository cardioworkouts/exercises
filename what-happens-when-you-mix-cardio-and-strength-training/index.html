What Happens When You Mix Cardio And Strength Training

Written by Eveline Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who is looking to improve their overall fitness and health? Do you find yourself wondering if you should be focusing on cardio or strength training? What if we told you that you can have the best of both worlds by combining these two types of exercises? Mixing cardio and strength training can have a positive impact on your body and overall fitness level. In this article, we will explore what happens when you mix cardio and strength training, and how it can benefit you.

For many people, the idea of mixing cardio and strength training can be intimidating. They may worry that they will not be able to keep up with the intensity or that it will lead to muscle fatigue and soreness. However, with the proper planning and guidance, you can safely and effectively combine these two types of exercises to achieve your fitness goals.

So, what exactly happens when you mix cardio and strength training? Essentially, you are combining two different types of exercise that work different parts of your body. Cardio exercises, such as running or cycling, focus on improving your cardiovascular health and endurance. Strength training, on the other hand, uses resistance exercises to build muscle and improve strength.

By combining these two types of exercise, you can improve your overall fitness level by burning fat, building muscle, and increasing your endurance. Mixing cardio and strength training can also help to prevent injury by strengthening your muscles and improving your flexibility.

My Personal Experience with Mixing Cardio and Strength Training

As a personal trainer, I work with clients who are looking to improve their overall fitness level. One of the most common questions I get is whether they should focus on cardio or strength training. My answer is always the same: why not both? When I first started mixing cardio and strength training, I was worried that it would be too intense for me. However, with the proper guidance and planning, I was able to safely and effectively combine these two types of exercise to achieve my fitness goals.

What I noticed was that by mixing cardio and strength training, I was able to burn fat and build muscle at the same time. I also noticed that my endurance improved, which allowed me to push myself harder during my workouts. Overall, mixing cardio and strength training has helped me to achieve my fitness goals and has made me a stronger and healthier person.

The Benefits of Mixing Cardio and Strength Training

There are many benefits to mixing cardio and strength training. Here are just a few:

1. Burn More Calories

When you combine cardio and strength training, you are able to burn more calories than you would with just one type of exercise. Cardio exercises help to burn fat, while strength training helps to build muscle. By combining these two types of exercise, you can burn fat and build muscle at the same time, which can lead to a higher calorie burn.

2. Build Muscle

Strength training is essential for building muscle. By adding resistance exercises to your workout routine, you can increase your muscle mass and improve your strength. This can help you to lift heavier weights and perform other exercises more easily.

3. Improve Endurance

Cardio exercises are great for improving endurance. By adding cardio exercises to your workout routine, you can improve your cardiovascular health and increase your endurance. This can help you to perform other exercises for longer periods of time.

4. Prevent Injury

Strength training can help to prevent injury by strengthening your muscles and improving your flexibility. By adding strength training exercises to your workout routine, you can reduce your risk of injury and improve your overall fitness level.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I mix cardio and strength training?

A: It is recommended that you mix cardio and strength training at least three times a week for best results.

Q: What are some examples of cardio exercises?

A: Examples of cardio exercises include running, cycling, swimming, and dancing.

Q: What are some examples of strength training exercises?

A: Examples of strength training exercises include weightlifting, push-ups, squats, and lunges.

Q: Can I mix cardio and strength training in the same workout?

A: Yes, you can mix cardio and strength training in the same workout. However, it is important to plan your workout carefully and to make sure that you are not overexerting yourself.

Conclusion of What Happens When You Mix Cardio and Strength Training

Mixing cardio and strength training can have a positive impact on your body and overall fitness level. By combining these two types of exercise, you can burn fat, build muscle, and increase your endurance. It is important to plan your workout carefully and to seek guidance from a professional if you are new to mixing cardio and strength training. With the proper planning and guidance, you can safely and effectively combine these two types of exercise to achieve your fitness goals.
