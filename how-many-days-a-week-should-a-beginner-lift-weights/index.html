How Many Days A Week Should A Beginner Lift Weights

Written by Christine Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a beginner in weightlifting and wondering how many days a week you should lift weights? You're not alone. Many beginners struggle with this question, and with so much conflicting information out there, it can be overwhelming to figure out what works best for you.

The short answer is that beginners should aim to lift weights two to three times a week. However, there are some factors to consider when determining the ideal frequency for weightlifting.

Firstly, your fitness goals will determine how often you should lift weights. Are you looking to build muscle, lose weight, or simply get stronger? Each goal requires a different approach.

Secondly, your current fitness level should be considered. If you're new to exercise or have been inactive for a while, it's best to start with two days a week and gradually increase to three as your fitness improves.

In summary, beginners should aim to lift weights two to three times a week, depending on their fitness goals and current fitness level.

Personal Experience with Lifting Weights Twice a Week

When I first started weightlifting, I was unsure of how often I should lift weights. After doing some research, I decided to start with twice a week. I focused on compound exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press, and gradually increased the weight and volume over time.

I found that lifting weights twice a week allowed me to recover properly, and I saw significant improvements in my strength and muscle mass. As a beginner, it's important to not overdo it, as this can lead to injury and burnout.

The Importance of Rest and Recovery

Rest and recovery are crucial components of any fitness routine, especially weightlifting. When you lift weights, you're breaking down muscle fibers, and it's during rest periods that these fibers repair and grow stronger.

It's recommended to have at least one rest day in between weightlifting sessions to allow for proper recovery. This means that if you lift weights twice a week, you should have at least two days of rest in between sessions.

The Benefits of Weightlifting

Weightlifting has numerous benefits for beginners, including improved strength, muscle mass, bone density, and metabolism. It can also boost self-confidence and overall well-being.

However, it's important to note that weightlifting should be done in conjunction with a balanced diet and other forms of exercise, such as cardio and stretching.

Tips for Beginners

As a beginner in weightlifting, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the weight and volume over time. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

- Focus on proper form to prevent injury
- Incorporate compound exercises into your routine
- Gradually increase weight and volume
- Allow for proper rest and recovery
- Eat a balanced diet to support muscle growth and recovery

Personal Experience with Lifting Weights Three Times a Week

After lifting weights twice a week for a few months, I felt ready to increase my frequency to three times a week. I added in more accessory exercises such as bicep curls and lateral raises, and increased the volume of my workouts.

While I did see further improvements in my strength and muscle mass, I also noticed that I was more fatigued and sore. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard, especially as a beginner.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it okay to lift weights every day?

A: No, it's not recommended to lift weights every day, as this can lead to overtraining and injury. As a beginner, it's best to start with two to three days a week and gradually increase from there.

Q: Should I do cardio on the same days as weightlifting?

A: Yes, it's recommended to incorporate cardio into your routine, either on the same days as weightlifting or on alternate days. Cardio can improve overall fitness and aid in weight loss.

Q: Can I lift weights without a spotter?

A: While it's recommended to have a spotter for certain exercises, such as bench press, it's possible to lift weights without one. However, it's important to start with lighter weights and focus on proper form to prevent injury.

Q: How long should weightlifting sessions last?

A: Weightlifting sessions should last around 45 minutes to an hour, depending on your fitness level and goals. It's important to not overdo it and allow for proper rest and recovery.

Conclusion

As a beginner in weightlifting, it's important to find a frequency that works best for you. Two to three times a week is a good starting point, but it's important to consider your fitness goals and current fitness level. Remember to focus on proper form, rest and recovery, and a balanced diet to support muscle growth and overall health.
