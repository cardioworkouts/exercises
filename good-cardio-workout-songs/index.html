Get Your Heart Pumping With These Good Cardio Workout Songs

Written by Bardi Oct 11, 2023 · 5 min read

Working out can be tough, especially when you're trying to push yourself to your limits. Sometimes, you just need a little extra motivation to keep going. That's where good cardio workout songs come in! Music can help distract you from the pain, keep you motivated, and even help you push yourself harder. But with so many songs out there, it can be hard to know which ones will really get you going. In this post, we'll share some of the best cardio workout songs to help you power through your next sweat session.

If you've ever tried to work out without music, you know how boring it can be. Without a good playlist, it's easy to get distracted and lose focus. But not just any music will do the trick. When it comes to cardio, you need songs that are high-energy, fast-paced, and have a good beat. You want music that will get your heart pumping and make you want to move.

The target of good cardio workout songs is to help you stay motivated and push yourself harder during your workout. These songs are specifically chosen for their high-energy beats, motivational lyrics, and the ability to keep you focused and engaged. Some of the best cardio workout songs include "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor, "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake, "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars, and "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott.

In summary, good cardio workout songs are essential for a successful workout. They can help keep you motivated, focused, and energized, all while pushing you to your limits. Some of the best songs for cardio workouts include "Eye of the Tiger," "Can't Stop the Feeling," "Uptown Funk," and "Lose Control."

"Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

When it comes to good cardio workout songs, "Eye of the Tiger" is a classic. This song was written specifically for the movie Rocky III and has since become a staple in the world of fitness. With its high-energy beat and motivational lyrics, "Eye of the Tiger" will make you feel like a champion. I personally love listening to this song when I'm running on the treadmill. It helps me push through the pain and keep going, even when I feel like giving up.

"Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake

If you're looking for a more modern song to add to your cardio playlist, "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake is a great choice. This upbeat song has a catchy melody and a positive message, making it the perfect pick-me-up during a tough workout. I like to listen to this song when I'm doing a high-intensity interval training (HIIT) workout. The fast-paced beat helps me keep up with the intervals and stay focused.

"Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars

"Uptown Funk" is another great song for cardio workouts. With its funky beat and catchy lyrics, this song is sure to get you moving. I love listening to this song when I'm doing a dance workout or some other type of cardio that involves a lot of movement. It helps me loosen up and have fun while I'm working out.

Benefits of Listening to Good Cardio Workout Songs

Aside from the obvious benefit of making your workout more enjoyable, there are a number of other benefits to listening to good cardio workout songs. For one, music can help distract you from the pain and fatigue of exercise. This can help you push yourself harder and stay focused on your goals. Additionally, music has been shown to increase endurance and performance during exercise. According to a study published in the Journal of Sports Medicine and Physical Fitness, listening to music during exercise can increase endurance by up to 15%.

How to Choose Good Cardio Workout Songs

Choosing the right songs for your cardio workout can be a bit of a challenge. You want to make sure you're picking songs that will keep you motivated and energized throughout your entire workout. Here are a few tips for choosing good cardio workout songs:

1. Look for songs with a high BPM (beats per minute). Generally, songs with a BPM of 120-140 are best for cardio workouts.

2. Choose songs with motivational lyrics. Songs with positive, empowering lyrics can help you stay focused and motivated.

3. Pick songs that you enjoy. If you don't like the song, you're not going to want to listen to it during your workout.

"Lose Control" by Missy Elliott

If you're looking for a song that will really get your heart pumping, "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott is a great choice. This high-energy song has a BPM of 125, making it perfect for a fast-paced cardio workout. The lyrics are also super motivational, making it a great choice for those tough workouts where you need a little extra push.

Question and Answer

Q: Can listening to music really improve my workout?

A: Yes! Studies have shown that listening to music during exercise can help distract you from the pain and fatigue of working out, increase endurance, and improve performance.

Q: What are some good BPMs for cardio workout songs?

A: Generally, songs with a BPM of 120-140 are best for cardio workouts. However, this can vary depending on your individual fitness level and the type of workout you're doing.

Q: How can I create a good cardio workout playlist?

A: Start by choosing songs with a high BPM and motivational lyrics. Make sure you're picking songs that you enjoy and that will keep you energized throughout your entire workout.

Q: Can I listen to any type of music during my workout?

A: While there are certain types of music that are better suited for cardio workouts, ultimately, it's up to you to choose the music that motivates you and gets you moving.

Conclusion of Good Cardio Workout Songs

Good cardio workout songs are an essential part of any successful workout. They can help keep you motivated, focused, and energized, all while pushing you to your limits. Whether you prefer classic rock, pop hits, or hip-hop jams, there's a cardio workout song out there that's perfect for you. So put on your headphones, crank up the volume, and get ready to sweat!
