What Is Hiit Cardio Training

Written by Robby Jun 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym and not seeing any results? Do you want to burn fat and improve your cardiovascular fitness in a shorter amount of time? If so, you may want to try HIIT cardio training. This type of exercise has become increasingly popular in recent years due to its effectiveness and efficiency.

Many people struggle with finding the time and motivation to exercise regularly. Traditional cardio workouts, such as running on a treadmill or cycling, can be monotonous and time-consuming. Additionally, some people may experience a plateau in their progress and not see any significant changes in their body composition or fitness level.

HIIT, or high-intensity interval training, is a type of cardio workout that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or lower-intensity exercise. The goal of HIIT is to elevate your heart rate and push your body to its limits, which can lead to greater fat loss and cardiovascular benefits.

In this article, we will explain what HIIT cardio training is and how it works, the benefits of HIIT, how to incorporate HIIT into your fitness routine, and answer some common questions about this type of exercise.

My Personal Experience with HIIT Cardio Training

As a busy professional who doesn't have a lot of free time, I was looking for a way to get the most out of my workouts without spending hours at the gym. I started incorporating HIIT cardio training into my routine, and I was amazed at how quickly I saw results.

Not only did I burn more calories during my workouts, but I also noticed that my endurance and overall fitness level improved. I was able to push myself harder and longer during each session, and I felt more energized throughout the day.

One of the things I love about HIIT is that it can be done anywhere, with little to no equipment. I often do HIIT workouts in my living room, using only my bodyweight or a set of dumbbells.

How Does HIIT Cardio Training Work?

HIIT cardio training works by alternating between periods of high-intensity exercise and periods of rest or lower-intensity exercise. The high-intensity intervals are designed to push your body to its limits, while the rest periods allow you to recover and catch your breath.

During the high-intensity intervals, your heart rate increases, and you start to breathe heavily. This is known as the "anaerobic" phase, where your body is using its stored energy to power through the exercise. During the rest periods, your heart rate slows down, and you enter the "aerobic" phase, where your body is using oxygen to recover and prepare for the next interval.

The cycle of high-intensity and rest periods can be repeated for as little as 10 minutes or as long as an hour, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Benefits of HIIT Cardio Training

There are many benefits to incorporating HIIT cardio training into your fitness routine. Here are some of the most notable advantages:

  • Increased calorie burn
  • Improved cardiovascular fitness
  • Greater fat loss
  • Increased endurance and stamina
  • Higher metabolism
  • Time-efficient
  • Can be done anywhere, with little to no equipment

How to Incorporate HIIT Cardio Training into Your Fitness Routine

If you're interested in trying HIIT cardio training, here are some tips on how to get started:

  • Consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise program
  • Start with shorter intervals and work your way up to longer ones
  • Choose exercises that you enjoy and that challenge you
  • Alternate between different exercises to keep your workouts interesting
  • Be consistent and stick to a regular schedule

Sample HIIT Cardio Training Workout

Here is an example of a simple HIIT cardio workout that you can do at home with no equipment:

  1. Warm-up: 5 minutes of light cardio, such as jogging or jumping jacks
  2. High-intensity interval: 30 seconds of burpees
  3. Rest interval: 30 seconds of rest
  4. High-intensity interval: 30 seconds of mountain climbers
  5. Rest interval: 30 seconds of rest
  6. High-intensity interval: 30 seconds of jumping lunges
  7. Rest interval: 30 seconds of rest
  8. Repeat the cycle 3-4 times
  9. Cool-down: 5 minutes of stretching

Question and Answer

Q: Is HIIT cardio training suitable for beginners?

A: Yes, HIIT cardio training can be modified to suit any fitness level. Beginners should start with shorter intervals and lower intensity exercises and gradually work their way up to longer and more challenging intervals.

Q: How often should I do HIIT cardio training?

A: It is recommended to do HIIT cardio training 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between. It's important to give your body time to recover and avoid overtraining.

Q: What are some common mistakes to avoid when doing HIIT cardio training?

A: Some common mistakes include not warming up properly, not hydrating enough, using incorrect form during exercises, and not giving yourself enough time to recover between intervals.

Q: Can HIIT cardio training be done with equipment?

A: Yes, HIIT cardio training can be done with equipment such as dumbbells, resistance bands, medicine balls, and kettlebells. The type of equipment you use will depend on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of What is HIIT Cardio Training

HIIT cardio training is a highly effective and efficient way to burn fat, improve cardiovascular fitness, and increase endurance. By incorporating HIIT into your fitness routine, you can achieve your goals in less time and with less monotony. Remember to start slowly, modify the exercises to your fitness level, and be consistent with your workouts. With a little dedication and effort, you can get the results you want with HIIT cardio training.
