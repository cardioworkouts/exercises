How Many Cardio Days When Lifting A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 04, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who is into weightlifting and looking to incorporate cardio into your routine? Do you often wonder how many cardio days when lifting is optimal? If yes, then you are at the right place. In this article, we will guide you through everything you need to know about how many cardio days when lifting.

Pain Points Related to How Many Cardio Days When Lifting

For individuals who love to lift weights, incorporating cardio into their routine can be a daunting task. They often wonder how much cardio they need to do to maintain their gains while still burning unwanted fat. The fear of losing muscle mass is real, and many people are skeptical about the benefits of cardio. Moreover, some individuals find it hard to balance both cardio and weightlifting in their schedule, leading to confusion about how many cardio days when lifting are required.

Answering the Target of How Many Cardio Days When Lifting

The answer to how many cardio days when lifting depends on your goals, your fitness level, and your schedule. If your main goal is to lose weight, then cardio can be beneficial, and you can do it 3-4 times a week. However, if you are trying to maintain muscle mass while burning fat, then you can do cardio 2-3 times a week.

It is important to remember that weightlifting should be your primary focus, and cardio should be used to supplement your weightlifting routine. Also, the intensity and duration of your cardio session should be based on your fitness level and goals.

Summary of How Many Cardio Days When Lifting

In summary, how many cardio days when lifting depends on your goals, fitness level, and schedule. If your goal is to lose weight, then 3-4 cardio sessions a week can be beneficial. However, if you want to maintain muscle mass while burning fat, 2-3 cardio sessions a week are enough. It is important to remember that weightlifting should be your primary focus, and cardio should be used to supplement your weightlifting routine.

How Many Cardio Days When Lifting and Its Target

As mentioned earlier, the target of how many cardio days when lifting depends on your goals, fitness level, and schedule. For me personally, I like to do 2-3 cardio sessions a week of moderate intensity for 30-45 minutes. I find this to be the optimal amount of cardio that doesn't interfere with my weightlifting routine while still helping me maintain a healthy weight.

It's important to note that everyone's body is different, and what works for me may not work for you. The key is to experiment and find a routine that works best for your body and lifestyle.

Benefits of Cardio When Lifting

Cardio has numerous benefits when it comes to weightlifting. It can help improve your endurance, increase your lung capacity, and reduce the risk of heart disease. Additionally, cardio can also help with weight loss and muscle definition, leading to a leaner physique.

Moreover, incorporating cardio into your routine can help break through plateaus in your weightlifting journey. It can help shock your body into new growth by increasing blood flow to your muscles and improving your overall fitness level.

How to Incorporate Cardio When Lifting

When it comes to incorporating cardio when lifting, there are a few things to keep in mind. Firstly, it is essential to prioritize weightlifting over cardio. You should never sacrifice your weightlifting routine for cardio as weightlifting is the primary way to build muscle mass.

Secondly, it is important to choose the right type of cardio for your goals. If your goal is to lose weight, then high-intensity interval training (HIIT) can be beneficial, while low-intensity steady-state (LISS) cardio is recommended for maintaining muscle mass.

Lastly, it is crucial to find a balance between cardio and weightlifting. You can either perform cardio on the same day as weightlifting or on alternate days, depending on your schedule and fitness level.

Tips for Cardio When Lifting

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when incorporating cardio when lifting:

  • Start slow and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your cardio sessions
  • Choose a form of cardio that you enjoy to make it more sustainable
  • Always prioritize weightlifting over cardio
  • Find a balance between cardio and weightlifting
  • Listen to your body and adjust your routine accordingly

FAQs About How Many Cardio Days When Lifting

Q. Can cardio affect muscle gains?

A. Yes, excessive cardio can lead to muscle loss. Therefore, it is important to find a balance between cardio and weightlifting.

Q. Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting?

A. It is recommended to do cardio after lifting as weightlifting is the primary focus.

Q. Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A. Yes, you can do cardio and weightlifting on the same day, but it is essential to prioritize weightlifting over cardio.

Q. How long should my cardio sessions be?

A. The duration of your cardio sessions should be based on your fitness level and goals. A moderate-intensity cardio session of 30-45 minutes is recommended.

Conclusion of How Many Cardio Days When Lifting

In conclusion, how many cardio days when lifting is a personal preference that depends on your fitness level, goals, and schedule. While cardio has numerous benefits when it comes to weightlifting, it is important to remember that weightlifting should always be your primary focus. By finding a balance between cardio and weightlifting and incorporating cardio in a sustainable manner, you can achieve your fitness goals while maintaining muscle mass.
