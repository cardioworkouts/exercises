What Are Good High Intensity Workouts A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to take your fitness routine to the next level? High intensity workouts might be just what you need. These workouts are designed to get your heart pumping and your muscles working, helping you burn fat, build muscle, and improve your overall fitness. In this post, we'll explore what are good high intensity workouts and how you can incorporate them into your routine.

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym without seeing results? Do you struggle to find the motivation to exercise? These are common pain points for many people, but high intensity workouts may be the solution. By pushing yourself to your limits in short bursts, you can achieve better results in less time and stay motivated by seeing progress quickly.

So what are good high intensity workouts? High intensity interval training, or HIIT, is one of the most popular types of workouts. This involves short bursts of intense exercise, followed by periods of rest or low-intensity activity. Other examples include circuit training, Tabata, and plyometrics. The key is to keep your heart rate elevated and challenge your body in new ways.

In summary, what are good high intensity workouts? They are workouts that challenge your body and push you to your limits. By incorporating HIIT, circuit training, Tabata, or plyometrics into your routine, you can achieve better results in less time.

High Intensity Interval Training

One of the most popular types of high intensity workouts is HIIT. This involves short bursts of intense exercise, followed by periods of rest or low-intensity activity. For example, you might sprint for 30 seconds, rest for 10 seconds, and then repeat for several rounds. This type of workout can be done with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, rowing, and bodyweight exercises.

Personally, I love doing HIIT workouts because they are challenging and effective. I always feel like I've accomplished something when I finish a tough HIIT session. Plus, they are great for burning fat and improving cardiovascular health.

Circuit Training

Circuit training involves performing a series of exercises back-to-back with little to no rest in between. This type of workout can be done with weights, bodyweight exercises, or a combination of both. The goal is to keep your heart rate elevated and challenge your muscles in new ways.

I enjoy circuit training because it keeps me engaged and prevents boredom. Plus, it's a great way to work multiple muscle groups at once.

Tabata

Tabata is a type of HIIT workout that involves 20 seconds of intense exercise followed by 10 seconds of rest, repeated for four minutes. This type of workout can be done with a variety of exercises, including bodyweight exercises, weights, and cardio machines.

I have found Tabata to be particularly effective for improving my endurance and burning fat. Plus, the short intervals make the workout fly by.

Plyometrics

Plyometrics involve explosive movements that require a lot of power, such as jumping or sprinting. This type of workout can be done with bodyweight exercises or with equipment like medicine balls or resistance bands.

I love adding plyometrics to my routine because they challenge me in new ways and help me improve my power and speed. Plus, they are a lot of fun!

Question and Answer

Q: Are high intensity workouts suitable for beginners?

A: Yes, high intensity workouts can be modified for any fitness level. It's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity as you get stronger.

Q: How often should I do high intensity workouts?

A: It's recommended to do high intensity workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between. It's important to listen to your body and not overdo it.

Q: Can high intensity workouts help me lose weight?

A: Yes, high intensity workouts are great for burning fat and improving overall fitness. However, it's important to pair them with a healthy diet for best results.

Q: Do I need equipment to do high intensity workouts?

A: No, many high intensity workouts can be done with just your bodyweight. However, equipment like weights, resistance bands, and cardio machines can add variety and challenge to your routine.

Conclusion of what are good high intensity workouts

In conclusion, high intensity workouts are a great way to challenge your body and achieve better results in less time. By incorporating HIIT, circuit training, Tabata, or plyometrics into your routine, you can improve your fitness and stay motivated. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and have fun!
