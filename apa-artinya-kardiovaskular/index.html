Q: What is the difference between cardiovascular disease and heart disease?

A: Cardiovascular disease refers to any condition that affects the heart and blood vessels, while heart disease specifically refers to conditions that affect the heart muscle.

Q: What are the risk factors for cardiovascular disease?

A: Risk factors for cardiovascular disease include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, family history of cardiovascular disease, and age.

Q: How can I prevent cardiovascular disease?

A: You can prevent cardiovascular disease by maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including eating a healthy diet, getting regular exercise, avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, and managing any existing medical conditions.

A: The warning signs of a heart attack include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, lightheadedness or fainting, nausea or vomiting, and pain or discomfort in the arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach.
