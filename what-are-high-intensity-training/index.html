What Are High Intensity Training A Beginner s Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym with little results? Do you want to take your fitness to the next level? High intensity training may be the answer you're looking for.

Many people struggle with finding the time and motivation to exercise regularly. Others may hit a plateau in their fitness journey, where they no longer see results despite their efforts. These are common pain points that high intensity training can address.

High intensity training, also known as HIT, is a form of exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity. The goal is to push your body to its limits in a short amount of time, rather than spending hours at a moderate intensity.

In this article, we'll explore what high intensity training is, its benefits, and how to get started.

What Are High Intensity Training?

High intensity training involves performing exercises at a maximum effort for a short period of time, followed by a period of rest. This cycle is repeated for a set number of rounds or time. The idea is to challenge your body with short bursts of intense activity, rather than spending hours at a moderate intensity.

High intensity training can be done with a variety of exercises, such as sprinting, weightlifting, or plyometrics. The key is to push yourself to your limit during each round.

When done correctly, high intensity training can have many benefits, such as:

  • Increased calorie burn
  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased muscle strength and endurance
  • Improved insulin sensitivity
  • Reduced risk of chronic diseases

My Personal Experience with High Intensity Training

Before I discovered high intensity training, I struggled to stay motivated at the gym. I would spend hours on the treadmill or elliptical with little results to show for it.

Once I started incorporating high intensity training into my routine, I noticed a significant difference in my fitness level. I was able to push myself harder during each workout and saw results much faster than before.

One of my favorite high intensity workouts is a Tabata-style routine, which involves performing an exercise at maximum effort for 20 seconds, followed by a 10-second rest. This cycle is repeated for eight rounds, totaling four minutes.

How to Get Started with High Intensity Training

If you're new to high intensity training, it's important to start slow and gradually build up your endurance. Here are some tips to get started:

  • Consult with a doctor before starting a new exercise routine
  • Start with shorter workouts and gradually increase the intensity and duration
  • Fuel your body with healthy foods and stay hydrated
  • Focus on proper form to prevent injury
  • Listen to your body and take breaks when needed

FAQs About High Intensity Training

1. Is high intensity training safe for beginners?

Yes, high intensity training can be safe for beginners as long as they start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of their workouts. It's important to consult with a doctor before starting a new exercise routine.

2. How often should I do high intensity training?

It's recommended to do high intensity training 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts.

3. Can high intensity training help with weight loss?

Yes, high intensity training can help with weight loss by increasing calorie burn and improving insulin sensitivity.

4. What are some examples of high intensity training exercises?

Some examples of high intensity training exercises include sprinting, weightlifting, plyometrics, and high-intensity interval training (HIIT).

Conclusion of What Are High Intensity Training

High intensity training can be a game-changer for those looking to take their fitness to the next level. By challenging your body with short bursts of intense activity, you can see results faster and more efficiently than with traditional moderate intensity workouts. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced gym-goer, high intensity training can help you reach your fitness goals.
