Best Shoulder Workouts Without Equipment A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Aug 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for effective shoulder workouts that don’t require any equipment? If so, you’re in the right place. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned fitness enthusiast, finding workouts that target your shoulders without any equipment can be a challenge. But don't worry, we’ve got you covered.

In this article, we’ll be discussing the best shoulder workouts that require no equipment. We’ll cover everything from the target of these workouts to how to perform them correctly. So, let’s get started!

To summarize, this article will provide you with a complete guide to the best shoulder workouts that require no equipment. You’ll learn about the target of these workouts and how to perform them correctly. So, let’s dive in!

Shoulder Press

Shoulder press is a great exercise that targets your shoulders, upper back, and triceps. To perform this exercise, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and place your hands on your shoulders. Push your hands upwards until they are straight above your head. Hold for a few seconds and then slowly lower your hands back to the starting position. Repeat this exercise for 3 sets of 10 reps.

Personally, I’ve found that shoulder press is an excellent exercise for building shoulder strength and improving posture. It’s also a great exercise to incorporate into your daily routine, as it can be done without any equipment.

Push-ups

Push-ups are an excellent exercise for targeting your shoulders, chest, and triceps. To perform this exercise, start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart. Lower your body down until your chest touches the ground, and then push yourself back up. Repeat this exercise for 3 sets of 10 reps.

Push-ups are a classic exercise that can be done anywhere, anytime, without any equipment. They’re also a great exercise for building upper body strength.

Bent-Over Lateral Raises

Bent-over lateral raises are a great exercise for targeting your shoulders and upper back. To perform this exercise, bend over at the waist and let your arms hang straight down. Raise your arms out to the side until they are parallel to the ground. Hold for a few seconds and then lower your arms back to the starting position. Repeat this exercise for 3 sets of 10 reps.

Bent-over lateral raises are a great exercise for improving shoulder and back strength. They’re also an effective exercise for improving posture.

Plank Taps

Plank taps are a great exercise for targeting your shoulders, core, and obliques. To perform this exercise, start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart. Tap your right hand to your left shoulder, and then tap your left hand to your right shoulder. Repeat this exercise for 3 sets of 10 reps.

Plank taps are a great exercise for improving shoulder stability and core strength. They’re also an excellent exercise for improving overall posture.

Conclusion of Best Shoulder Workouts Without Equipment

So there you have it, a comprehensive guide to the best shoulder workouts that require no equipment. Incorporate these exercises into your daily routine, and you’ll be well on your way to building strong, healthy shoulders. Remember to always perform these exercises with proper form and technique to avoid injury.

If you have any questions or comments, feel free to leave them below!

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do these exercises every day?

A: It’s generally recommended to give your muscles a break in between workouts, so aim to do these exercises every other day.

Q: How many reps should I do?

A: Aim for 3 sets of 10 reps for each exercise.

Q: Can I modify these exercises to make them easier or harder?

A: Yes, you can modify these exercises to suit your fitness level. For example, if push-ups are too difficult, try doing them on your knees. If you want to make them harder, try doing diamond push-ups.

Q: Are these exercises safe for people with shoulder injuries?

A: It’s always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine, especially if you have a pre-existing injury.
