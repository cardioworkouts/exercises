Should You Do Cardio Before Or After Lifting To Lose Weight

Written by Bardi Nov 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you looking to shed some extra pounds and wondering whether you should do cardio before or after lifting? This is a common question many people have when trying to lose weight. It can be challenging to figure out the best approach, especially when conflicting information is available online. In this blog post, we will discuss the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before or after lifting to help you determine which method is right for you.

When trying to lose weight, many people focus solely on cardio exercises such as running, cycling, or swimming. However, strength training is equally important as it helps to build muscles, which boosts metabolism and burns more calories even at rest. Therefore, combining cardio and strength training is the most effective way to lose weight and tone your body.

If your goal is to build muscle and get stronger, you should always lift before doing cardio. This is because strength training requires more energy and focus, which can be compromised if you are already tired from doing cardio. By lifting first, you can ensure that you are giving your muscles the attention they need to grow and develop. Additionally, lifting before doing cardio helps to warm up your body, which can help you perform better during your cardio workout.

On the other hand, if your primary goal is to burn fat and lose weight, doing cardio before lifting may be more effective. Research has shown that doing cardio first can lead to a higher calorie burn during the workout, which can result in greater weight loss over time. Additionally, doing cardio first can help to warm up your body and increase your heart rate, which can prepare you for a more intense lifting session.

In summary, the best approach to cardio and lifting depends on your goals. If you want to build muscle and get stronger, always lift first. If your primary goal is to burn fat and lose weight, doing cardio before lifting may be more effective.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Lifting

Personally, I prefer doing cardio before lifting as it helps me to warm up my body and prepare for a more intense lifting session. I usually start with 20-30 minutes of cardio to get my heart rate up, followed by 45 minutes of lifting. I find that doing cardio first also helps me to stay focused during my lifting session as I am already feeling energized and motivated. Additionally, I have noticed that doing cardio before lifting helps me to burn more calories overall, which has contributed to my weight loss journey.

The Benefits of Doing Lifting Before Cardio

While I prefer doing cardio before lifting, I understand that lifting before cardio may be more beneficial for some people. By lifting first, you can ensure that you are giving your muscles the attention they need to grow and develop. This is especially important if you are trying to build muscle and increase strength. Additionally, lifting before cardio can help you to perform better during your cardio workout as your body will already be warmed up.

The Importance of Combining Cardio and Lifting

It's important to note that combining cardio and lifting is the most effective way to lose weight and tone your body. Cardio helps to burn calories and fat, while lifting helps to build muscle and boost metabolism. Therefore, it's essential to incorporate both types of exercises into your fitness routine. Additionally, alternating between cardio and lifting can help to prevent boredom and keep your workouts challenging and engaging.

How to Incorporate Cardio and Lifting into Your Fitness Routine

If you're new to cardio and lifting, it's essential to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. Aim to do cardio and lifting at least three times a week, with at least one rest day in between. Additionally, make sure to warm up before each workout to prevent injury and cool down afterward to help your body recover. If you're not sure where to start, consider hiring a personal trainer who can create a customized workout plan based on your fitness goals and needs.

Personal Experience

Personally, I have found that incorporating both cardio and lifting into my fitness routine has been the most effective way to lose weight and tone my body. I usually do cardio first to warm up my body and get my heart rate up, followed by lifting to build muscle and increase strength. I find that alternating between the two types of exercises helps to keep my workouts challenging and engaging. Additionally, I have noticed that combining cardio and lifting has helped me to burn more calories overall, which has contributed to my weight loss journey.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I do cardio or lifting first?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle and get stronger, always lift first. If your primary goal is to burn fat and lose weight, doing cardio before lifting may be more effective.

Q: How often should I do cardio and lifting?

A: Aim to do cardio and lifting at least three times a week, with at least one rest day in between.

Q: Is it better to do cardio and lifting on the same day or separate days?

A: It's up to personal preference. Some people prefer to do cardio and lifting on the same day, while others prefer to do them on separate days. It's important to listen to your body and do what feels best for you.

Q: Do I need to hire a personal trainer to create a fitness plan?

A: While it's not necessary to hire a personal trainer, it can be helpful for those who are new to cardio and lifting or need extra motivation and accountability. A personal trainer can create a customized workout plan based on your fitness goals and needs.

Conclusion

When it comes to cardio and lifting, there is no one-size-fits-all approach. The best approach depends on your goals and personal preferences. If your goal is to build muscle and get stronger, always lift first. If your primary goal is to burn fat and lose weight, doing cardio before lifting may be more effective. Remember to incorporate both types of exercises into your fitness routine to achieve the best results. Always listen to your body and do what feels best for you.
