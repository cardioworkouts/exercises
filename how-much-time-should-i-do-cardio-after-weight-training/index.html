How Much Time Should I Do Cardio After Weight Training

Written by Daniel Aug 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who likes to hit the weights at the gym and then jump onto a cardio machine? Do you find yourself wondering how much time you should spend on cardio after weight training? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with figuring out the right balance between strength training and cardio, and how much time to dedicate to each. In this article, we'll answer the question of how much time you should do cardio after weight training and related keywords.

The idea of incorporating cardio into your workout routine after weight training can be daunting, especially if you don't know how much time you should dedicate to it. It can also be difficult to know if you're overworking your body or not doing enough.

The general recommendation for how much time you should do cardio after weight training is around 20-30 minutes. This amount of time is enough to get your heart rate up, burn some extra calories, and improve your cardiovascular health without overworking your body. However, the amount of time you should spend on cardio after weight training ultimately depends on your fitness goals, your current fitness level, and your overall workout routine.

In summary, the ideal amount of time you should do cardio after weight training is around 20-30 minutes. However, it's important to consider your individual fitness goals and workout routine to find the right balance for you.

How Much Time Should I Do Cardio After Weight Training: Personal Experience

Personally, I like to spend about 25 minutes on the elliptical after my weight training sessions. I find that this amount of time helps me to burn some extra calories and get my heart rate up without feeling overworked. It also helps me to cool down after my weight training and stretch out any sore muscles.

Factors to Consider When Deciding How Much Time to Do Cardio After Weight Training

When deciding how much time to spend on cardio after weight training, there are a few factors to consider. These include:

1. Fitness Goals

Your fitness goals will play a big role in how much time you should spend on cardio after weight training. If you're looking to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health, you may want to spend more time on cardio. If you're looking to build muscle, you may want to spend less time on cardio and more time on strength training.

2. Current Fitness Level

Your current fitness level should also be taken into consideration when deciding how much time to spend on cardio after weight training. If you're new to working out, you may want to start with shorter cardio sessions and gradually work your way up. If you're already in good shape, you may be able to handle longer cardio sessions.

3. Workout Routine

Your overall workout routine should also be considered when deciding how much time to spend on cardio after weight training. If you're doing a full-body workout, you may want to spend less time on cardio so that you have enough energy for your strength training exercises. If you're only working on one muscle group, you may have more energy to devote to cardio.

4. Personal Preference

Ultimately, how much time you spend on cardio after weight training also comes down to personal preference. Some people enjoy cardio and want to spend more time on it, while others prefer strength training and want to focus on that. It's important to find a balance that works for you and makes you feel good.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to do cardio after weight training?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio after weight training, but it can be beneficial for your overall health and fitness goals.

Q: Can too much cardio after weight training be harmful?

A: Yes, too much cardio after weight training can lead to overtraining, which can cause fatigue, injury, and other health problems.

Q: How often should I do cardio after weight training?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and workout routine. Ideally, you should aim to do cardio after weight training 2-3 times per week.

Q: Can I do cardio before weight training instead?

A: Yes, you can do cardio before weight training if you prefer. Just be sure to warm up properly before your weight training session.

Conclusion of How Much Time Should I Do Cardio After Weight Training

In conclusion, the amount of time you should do cardio after weight training depends on your individual fitness goals and workout routine. However, a general recommendation is around 20-30 minutes. It's important to find a balance that works for you and makes you feel good, while also taking into consideration your current fitness level and overall health. By following these tips, you'll be able to incorporate cardio into your workout routine after weight training without overworking your body.
