How To Build Muscle Endurance For Push Ups A Beginner s Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Push ups are a great exercise for building upper body strength, but many people struggle with completing even a few reps. If you're looking to build muscle endurance for push ups, you're not alone. This is a common pain point for many fitness enthusiasts, especially beginners. However, with the right techniques and training, you can improve your push up game and reach your fitness goals.

In this article, we'll cover some effective strategies for building muscle endurance for push ups. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced fitness enthusiast, you'll find some helpful tips and tricks to take your push up game to the next level.

My Personal Experience with Building Muscle Endurance for Push Ups

When I first started working out, push ups were a real challenge for me. I could barely complete a few reps without collapsing on the floor. But I was determined to improve my strength and build muscle endurance for push ups. Over time, I developed a few strategies that helped me achieve my goals.

One of the most effective techniques I found was practicing variations of push ups. For example, I started with knee push ups and gradually worked my way up to full push ups. This helped me build strength and endurance in my upper body muscles, and I was able to perform more reps with ease.

How to Build Muscle Endurance for Push Ups: Tips and Tricks

Here are some strategies you can use to build muscle endurance for push ups:

1. Practice Push Ups Regularly

The more you practice push ups, the better you'll get at them. Start by doing a few reps every day, and gradually increase the number of reps as you get stronger. You can also try different variations of push ups to challenge your muscles and build endurance.

2. Focus on Proper Form

Proper form is crucial for building muscle endurance for push ups. Make sure your body is in a straight line, your hands are shoulder-width apart, and your elbows are tucked in. Keep your core engaged and breathe deeply as you perform each rep.

3. Incorporate Strength Training Exercises

Strength training exercises like bench presses, dumbbell flyes, and tricep extensions can help you build the muscles you need to perform more push ups. Incorporate these exercises into your workout routine to improve your overall upper body strength.

4. Try Plyometric Exercises

Plyometric exercises like clap push ups, explosive push ups, and plyometric push ups can help you build explosive power and endurance in your upper body muscles. These exercises are more challenging than regular push ups, so start with a few reps and gradually increase as you get stronger.

Question and Answer

Q: How many push ups should I do to build endurance?

A: Start with a few reps every day and gradually increase the number of reps as you get stronger. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 reps, with short breaks in between sets.

Q: How long does it take to build muscle endurance for push ups?

A: It depends on your fitness level and how often you train. With regular practice and dedication, you can expect to see improvements in 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I build muscle endurance for push ups without weights?

A: Yes, push ups are a great bodyweight exercise that can help you build muscle endurance without weights. However, incorporating strength training exercises can also help you build the muscles you need to perform more push ups.

Q: What if I can't do a full push up?

A: Start with knee push ups or incline push ups until you build enough strength to perform a full push up. Gradually decrease the incline or move to full push ups as you get stronger.

Conclusion of How to Build Muscle Endurance for Push Ups

Building muscle endurance for push ups takes time, dedication, and practice. By incorporating these tips and tricks into your workout routine, you can improve your upper body strength and perform more push ups with ease. Remember to focus on proper form, practice regularly, and challenge your muscles with different variations of push ups and strength training exercises. With persistence and patience, you'll be able to achieve your fitness goals and build the body you've always wanted.
