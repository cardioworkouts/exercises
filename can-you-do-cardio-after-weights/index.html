Can You Do Cardio After Weights

Written by Robby Oct 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those gym-goers who loves to lift weights but hates the thought of doing cardio? Or maybe you're the opposite, and you love to run but avoid the weight section like the plague. Either way, you might be wondering if it's okay to do cardio after weights, or if it's better to stick to one type of exercise per session. In this post, we'll answer that question and give you some tips for incorporating both types of exercise into your routine.

Pain Points of Can You Do Cardio After Weights

For many people, the idea of doing both weights and cardio in the same session can be daunting. They might worry that they won't have enough energy for both, or that they'll be too tired to do a good job at either one. Others might be concerned that doing cardio after weights will hinder their muscle gains, or that lifting weights before cardio will leave them feeling too fatigued to do a proper cardio workout. These are all valid concerns, and we'll address them in the next section.

Answering Can You Do Cardio After Weights

The short answer is: yes, you can do cardio after weights. In fact, doing both types of exercise in the same session can be highly effective for achieving your fitness goals. The key is to plan your workout carefully so that you're not overexerting yourself and compromising your performance.

The best way to structure a weights-and-cardio workout is to start with weights and finish with cardio. This is because weightlifting requires more energy and effort than cardio, so you want to make sure you're fresh and energized at the beginning of your workout. Doing cardio after weights is also a great way to cool down and stretch your muscles, which can help prevent soreness and injury.

Another option is to split your workouts into two separate sessions, with weights on one day and cardio on another. This can be a good choice if you have limited time or if you prefer to focus more on one type of exercise than the other.

Summary of Can You Do Cardio After Weights

In summary, it's definitely possible to do cardio after weights, and it can be a great way to get a full-body workout and burn more calories. To make the most of your workouts, start with weights and finish with cardio, or split your workouts into two separate sessions. By planning ahead and listening to your body, you can achieve your fitness goals and enjoy the benefits of both types of exercise.

Personal Experience with Can You Do Cardio After Weights

As a personal trainer, I often recommend incorporating both weights and cardio into my clients' workouts. One of my favorite ways to do this is to start with a 20-30 minute weightlifting routine that targets the major muscle groups, followed by 20-30 minutes of cardio on the treadmill or bike. I find that this combination helps my clients build strength and endurance, and it keeps their workouts interesting and challenging.

Tips for Can You Do Cardio After Weights

If you're new to combining weights and cardio, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with a warm-up to get your blood flowing and your muscles ready for exercise.
  • Choose weights and cardio exercises that complement each other, such as squats and lunges followed by running or biking.
  • Be sure to stretch after your workout to prevent soreness and injury.
  • Listen to your body and adjust your workout as needed. If you're feeling too fatigued or sore, take a break or switch to a lower-intensity exercise.

Question and Answer

Q: Will doing cardio after weights hinder my muscle gains?

A: No, as long as you're not overexerting yourself and you're following a proper strength-training program, doing cardio after weights should not hinder your muscle gains. In fact, it can help improve your cardiovascular health and boost your endurance, which can enhance your overall fitness performance.

Q: Should I do weights and cardio in the same session or split them up?

A: This depends on your personal preferences and fitness goals. If you have limited time or prefer to focus more on one type of exercise than the other, splitting your workouts into two separate sessions might be a better choice. However, if you want to get a full-body workout and burn more calories, doing both weights and cardio in the same session can be highly effective.

Q: Is it better to lift weights before or after cardio?

A: Generally, it's better to lift weights before cardio. This is because weightlifting requires more energy and effort than cardio, so you want to make sure you're fresh and energized at the beginning of your workout. Doing cardio after weights is also a great way to cool down and stretch your muscles, which can help prevent soreness and injury.

Q: How can I structure a weights-and-cardio workout?

A: The best way to structure a weights-and-cardio workout is to start with weights and finish with cardio. This will ensure that you're fresh and energized for your weightlifting routine, and it will help you cool down and stretch your muscles after your cardio workout. Alternatively, you can split your workouts into two separate sessions, with weights on one day and cardio on another.

Conclusion of Can You Do Cardio After Weights

In conclusion, doing cardio after weights is not only possible, but it can be highly effective for achieving your fitness goals. By planning your workouts carefully, listening to your body, and incorporating both types of exercise into your routine, you can build strength, endurance, and overall fitness. So next time you hit the gym, don't be afraid to mix things up and try a weights-and-cardio combo!
