Best Back Exercises At Home Without Equipment

Written by Eveline Nov 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you constantly feeling pain in your back? Do you find it hard to do simple tasks like picking up something from the ground or even sitting for long hours? Back pain can be a real nuisance and can limit your mobility. But fear not, as there are many exercises you can do at home without any equipment to strengthen your back muscles and alleviate the pain.

Back pain is a common problem faced by people of all ages and can be caused by various reasons such as poor posture, lack of exercise, or even injury. However, with the right exercises, you can improve your back strength and flexibility, and reduce the pain.

In this article, we will discuss the best back exercises at home without equipment that you can incorporate into your daily routine to get rid of back pain and improve your overall health.

Back Extension Exercise

Back extension is a great exercise to strengthen your lower back muscles. To perform this exercise:

  1. Lie on your stomach with your hands placed on your lower back
  2. Lift your chest and upper body off the ground using your lower back muscles
  3. Hold for 5-10 seconds and then slowly lower back down
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps

This exercise targets the erector spinae muscles in your lower back and helps improve your posture.

Bridge Exercise

The bridge exercise is a great exercise to target your glutes, lower back, and core muscles. To perform this exercise:

  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground
  2. Lift your hips off the ground until your body forms a straight line from your knees to your shoulders
  3. Hold for 5-10 seconds and then slowly lower back down
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps

This exercise helps improve your posture and strengthens your lower back and glutes.

Bird Dog Exercise

The bird dog exercise targets your core, lower back, and glutes. To perform this exercise:

  1. Start on your hands and knees with your hands directly under your shoulders and knees under your hips
  2. Extend your right arm and left leg out straight until they are parallel to the ground
  3. Hold for 5-10 seconds and then switch sides
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps on each side

This exercise helps improve your balance, stability, and posture.

Superman Exercise

The superman exercise targets your lower back muscles and helps improve your posture. To perform this exercise:

  1. Lie on your stomach with your arms extended out in front of you and your legs straight out behind you
  2. Lift your arms, chest, and legs off the ground at the same time
  3. Hold for 5-10 seconds and then slowly lower back down
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps

This exercise helps strengthen your lower back and improve your posture.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do these exercises?

A: It is recommended to do these exercises at least 2-3 times per week. However, if you have severe back pain, it is best to consult with your healthcare provider before starting any exercise routine.

Q: Can these exercises help with upper back pain?

A: While these exercises primarily target the lower back muscles, they can also help improve upper back strength and flexibility.

Q: Can I do these exercises if I have a herniated disc?

A: It is best to consult with your healthcare provider before starting any exercise routine if you have a herniated disc. They may recommend modified versions of these exercises or other exercises that are more suitable for your condition.

Q: Can these exercises be done by beginners?

A: Yes, these exercises are suitable for beginners. However, it is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and repetitions over time.

Conclusion of Best Back Exercises at Home without Equipment

Back pain can be a real hindrance to your daily life, but with the right exercises, you can improve your back strength and flexibility, and alleviate the pain. The back extension, bridge, bird dog, and superman exercises are great exercises you can do at home without any equipment. Remember to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and repetitions over time. If you have severe back pain, it is best to consult with your healthcare provider before starting any exercise routine.
