Do You Have To Do Cardio After Weight Training

Written by William Oct 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering if you need to do cardio after weight training? If so, you're not alone. Many people are unsure if they should add cardio to their workout routine or if weight training is enough. In this article, we will discuss the benefits of cardio after weight training and help you decide if it's right for you.

After weight training, you may experience fatigue or feel like you've completed a full workout. However, adding cardio can help improve your overall fitness level and help you reach your goals faster. Cardio after weight training can help increase your cardiovascular endurance, burn more calories, and improve your body composition.

If your goal is to lose weight or improve your overall fitness level, adding cardio after weight training can be beneficial. Cardio can help you burn more calories, which can lead to a calorie deficit and help you lose weight. Additionally, cardio can help improve your cardiovascular health and endurance, which can help you perform better during weight training sessions.

In conclusion, adding cardio after weight training can help you reach your fitness goals faster. Cardio can help you burn more calories, improve your body composition, and increase your cardiovascular endurance. However, it's important to listen to your body and make sure you're not overworking yourself. If you're feeling fatigued or experiencing muscle soreness, take a break and rest.

Benefits of Cardio After Weight Training

When I first started working out, I only focused on weight training. I thought that lifting weights was enough to reach my fitness goals. However, after talking to a personal trainer, I learned the benefits of adding cardio after weight training. Not only did it help me burn more calories, but it also helped me improve my overall fitness level.

Cardio after weight training can help you burn more calories and improve your body composition. When you do cardio after weight training, your body is already in a calorie-burning state. Adding cardio can help you burn even more calories and improve your overall body composition. Additionally, cardio can help improve your cardiovascular endurance, which can help you perform better during weight training sessions.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Training

When I first started adding cardio after weight training, I started with low-intensity exercises like walking on the treadmill or cycling. As my endurance improved, I increased the intensity and duration of my cardio sessions. It's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your cardio sessions.

Another way to incorporate cardio after weight training is by doing circuit training. This is where you alternate between weight training exercises and cardio exercises. For example, you could do a set of squats followed by a minute of jumping jacks. This can help keep your heart rate up and help you burn more calories.

My Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Training

After adding cardio after weight training, I noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness level. I was able to perform better during weight training sessions and felt more energized throughout the day. Additionally, I was able to lose weight and improve my body composition.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need to do cardio after every weight training session?

A: No, you don't need to do cardio after every weight training session. It's important to listen to your body and make sure you're not overworking yourself. If you're feeling fatigued or experiencing muscle soreness, take a break and rest.

Q: How long should I do cardio after weight training?

A: It's recommended to do cardio for at least 20-30 minutes after weight training. However, you can gradually increase the duration as your endurance improves.

Q: What are some good cardio exercises to do after weight training?

A: Some good cardio exercises to do after weight training include jogging, cycling, swimming, and using the elliptical machine.

Q: Can I do cardio before weight training?

A: Yes, you can do cardio before weight training. However, it's important to warm up properly and not overwork yourself before weight training.

Conclusion of Do You Have to Do Cardio After Weight Training

In conclusion, adding cardio after weight training can help you reach your fitness goals faster. It can help you burn more calories, improve your body composition, and increase your cardiovascular endurance. However, it's important to listen to your body and not overwork yourself. Start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your cardio sessions. With consistency and patience, you can achieve your fitness goals.
