The What Happens When You Do Cardio And Strength Training Gaining Muscle

Written by Daniel Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Incorporating both cardio and strength training into your fitness routine can have a plethora of benefits for your body and overall health. But have you ever wondered what happens when you do cardio and strength training? Let's dive into the details.

Many people think that cardio and strength training are two separate entities, and to some extent, they are. However, there are significant ways in which they intertwine and impact each other. For example, cardio can help build endurance and stamina, while strength training can help build muscle and increase metabolic rate. But what happens when you combine the two?

What happens when you do cardio and strength training?

The combination of cardio and strength training can lead to a variety of positive outcomes. Firstly, it can help you achieve your fitness goals more efficiently. When you combine cardio and strength training, you'll be working your entire body in different ways, which can help you burn fat and build muscle at the same time.

Secondly, combining these exercises can help improve your overall health. Cardio is known to be beneficial for the heart and lungs, while strength training can help improve bone density and reduce the risk of injury. By combining the two, you can achieve a well-rounded workout that benefits your entire body.

Lastly, combining cardio and strength training can help break up the monotony of your workout routine. Doing the same exercises repeatedly can become boring and less effective over time. By mixing up your routine with a combination of cardio and strength training, you'll keep your body guessing and your mind engaged.

My personal experience with cardio and strength training

Personally, I've been incorporating both cardio and strength training into my fitness routine for years, and I've noticed significant changes in my body and overall health. Not only have I lost weight and gained muscle, but I also feel more energized and focused throughout the day.

One of my favorite ways to combine cardio and strength training is through high-intensity interval training (HIIT). This type of workout involves short bursts of intense exercise, followed by brief periods of rest. HIIT workouts can be done with a variety of exercises, such as running, cycling, or weightlifting, making them a versatile and effective addition to any fitness routine.

The benefits of cardio and strength training for weight loss

When it comes to weight loss, combining cardio and strength training can be especially effective. Cardio exercises, such as running or cycling, can help burn calories and fat, while strength training can help build muscle and increase metabolism. By burning fat and building muscle simultaneously, you can achieve a leaner, more toned physique.

How to incorporate cardio and strength training into your routine

If you're new to combining cardio and strength training, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio and 30 minutes of strength training per session, with a rest day in between. As you become more comfortable with the exercises, you can increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

The importance of proper nutrition

Remember, proper nutrition is just as important as exercise when it comes to achieving your fitness goals. Be sure to fuel your body with nutrient-dense foods, such as whole grains, lean protein, fruits, and vegetables. Avoid processed foods and excess sugar, which can hinder your progress.

FAQs about cardio and strength training

Q: Is it better to do cardio or strength training first?
A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle, it's best to start with strength training, as cardio can deplete your energy levels. If you're looking to improve your endurance, start with cardio and end with strength training.

Q: How often should I do cardio and strength training?
A: Aim for at least three days per week, with a rest day in between. Listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?
A: Yes, you can. Just be sure to warm up properly and give yourself time to rest in between exercises.

Q: Will combining cardio and strength training make me bulky?
A: No, it won't. Building bulky muscles requires a specific type of training and diet. Combining cardio and strength training can help you achieve a lean, toned physique.

Conclusion of what happens when you do cardio and strength training

Combining cardio and strength training can have a variety of benefits for your body and overall health. From achieving your fitness goals more efficiently to improving your overall health, cardio and strength training can help you take your workouts to the next level. So, what are you waiting for? Start incorporating these exercises into your routine and see the results for yourself!
