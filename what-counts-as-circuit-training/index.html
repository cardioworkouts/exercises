What Counts As Circuit Training A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to switch up your workout routine and challenge your body? Circuit training might be just what you need. But what exactly counts as circuit training, and how do you get started? In this article, we'll answer those questions and more, so you can hit the gym with confidence.

Circuit training is a form of exercise that involves completing a series of different exercises in a specific order, with little to no rest in between. This type of workout is designed to keep your heart rate up and challenge your muscles in various ways. It's a great way to burn calories, build strength, and improve your overall fitness level.

So, what counts as circuit training? In general, a circuit should include at least three different exercises that work different muscle groups. You'll perform each exercise for a set amount of time or a certain number of reps, before moving on to the next exercise in the circuit. Once you've completed all the exercises in the circuit, you can take a short break before starting again from the beginning.

To make things even more challenging, you can also add weights or resistance bands to your circuit exercises. This will help you build muscle and increase your strength over time. Just be sure to start with lighter weights and focus on proper form to avoid injury.

My Experience with Circuit Training

When I first started circuit training, I was a bit intimidated. I wasn't sure if I was doing the exercises correctly, and I was worried about getting tired too quickly. But once I got started, I was hooked. I loved the variety of exercises and the challenge of pushing myself to complete each circuit.

One of my favorite circuit workouts includes squats, lunges, and push-ups. I start with 30 seconds of squats, then move on to 30 seconds of lunges, followed by 30 seconds of push-ups. After a short break, I repeat the circuit for a total of three sets. It's a great full-body workout that gets my heart rate up and leaves me feeling energized.

The Benefits of Circuit Training

One of the biggest benefits of circuit training is that it's incredibly efficient. Since you're working multiple muscle groups at once, you can get a full-body workout in a relatively short amount of time. Plus, since you're constantly moving from one exercise to the next, you'll burn more calories than you would with a traditional weightlifting routine.

Another benefit of circuit training is that it's highly customizable. You can create circuits that focus on specific muscle groups or target different areas of the body. Plus, you can adjust the intensity of each exercise to match your fitness level and goals.

Tips for Getting Started with Circuit Training

If you're new to circuit training, it's important to start slowly and focus on proper form. You don't want to rush through the exercises and risk injury. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

  • Choose exercises that work different muscle groups, such as squats, lunges, and push-ups
  • Start with lighter weights and focus on proper form
  • Set a timer or choose a number of reps for each exercise
  • Take short breaks between circuits to allow your body to recover
  • Gradually increase the intensity of your circuits over time

Common Circuit Training Mistakes to Avoid

While circuit training can be a great way to challenge your body and improve your fitness, there are a few common mistakes to avoid. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using weights that are too heavy, as this can lead to injury
  • Don't rush through the exercises - focus on proper form instead
  • Take breaks when you need them, but don't rest for too long
  • Be sure to warm up before starting your circuit, and cool down afterwards

FAQs About Circuit Training

1. How long should a circuit training workout last?

A: Circuit training workouts can vary in length depending on your goals and fitness level. A typical circuit might last anywhere from 20-45 minutes.

2. How often should I do circuit training?

A: It's generally recommended to do circuit training 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between each workout.

3. Can I do circuit training at home?

A: Absolutely! You don't need a gym to do circuit training - you can create your own circuits using bodyweight exercises or simple equipment like resistance bands.

4. Is circuit training good for weight loss?

A: Yes! Circuit training is a great way to burn calories and boost your metabolism, making it an effective tool for weight loss.

Conclusion

Now that you know what counts as circuit training, it's time to give it a try for yourself. Remember to start slowly, focus on proper form, and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts over time. With a little bit of practice, you'll be crushing your circuits in no time!
