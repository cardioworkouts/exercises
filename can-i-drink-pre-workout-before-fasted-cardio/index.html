Can I Drink Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Written by Robby Jul 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you an early bird who loves to hit the gym or go for a run before breakfast? Or are you someone who prefers to exercise on an empty stomach to burn more fat? If you fall into either of these categories, you may be wondering whether it's safe and effective to consume pre-workout supplements before fasted cardio. Here's what you need to know.

What is Fasted Cardio and Why Do People Do It?

Fasted cardio refers to any form of cardiovascular exercise done on an empty stomach, usually first thing in the morning. The idea behind it is that when you haven't eaten for several hours, your body has depleted its glycogen stores and must resort to burning fat for fuel instead. Proponents of fasted cardio claim that it helps them achieve a leaner physique and boosts their metabolism.

Can You Take Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio?

The short answer is yes, you can take pre-workout supplements before fasted cardio. However, there are a few caveats to keep in mind. First and foremost, make sure that the pre-workout supplement you're taking doesn't contain any calories or carbohydrates. Fasted cardio is all about keeping your insulin levels low, so consuming anything that raises them can sabotage your efforts.

Secondly, be aware that pre-workout supplements can cause stomach upset or nausea if taken on an empty stomach. This is especially true if you're sensitive to caffeine or other stimulants. If you do decide to take pre-workout before fasted cardio, start with a smaller dose than you would normally take and see how your body reacts.

Benefits and Risks of Drinking Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

The benefits of drinking pre workout before fasted cardio include increased energy, focus, and endurance, which can help you power through your workout even when you're feeling tired or hungry. Additionally, some pre-workout supplements contain ingredients that can help enhance fat burning, such as caffeine, green tea extract, and carnitine.

However, there are also risks associated with taking pre-workout before fasted cardio. If you're not careful, you could end up consuming too much caffeine or other stimulants, which can lead to jitteriness, heart palpitations, and other unpleasant side effects. Additionally, taking pre-workout supplements on an empty stomach can irritate your digestive system and cause nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.

Tips for Taking Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

If you've decided to take pre-workout supplements before fasted cardio, here are some tips to help you do it safely and effectively:

  • Choose a pre-workout supplement that's specifically formulated for fasted cardio or that doesn't contain any calories or carbohydrates.
  • Start with a smaller dose than you would normally take and see how your body reacts.
  • Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated.
  • Listen to your body and stop exercising if you feel lightheaded, dizzy, or nauseous.

Conclusion

In conclusion, drinking pre workout before fasted cardio is generally safe as long as you choose the right supplement and use it responsibly. Pre-workout supplements can provide a much-needed energy boost and help you burn more fat during your workout, but they can also have side effects if taken in excess. As with any supplement, it's important to read the label, follow the instructions, and listen to your body.

Question and Answer

Q. Is it better to do cardio before or after breakfast?

A. It depends on your goals and preferences. Doing cardio before breakfast can help you burn more fat, but it may also lead to muscle breakdown and fatigue. Doing cardio after breakfast can provide you with more energy and help you perform better, but it may also interfere with fat burning.

Q. What should I eat before fasted cardio?

A. If you're doing fasted cardio, you shouldn't eat anything before your workout. However, you may want to drink some water or a cup of black coffee to help you stay hydrated and alert.

Q. Can I drink BCAAs before fasted cardio?

A. Yes, you can drink BCAAs (branched-chain amino acids) before fasted cardio. BCAAs are a type of protein that can help preserve muscle mass and improve endurance during exercise.

Q. How long should I wait after taking pre-workout before doing fasted cardio?

A. It's recommended that you wait at least 30 minutes after taking pre-workout before doing fasted cardio. This will give your body time to digest the supplement and avoid any digestive discomfort during your workout.

Conclusion of Can I Drink Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

In conclusion, drinking pre workout before fasted cardio can be a great way to boost your energy and fat burning potential, but it's important to do it safely and responsibly. By choosing the right supplement, starting with a smaller dose, and staying hydrated, you can get the most out of your fasted cardio workout without risking any negative side effects.
