Can You Do Cardio While Strength Training

Written by Robby Sep 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who wants to stay in shape but is unsure of how to balance your strength training and cardio workouts? If so, you're not alone. Many people wonder if they can do cardio while strength training and how to properly balance both types of exercises.

The idea of strength training and cardio workouts can be daunting, especially if you're new to exercise. But with the right approach, you can easily incorporate both types of workouts into your routine for maximum health benefits.

So, can you do cardio while strength training? The answer is yes! In fact, incorporating cardio into your strength training routine can provide a variety of benefits, including increased endurance, improved heart health, and better overall fitness.

Let's dive deeper into the topic of can you do cardio while strength training and explore how to properly balance both types of exercises for optimal results.

Cardio and Strength Training: My Personal Experience

As a personal trainer, I often get asked if it's possible to do cardio while strength training. My answer is always yes! In fact, I encourage my clients to incorporate both types of workouts into their routines.

Personally, I have found that incorporating cardio into my strength training routine has helped me to maintain a healthy weight, increase my endurance, and improve my overall fitness level. I typically do 30 minutes of cardio before my strength training workout, such as running on the treadmill or cycling.

By doing cardio first, I warm up my muscles and get my heart rate up, which helps me to perform better during my strength training exercises. I also find that doing cardio before my strength training workout helps me to burn more calories overall.

Tips for Balancing Cardio and Strength Training

If you're looking to incorporate both cardio and strength training into your routine, here are a few tips to help you properly balance both types of exercises:

1. Plan your workouts in advance

Planning your workouts in advance can help you to properly balance your cardio and strength training exercises. Consider scheduling specific days for each type of workout or alternating between the two.

2. Warm up before strength training

Before you begin your strength training exercises, be sure to warm up your muscles with some light cardio. This can help you to perform better during your workout and reduce your risk of injury.

3. Don't overdo it

While it's important to challenge yourself during your workouts, it's also important not to overdo it. Incorporating too much cardio into your routine can hinder your strength training progress and vice versa. Find a balance that works for you and your fitness goals.

The Benefits of Cardio and Strength Training

Now that we've explored the topic of can you do cardio while strength training and how to properly balance both types of exercises, let's look at the benefits of incorporating both into your routine:

1. Increased endurance

Cardiovascular exercise, such as running or cycling, can help to increase your endurance, allowing you to perform better during your strength training exercises.

2. Improved heart health

Cardiovascular exercise can help to improve your heart health by strengthening your heart and reducing your risk of heart disease.

3. Better overall fitness

By incorporating both cardio and strength training into your routine, you can improve your overall fitness level, including your strength, endurance, and flexibility.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio while strength training?

A: It's recommended to do cardio at least three times per week and strength train at least two times per week for optimal health benefits.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It's recommended to do cardio before strength training to warm up your muscles and get your heart rate up.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and strength training on the same day. Just be sure to properly warm up before your strength training exercises.

Q: How long should my cardio and strength training workouts be?

A: Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio and 30 minutes of strength training per workout.

Conclusion of Can You Do Cardio While Strength Training

So, can you do cardio while strength training? The answer is yes! By incorporating both types of exercises into your routine, you can enjoy a variety of health benefits, including increased endurance, improved heart health, and better overall fitness. Just be sure to properly balance both types of workouts and listen to your body to avoid overdoing it. With the right approach, you can achieve your fitness goals and enjoy a healthier, more active lifestyle.
