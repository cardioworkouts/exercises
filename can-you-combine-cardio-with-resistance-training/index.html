Can You Combine Cardio With Resistance Training

Written by Robby Nov 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering whether you can combine cardio with resistance training? You're not alone. Many people want to know whether it's possible to do both types of exercises in one workout routine, and whether it's effective for achieving fitness goals. In this post, we'll explore this topic in-depth and provide you with all the information you need to know about combining cardio with resistance training.

The Pain Points of Combining Cardio with Resistance Training

One of the main pain points people have when it comes to combining cardio with resistance training is time. Many people struggle to find the time to do both types of exercises in one workout routine, and may feel overwhelmed by the thought of having to do two types of exercises in one session. Another pain point is the fear of overtraining or not seeing results from their workouts.

Answering the Target: Can You Combine Cardio with Resistance Training?

The answer is yes, you can combine cardio with resistance training. In fact, doing so can be an effective way to achieve your fitness goals. Combining these two types of exercises can help you burn fat, build muscle, improve your cardiovascular health, and boost your metabolism.

Summary of Main Points

Combining cardio with resistance training is possible and effective for achieving fitness goals. It can help you burn fat, build muscle, improve your cardiovascular health, and boost your metabolism. However, it's important to be mindful of your time and avoid overtraining.

Personal Experience: Cardio and Resistance Training

As a personal trainer, I always recommend my clients to combine cardio with resistance training. I believe that doing so is a great way to achieve a well-rounded fitness routine. Personally, I like to combine running on the treadmill with weightlifting exercises like squats, lunges, and deadlifts. I find that this combination helps me burn fat, build muscle, and improve my endurance.

The Benefits of Combining Cardio with Resistance Training

There are many benefits to combining cardio with resistance training. For one, it can help you burn more calories during your workout. This is because resistance training helps to build muscle, which in turn helps to increase your metabolism. Additionally, cardio exercises can help you burn fat, which can help you achieve a leaner physique. Finally, combining these two types of exercises can help you improve your overall fitness level and reduce your risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and diabetes.

How to Combine Cardio with Resistance Training

If you're interested in combining cardio with resistance training, there are a few ways you can do so. One option is to alternate between cardio and resistance training days. For example, you could do cardio on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and resistance training on Tuesday and Thursday. Another option is to do both types of exercises in one workout session. For example, you could do five minutes of cardio, followed by a set of weightlifting exercises, and then repeat the cycle for the duration of your workout.

Tips for Combining Cardio with Resistance Training

Here are a few tips for combining cardio with resistance training:

  1. Start with a warm-up: Before you begin your workout, it's important to warm up your muscles. This can help prevent injury and ensure that you're able to perform your exercises properly.
  2. Focus on form: When you're doing resistance training exercises, it's important to focus on your form. Make sure that you're using proper technique and that you're not sacrificing form for weight.
  3. Choose the right weights: When you're lifting weights, it's important to choose the right weights for your fitness level. Start with lighter weights and gradually increase the weight as you get stronger.
  4. Listen to your body: It's important to listen to your body and take breaks when you need them. If you're feeling fatigued or experiencing pain, take a break and rest.

Personal Experience: My Favorite Cardio and Resistance Training Combination

My favorite way to combine cardio with resistance training is to do a circuit workout. I like to start with a five-minute warm-up on the treadmill, followed by a set of squats, lunges, and deadlifts. Then, I'll do a minute of jumping jacks, followed by a set of bicep curls, tricep dips, and shoulder presses. I'll repeat this cycle for the duration of my workout, and finish with a five-minute cool-down on the treadmill.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio or resistance training first?

A: There is no right or wrong answer to this question. Some people prefer to do cardio first to warm up their muscles, while others prefer to do resistance training first to build strength. Ultimately, it's up to you to decide which order works best for your fitness goals.

Q: How often should I combine cardio with resistance training?

A: It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week, along with two days of resistance training per week. However, you can adjust this based on your fitness goals and schedule.

Q: Can combining cardio with resistance training lead to overtraining?

A: Yes, it is possible to overtrain if you're not careful. It's important to listen to your body and take breaks when you need them. Additionally, it's important to vary your workouts and not do the same exercises every day.

Q: Can I combine cardio with resistance training if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, you can combine cardio with resistance training if you're a beginner. However, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time.

Conclusion of Can You Combine Cardio with Resistance Training

Combining cardio with resistance training is a great way to achieve your fitness goals. It can help you burn fat, build muscle, improve your cardiovascular health, and boost your metabolism. However, it's important to be mindful of your time and avoid overtraining. By following these tips and listening to your body, you can create a well-rounded workout routine that includes both cardio and resistance training.
