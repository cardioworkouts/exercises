4 Week Beginner Bodybuilding Workout A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to get into bodybuilding but don't know where to start? Do you find yourself overwhelmed by the amount of information out there? You're not alone. Starting a new fitness routine can be challenging, but with the right guidance, you can make progress in just 4 weeks.

Many beginners struggle with finding the right workout routine that suits their needs. Some may feel intimidated by the gym, while others may not know what exercises to do or how to structure a workout. These pain points can be discouraging, but they're not insurmountable.

The target of this 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout is to introduce you to the basics of bodybuilding and help you build a foundation for future progress. By the end of the program, you should feel more confident in the gym and have a better understanding of how to approach your fitness goals.

In this article, we'll cover the essential elements of a 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout, including exercises, sets, reps, and rest periods. We'll also dive into proper form and technique, so you can perform each exercise safely and effectively.

Week 1-2: Building Strength

During the first two weeks of this program, the focus will be on building strength. You'll perform compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups and stimulate overall muscle growth. These exercises include squats, deadlifts, bench presses, and rows.

Personal Experience: During my first two weeks, I struggled with squatting and deadlifting due to poor form. However, with the help of a trainer, I was able to improve my technique and increase my weights.

For each exercise, you'll perform 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps with 2-3 minutes of rest in between. The weights should be challenging but manageable, so aim to increase them slightly each week. Don't forget to warm up before each workout to prevent injury.

Week 3-4: Building Muscle

During the last two weeks of the program, the focus will shift to building muscle. You'll perform isolation exercises that target specific muscle groups and promote hypertrophy. These exercises include bicep curls, tricep extensions, lateral raises, and calf raises.

Personal Experience: During my last two weeks, I noticed a significant increase in muscle size and definition. My arms and shoulders looked more defined, and I felt more confident in my appearance.

For each exercise, you'll perform 3-4 sets of 10-15 reps with 1-2 minutes of rest in between. Again, aim to increase the weights slightly each week, but be sure to maintain proper form and technique.

Proper Form and Technique

Proper form and technique are essential for preventing injury and maximizing muscle growth. When performing each exercise, focus on the following:

  • Keep your core engaged and your back straight
  • Use a full range of motion
  • Control the weight throughout the movement
  • Breathe in on the eccentric (lowering) phase and exhale on the concentric (lifting) phase

How to Progress Beyond the 4 Week Beginner Bodybuilding Workout

After completing this program, you may be wondering what's next. The key to continued progress is to change up your routine every 4-6 weeks to prevent plateaus and keep your muscles guessing. Some ways to do this include:

  • Incorporate new exercises
  • Change the number of sets and reps
  • Vary the rest periods
  • Increase the weights

Conclusion of 4 Week Beginner Bodybuilding Workout

The 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout is a great starting point for anyone looking to get into bodybuilding. By following this program, you'll build strength, gain muscle, and improve your overall fitness. Remember to focus on proper form and technique, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With dedication and consistency, you can achieve your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to lift heavy weights during the 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout?

A: While lifting heavy weights can be beneficial for building strength and muscle, it's not necessary for beginners. Focus on lifting weights that are challenging but manageable, and gradually increase them over time.

Q: Can I do cardio during the 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout?

A: Yes, you can incorporate cardio into your routine, but be sure to prioritize your weightlifting sessions. Aim for 2-3 weightlifting sessions per week and 1-2 cardio sessions per week.

Q: Do I need to follow a strict diet during the 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout?

A: While nutrition is an essential component of any fitness program, it's not necessary to follow a strict diet during the 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout. Focus on eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

Q: How long should I rest between sets during the 4 week beginner bodybuilding workout?

A: Rest periods should be between 1-3 minutes, depending on your goals and fitness level. If you're looking to build strength, longer rest periods may be necessary, while shorter rest periods can promote muscle endurance.
