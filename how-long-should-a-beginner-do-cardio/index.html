How Long Should A Beginner Do Cardio

Written by Robby Jun 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you new to exercising and wondering how long you should be doing cardio? You're not alone. Many beginners struggle with finding the right amount of time to dedicate to cardio workouts. In this blog post, we will explore the recommended length of time for beginners to do cardio and provide helpful tips to get started.

What Are the Pain Points Related to How Long Should a Beginner Do Cardio?

Beginners often feel overwhelmed when starting a new exercise routine. They may not know how much time to dedicate to cardio, which can lead to frustration and a lack of motivation. Additionally, beginners may not know how to properly perform cardio exercises or which ones to choose, which can cause discomfort or injury.

How Long Should a Beginner Do Cardio?

As a beginner, it is recommended to start with 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise, five days a week. This can include activities such as brisk walking, cycling, or swimming. Gradually increase the time and intensity of your workouts as your fitness level improves. Aim for a total of 150 minutes of cardio exercise per week.

It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. If you feel exhausted or experience pain during your workouts, take a break and rest. Remember to warm up and cool down before and after your workouts to prevent injury.

Summary of How Long Should a Beginner Do Cardio

As a beginner, it is recommended to start with 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise, five days a week. Gradually increase the time and intensity of your workouts as your fitness level improves. Aim for a total of 150 minutes of cardio exercise per week. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. Remember to warm up and cool down before and after your workouts to prevent injury.

How to Make Cardio Workouts Enjoyable as a Beginner

As a beginner, it's important to find an activity that you enjoy to make cardio workouts more enjoyable. If you don't like running, try cycling or swimming instead. You can also listen to music or podcasts during your workouts to keep you motivated. Find a workout buddy to keep you accountable and make your workouts more social.

How to Avoid Common Mistakes as a Beginner

One common mistake beginners make is pushing themselves too hard too soon. Remember to start with moderate-intensity workouts and gradually increase the time and intensity. Another mistake is not warming up or cooling down properly, which can lead to injury. Take the time to properly stretch and prepare your body before and after your workouts.

How to Choose the Right Cardio Exercises as a Beginner

As a beginner, it's important to choose exercises that you enjoy and that are appropriate for your fitness level. Walking, cycling, and swimming are all great options for beginners. You can also try group fitness classes or workout videos to add variety to your routine. Remember to start with moderate-intensity exercises and gradually increase the time and intensity.

How to Incorporate Cardio Workouts into Your Daily Routine as a Beginner

As a beginner, it can be challenging to find time for cardio workouts. Try incorporating exercise into your daily routine by walking or cycling to work or doing a quick workout during your lunch break. You can also try waking up earlier to fit in a morning workout or working out in the evening after dinner. Remember to choose a time that works best for you and your schedule.

Personal Experience

When I first started exercising, I found it challenging to find the right amount of time to dedicate to cardio workouts. I started with 30 minutes of brisk walking, five days a week, and gradually increased the time and intensity of my workouts. I found that listening to music during my workouts helped me stay motivated and make them more enjoyable. It took time, but I eventually built up to 150 minutes of cardio exercise per week and felt great.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do more than 30 minutes of cardio as a beginner?

A: It's recommended to start with 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio, five days a week, and gradually increase the time and intensity as your fitness level improves. If you feel comfortable doing more, you can gradually increase the time and intensity, but it's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard.

Q: Is it better to do cardio in the morning or evening?

A: The best time to do cardio is when it works best for your schedule. Some people prefer morning workouts to start their day off on the right foot, while others prefer evening workouts after work to relieve stress. Choose a time that works best for you and stick with it.

Q: What are some low-impact cardio options for beginners?

A: Walking, cycling, and swimming are all great low-impact cardio options for beginners. You can also try group fitness classes or workout videos that focus on low-impact exercises.

Q: How long does it take to see results from cardio workouts?

A: It can take several weeks to see results from cardio workouts. Be patient and consistent with your workouts, and remember that progress takes time.

Conclusion of How Long Should a Beginner Do Cardio

As a beginner, it's recommended to start with 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise, five days a week, and gradually increase the time and intensity as your fitness level improves. It's important to listen to your body, choose exercises that you enjoy, and make your workouts enjoyable. By following these tips, you can build a sustainable cardio routine and improve your overall health and fitness.
