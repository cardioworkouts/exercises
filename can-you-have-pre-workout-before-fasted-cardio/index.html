Can You Have Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Written by Eveline Sep 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who likes to do fasted cardio in the morning but also prefers to have a pre workout supplement beforehand? If so, you may be wondering if it's okay to have pre workout before fasted cardio. In this blog post, we will explore this topic and provide you with some insights and tips to help you make an informed decision.

Pain Points

When it comes to fasted cardio, many people believe that working out on an empty stomach can help burn more fat. On the other hand, pre workout supplements are designed to give you an energy boost and enhance your performance during exercise. However, combining these two practices can raise some concerns and confusion.

Can You Have Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio?

The short answer is yes, you can have pre workout before fasted cardio. However, it's important to choose the right type of pre workout supplement and use it in moderation. Some pre workouts contain high amounts of caffeine and other stimulants, which can increase your heart rate and blood pressure. If you're not used to these effects, they can make you feel jittery, anxious, or even sick.

Instead, look for pre workouts that are specifically formulated for fasted cardio or low-intensity exercise. These products usually contain less caffeine and more amino acids, electrolytes, and vitamins to help support your body's needs during fasted cardio. You can also try natural alternatives like black coffee, green tea, or beetroot juice.

Main Points

In summary, here are some main points to consider when it comes to having pre workout before fasted cardio:

  • Choose a pre workout supplement that's suitable for low-intensity exercise and contains moderate caffeine levels.
  • Avoid pre workouts that may cause adverse effects like palpitations, headaches, or insomnia.
  • Start with a small dose and gradually increase it to assess your tolerance and response.
  • Don't rely solely on pre workout to fuel your fasted cardio. Make sure to hydrate well and eat a balanced meal after your workout.

Personal Experience

As someone who regularly does fasted cardio in the morning, I've tried different types of pre workout supplements to see how they affect my performance and energy levels. Initially, I used to consume pre workouts with high caffeine content, but I soon realized that they made me feel too jittery and anxious.

Then, I switched to a pre workout that's specifically designed for fasted cardio and contains amino acids, electrolytes, and vitamins. I found that this type of pre workout gave me a more sustained and natural energy boost without any side effects. I also noticed that I could maintain a steady heart rate and pace during my cardio session.

Benefits of Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Having pre workout before fasted cardio can offer some benefits, such as:

  • Increased energy and focus
  • Improved endurance and performance
  • Better muscle recovery and growth
  • Reduced muscle soreness and fatigue

Things to Keep in Mind

Despite the potential benefits, there are some things to keep in mind when it comes to having pre workout before fasted cardio:

  • Pre workout supplements are not a substitute for a healthy diet, sleep, and hydration.
  • Pre workout supplements may interact with certain medications or health conditions, so consult your doctor before taking them.
  • Pre workout supplements may not work the same way for everyone, so listen to your body and adjust accordingly.
  • Pre workout supplements may not be necessary for everyone, especially if you're doing low-intensity or short-duration cardio.

How to Choose the Right Pre Workout for Fasted Cardio

If you're interested in having pre workout before fasted cardio, here are some tips to help you choose the right product:

  • Read the label carefully and look for ingredients that support fasted cardio, such as BCAAs, L-carnitine, beta-alanine, or nitric oxide boosters.
  • Avoid products that contain excessive caffeine or other stimulants, artificial sweeteners, or fillers.
  • Look for products that have positive reviews and ratings from other users who have similar goals and preferences.
  • Try a sample or a small size first before committing to a full bottle to see how your body reacts to the product.

Question and Answer

Q: Can pre workout supplements break my fast?

A: It depends on the type and amount of pre workout supplement you consume. Some pre workouts contain calories or carbohydrates that can break your fast, while others are calorie-free and won't affect your fasting state. Read the label and choose a product that's suitable for your fasting goals.

Q: How long should I wait after taking pre workout before starting my fasted cardio?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 30 minutes to 1 hour after taking pre workout before starting your cardio session. This allows the pre workout to be absorbed and metabolized by your body and prevents any digestive discomfort or cramping during exercise.

Q: Can I have pre workout and coffee before fasted cardio?

A: It's generally not recommended to combine pre workout and coffee before fasted cardio, as both contain caffeine and may cause overstimulation or dehydration. Instead, choose one or the other and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Q: Can I have a protein shake before fasted cardio?

A: It depends on your goals and preferences. If you're doing low-intensity cardio for a short duration, having a protein shake before may not be necessary. However, if you're doing high-intensity cardio or resistance training, having a protein shake before can help prevent muscle breakdown and promote muscle growth. Just make sure to choose a protein powder that's low in sugar and fat and doesn't contain any added fillers or artificial ingredients.

Conclusion of Can You Have Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Having pre workout before fasted cardio is possible and can offer some benefits, but it requires some caution and attention to detail. By choosing the right type of pre workout supplement, using it in moderation, and listening to your body's response, you can optimize your fasted cardio performance and achieve your fitness goals.
