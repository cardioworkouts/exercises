What Are The Different Types Of Cardio Exercise

Written by Aprilia Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to incorporate cardio exercise into your fitness routine, but not sure where to start? Do terms like "HIIT" and "steady-state" leave you feeling confused? Look no further! This post will break down the different types of cardio exercise and help you find the right one for your fitness goals.

Cardio exercise, also known as cardiovascular exercise, is any type of exercise that raises your heart rate and improves the health of your cardiovascular system. This type of exercise can range from low-impact options like walking or cycling to high-intensity workouts like sprinting or jumping jacks.

There are several different types of cardio exercise, including:

Steady-State Cardio

Steady-state cardio, also known as continuous cardio, is any type of exercise that is performed at a consistent, steady pace for an extended period of time. Examples of steady-state cardio include jogging, cycling, swimming, and rowing. This type of cardio exercise is great for building endurance and improving cardiovascular health.

Personally, I love going for a long run outdoors. It's a great way to clear my mind and get some fresh air while also improving my heart health.

Interval Training

Interval training, also known as HIIT (high-intensity interval training), is a type of cardio exercise that alternates short periods of high-intensity exercise with longer periods of lower-intensity exercise or rest. This type of training can be done with any type of cardio exercise, including running, cycling, or even bodyweight exercises like burpees or jump squats. HIIT is great for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health in a shorter amount of time compared to steady-state cardio.

When I want to push myself and challenge my body, I like to incorporate HIIT workouts into my routine. It's tough, but the feeling of accomplishment afterwards is worth it!

Circuit Training

Circuit training is a type of cardio exercise that combines strength training exercises with short bursts of cardio exercise. This type of training is great for building muscular endurance and improving cardiovascular health. Examples of circuit training workouts include full-body workouts that incorporate exercises like squats, lunges, push-ups, and jumping jacks.

Personally, I love incorporating circuit training into my routine when I want to mix things up and challenge my body in new ways.

Dance Cardio

Dance cardio is a type of cardio exercise that combines dance moves with cardio exercise. Examples of dance cardio workouts include Zumba, hip-hop dance classes, and barre workouts. This type of cardio exercise is great for improving cardiovascular health while also having fun and learning new dance moves.

While I'm not the best dancer, I love taking dance cardio classes with friends. It's a great way to get a workout in while also having fun and letting loose!

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio exercise?

A: It's recommended to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio exercise per week. This can be broken down into shorter sessions throughout the week.

Q: Is cardio exercise the only way to improve cardiovascular health?

A: While cardio exercise is a great way to improve cardiovascular health, it's not the only way. Eating a healthy diet, managing stress, and getting enough sleep are also important factors in maintaining a healthy heart.

Q: Can I do cardio exercise if I have joint pain?

A: Yes, low-impact options like cycling, swimming, or using an elliptical machine can be great options for those with joint pain. It's important to listen to your body and modify exercises as needed to avoid exacerbating any pain or discomfort.

Q: How can I make cardio exercise more enjoyable?

A: Try incorporating different types of cardio exercise into your routine to keep things interesting. You can also listen to music or podcasts, take classes with friends, or exercise outdoors to make your workouts more enjoyable.

Conclusion of What Are the Different Types of Cardio Exercise

Incorporating cardio exercise into your fitness routine can have numerous benefits for your health, including improving cardiovascular health and burning calories. Whether you prefer steady-state cardio, HIIT, circuit training, or dance cardio, there's a type of cardio exercise out there for everyone. Experiment with different types of cardio exercise to find what works best for you and your fitness goals.
