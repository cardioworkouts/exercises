Top Hiit Workout Songs To Get You Pumped Up

Written by Luffy Aug 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of going to the gym and feeling unmotivated during your workout? Do you struggle to find the energy to push through your HIIT routine? Look no further than these top HIIT workout songs to get you pumped up and ready to crush your fitness goals.

We all know the feeling of dragging ourselves to the gym after a long day at work or school. The last thing we want is to be stuck on a treadmill or doing burpees without any motivation. That's where music comes in - it can be the key to unlocking a more intense and enjoyable workout.

So, what exactly are the top HIIT workout songs? The answer is subjective and varies from person to person. However, there are a few key characteristics that make a great workout song: a fast tempo, a strong beat, and lyrics that inspire and motivate.

In this article, we'll be sharing some of the best HIIT workout songs that have been tried and tested by fitness enthusiasts. We'll also explore why music is so beneficial for your workout and how you can build your own ultimate workout playlist.

1. "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott ft. Ciara & Fatman Scoop

I remember the first time I heard "Lose Control" during a spinning class. The beat dropped, and suddenly I was pedaling faster than I ever had before. This song is perfect for HIIT because of its high energy and fast tempo. Plus, the lyrics are all about letting loose and having a good time - exactly what you need to power through a tough workout.

2. "Can't Hold Us" by Macklemore & Ryan Lewis ft. Ray Dalton

"Can't Hold Us" is a classic workout song that never fails to get me hyped up. The beat is strong and consistent, which makes it easy to synchronize your movements. The lyrics are also incredibly motivational - "This is the moment, tonight is the night" - perfect for pushing yourself to the limit.

3. "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

No workout playlist is complete without "Eye of the Tiger." This iconic song from Rocky III is the ultimate pump-up anthem. The driving beat and powerful lyrics will make you feel like you can conquer anything. Plus, it's a great reminder that every workout is a fight worth winning.

4. "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars

If you're looking for a song that will make you want to dance and move, "Uptown Funk" is the one for you. The upbeat tempo and catchy lyrics will have you forgetting that you're even working out. Plus, it's a great way to add some fun to your routine.

Why is music so beneficial for your workout?

Music has been shown to have a number of benefits when it comes to exercise. For one, it can distract you from feelings of fatigue or discomfort, allowing you to push through your workout for longer. It can also help regulate your breathing and heart rate, leading to a more efficient workout.

Additionally, music can improve your mood and reduce stress levels, which can ultimately lead to a more enjoyable workout experience. It's no wonder that so many fitness enthusiasts swear by their workout playlists.

How can you build your own ultimate workout playlist?

Building a workout playlist is a personal process, and it's important to choose songs that you enjoy and that motivate you. That being said, there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose songs with a tempo of 120-140 beats per minute, as this has been shown to be the most effective for exercise.
  • Look for songs with a strong beat and consistent rhythm.
  • Consider adding songs with motivational lyrics that inspire you to work harder.
  • Don't be afraid to mix it up - include a variety of genres and artists to keep things interesting.

Question and Answer

Q: How many songs should I include in my workout playlist?

A: It's up to you! Some people prefer shorter playlists with only a few songs, while others like longer playlists to keep them motivated throughout their entire workout. Aim for at least 30 minutes of music to align with your workout time.

Q: Can listening to music during a workout improve performance?

A: Yes! Studies have shown that listening to music during exercise can improve endurance, increase speed and strength, and even reduce the perception of effort.

Q: Should I choose songs that I like or songs that are specifically designed for exercise?

A: It's important to choose songs that you enjoy and that motivate you, but songs that are specifically designed for exercise can also be effective. Look for workout playlists on streaming services like Spotify or Apple Music to get some ideas.

Q: Can listening to music during a workout be dangerous?

A: It's important to be aware of your surroundings and not to listen to music at dangerously high volumes, as this can lead to hearing damage. Additionally, if you're doing a workout that requires your full attention, like running outside, it's best to skip the music altogether.

Conclusion of Top HIIT Workout Songs

Music is a powerful tool when it comes to working out, and the right playlist can make all the difference. Whether you prefer fast-paced hip-hop or classic rock anthems, there's a perfect HIIT workout song out there for you. So, put on your headphones, turn up the volume, and let the music motivate you to push yourself to new heights.
