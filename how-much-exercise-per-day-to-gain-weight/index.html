How Much Exercise Per Day To Gain Weight

Written by Luffy Jun 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you struggling to gain weight despite your efforts to exercise regularly? Do you feel like you're not making progress no matter how much time you spend at the gym? If so, you're not alone. Many people have difficulty gaining weight, and figuring out how much exercise per day to gain weight can be a daunting task. In this post, we'll explore the topic of how much exercise per day to gain weight and provide you with some helpful tips to achieve your weight gain goals.

When it comes to gaining weight, exercise is just one piece of the puzzle. Nutrition is also a critical factor in achieving your desired weight gain. Eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats is essential. However, when it comes to exercise, you want to focus on activities that build muscle and increase your overall strength.

The amount of exercise you need to gain weight depends on your individual goals and fitness level. In general, it's recommended to engage in strength training activities at least three to four times per week. Each session should last between 30 and 60 minutes and focus on working different muscle groups. Cardiovascular exercise can also be helpful for improving your overall fitness level, but it should be done in moderation.

To gain weight, you need to consume more calories than you burn. Therefore, it's essential to pay attention to your diet and make sure you're eating enough to support your exercise routine. Incorporating protein-rich foods like lean meats, eggs, and dairy products can help build muscle and aid in weight gain.

Personal Experience:

When I first started my weight gain journey, I was only focusing on cardio exercises like running and cycling. However, I soon realized that I needed to incorporate strength training to see the results I wanted. I started lifting weights three times a week, focusing on different muscle groups each session. I also made sure to eat more protein and healthy fats, which helped me build muscle and increase my overall weight.

Tips for Beginners:

If you're just starting your weight gain journey, it's essential to take it slow and listen to your body. Here are some tips to get started:

  1. Start with bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, and lunges before moving on to weights.
  2. Focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups, such as bench presses, rows, and deadlifts.
  3. Gradually increase the weight and intensity of your exercises as you become stronger.
  4. Make sure to rest and allow your muscles time to recover between exercise sessions.

How to Track Your Progress:

Tracking your progress is essential to stay motivated and monitor your results. Here are some ways to track your progress:

  • Take measurements of your body, including your weight, body fat percentage, and muscle mass.
  • Keep a workout journal to track your exercises, weight, and reps.
  • Take progress photos to see how your body is changing over time.

How to Stay Motivated:

Staying motivated can be challenging when you're not seeing the results you want. Here are some tips to stay motivated:

  • Set realistic goals and celebrate your progress along the way.
  • Find a workout buddy or join a fitness community for support and accountability.
  • Switch up your exercises and try new things to keep your workouts interesting.

Personal Experience:

When I hit a plateau in my weight gain journey, I started to feel discouraged. However, I joined a fitness community and found a workout buddy who helped me stay accountable and motivated. I also started trying new exercises and found that mixing things up helped me break through my plateau and continue making progress.

Question and Answer:

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: Rest periods between sets can vary depending on your goals and fitness level. However, a good rule of thumb is to rest between 30 and 90 seconds between sets. This allows your muscles time to recover and helps you maintain your intensity throughout your workout.

Q: Should I do cardio or strength training to gain weight?

A: Both cardio and strength training can be helpful for gaining weight. Cardiovascular exercise can help improve your overall fitness level and aid in weight gain, but it should be done in moderation. Strength training is essential for building muscle and increasing your overall strength, which can lead to weight gain.

Q: How many calories should I eat to gain weight?

A: The number of calories you need to eat to gain weight will depend on your individual goals and fitness level. It's recommended to consume an extra 500 to 1000 calories per day to gain one to two pounds per week. However, it's essential to pay attention to your body and adjust your calorie intake as needed.

Q: Can I gain weight without exercise?

A: While exercise is an essential part of gaining weight, it's possible to gain weight without exercise by focusing on your diet. Eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats can help you gain weight, but it may not lead to the same muscle gain and strength improvements as exercise.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to how much exercise per day to gain weight. It depends on your individual goals and fitness level. However, incorporating strength training activities at least three to four times per week, focusing on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups, and eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats can help you achieve your weight gain goals. Remember to track your progress, stay motivated, and listen to your body as you work towards your desired weight.
