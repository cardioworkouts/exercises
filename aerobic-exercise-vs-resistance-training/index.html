Aerobic Exercise Vs Resistance Training Which One Is Better For You

Written by Frank Sep 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering which type of exercise is best for you? Do you want to know the difference between aerobic exercise and resistance training? If so, you've come to the right place.

Aerobic exercise and resistance training are two of the most popular types of exercise. They both have their benefits and drawbacks, and it's important to understand the differences between them to choose the right one for your needs.

In this article, we'll discuss the target of aerobic exercise vs resistance training, the benefits and drawbacks of each, and which one might be best for your fitness goals.

The Target of Aerobic Exercise vs Resistance Training

Let's start with the basics. Aerobic exercise, also known as cardio, is any activity that increases your heart rate and breathing rate. Examples of aerobic exercise include running, swimming, and cycling.

Resistance training, on the other hand, is any activity that involves using weights or resistance to build strength and muscle. Examples of resistance training include weight lifting, bodyweight exercises, and resistance bands.

Aerobic exercise is great for improving cardiovascular health, burning calories, and increasing endurance. It can also reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and obesity. Resistance training is great for building strength, increasing muscle mass, and improving bone density. It can also help to prevent injury and improve balance and posture.

My Personal Experience with Aerobic Exercise vs Resistance Training

Personally, I have found that a combination of both aerobic exercise and resistance training works best for me. I enjoy running and cycling for cardio, but I also like to include weight lifting and bodyweight exercises in my routine to build strength and muscle.

One of the benefits of combining both types of exercise is that it can help to prevent boredom and keep your workouts interesting. It also allows you to target different areas of your body and achieve a more well-rounded level of fitness.

The Benefits and Drawbacks of Aerobic Exercise vs Resistance Training

While both types of exercise have their benefits, they also have their drawbacks. Here are some of the pros and cons of each:

Aerobic Exercise

Benefits:

  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Burns calories
  • Increases endurance
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases

Drawbacks:

  • Can be repetitive and boring
  • May not be effective for building strength and muscle
  • May not be suitable for people with certain health conditions

Resistance Training

Benefits:

  • Builds strength and muscle
  • Improves bone density
  • Can help to prevent injury
  • Improves balance and posture

Drawbacks:

  • Can be intimidating for beginners
  • May not be effective for improving cardiovascular health
  • May be more time-consuming than aerobic exercise

How to Incorporate Aerobic Exercise and Resistance Training Into Your Routine

If you're interested in incorporating both aerobic exercise and resistance training into your routine, here are some tips to get started:

  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts
  • Choose activities that you enjoy and that fit your fitness level
  • Consider working with a personal trainer to develop a personalized workout plan
  • Be consistent with your workouts and aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise per day

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do aerobic exercise and resistance training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both types of exercise on the same day. Just make sure to give your body enough time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: Do I need to use weights for resistance training?

A: No, you don't need to use weights for resistance training. Bodyweight exercises such as push-ups, squats, and lunges can be just as effective.

Q: How often should I do aerobic exercise and resistance training?

A: It's recommended to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week, as well as at least two days of resistance training per week.

Q: Is one type of exercise better than the other for weight loss?

A: Both types of exercise can be effective for weight loss. However, aerobic exercise may be more effective for burning calories in the short term, while resistance training may be more effective for building muscle and increasing metabolism in the long term.

Conclusion of Aerobic Exercise vs Resistance Training

In conclusion, both aerobic exercise and resistance training have their benefits and drawbacks. The best type of exercise for you depends on your fitness goals, preferences, and health status. By incorporating a combination of both types of exercise into your routine, you can achieve a well-rounded level of fitness and improve your overall health and well-being.
