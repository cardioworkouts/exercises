Should I Do Cardio Before Or After Workout For Weight Loss

Written by Aprilia Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you trying to lose weight but unsure about whether to do cardio before or after your workout? It can be confusing to know the best way to structure your exercise routine for optimal weight loss. This post will help you understand the benefits of doing cardio before or after your workout and give you guidance on which one to choose.

The decision to do cardio before or after your workout depends on your goals and personal preferences. If weight loss is your primary goal, then doing cardio after your workout is generally the most effective. However, if you are training for a specific event, such as a marathon, doing cardio before your workout may be more beneficial for you.

Doing cardio before your workout can help to warm up your muscles and prepare your body for the rest of your workout. It can also help to increase your heart rate and improve your endurance. However, doing cardio before your workout can also leave you feeling fatigued and may reduce the intensity of your weight training. This is why it's important to choose the right type of cardio before your workout, such as low-intensity steady-state cardio (LISS).

On the other hand, doing cardio after your workout can help to burn more calories and fat, as your body will already be in a fat-burning state after your weight training. It can also help to improve your recovery and reduce muscle soreness. However, doing cardio after your workout can also be challenging as you may already be tired and less motivated to push yourself. This is why it's important to choose the right type of cardio after your workout, such as high-intensity interval training (HIIT).

My Personal Experience with Cardio Before and After Workout

As someone who is trying to lose weight, I have found that doing cardio after my workout works best for me. I usually do a 30-minute weight training session followed by a 20-minute HIIT cardio session. This helps me to burn more calories and fat and also improves my recovery time. I also find that I am more motivated to push myself during my cardio session after my weight training, as I feel energized and ready to go.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Before and After Workout

Doing cardio before or after your workout has its benefits. Here are some of the benefits:

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Your Workout

1. Helps to warm up your muscles and prepare your body for the rest of your workout.

2. Increases your heart rate and improves your endurance.

3. Can help to burn fat and calories when done correctly.

How to Do Cardio Before Your Workout

1. Choose the right type of cardio, such as LISS.

2. Keep it low-intensity and steady-state to avoid fatigue.

3. Do it for 10-20 minutes before your weight training.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After Your Workout

1. Burns more calories and fat as your body is already in a fat-burning state.

2. Improves your recovery time and reduces muscle soreness.

3. Increases your metabolism and helps to keep your heart rate elevated after your workout.

How to Do Cardio After Your Workout

1. Choose the right type of cardio, such as HIIT.

2. Keep it high-intensity and interval-based to maximize fat burning.

3. Do it for 20-30 minutes after your weight training.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training for weight loss?

A: If weight loss is your primary goal, then doing cardio after your weight training is generally more effective.

Q: Can doing cardio before your workout reduce the intensity of your weight training?

A: Yes, doing cardio before your workout can leave you feeling fatigued and may reduce the intensity of your weight training.

Q: Can doing cardio after your workout improve your recovery time?

A: Yes, doing cardio after your workout can help to improve your recovery time and reduce muscle soreness.

Q: What type of cardio should I do after my workout?

A: High-intensity interval training (HIIT) is the best type of cardio to do after your workout for optimal fat burning.

Conclusion

Whether you choose to do cardio before or after your workout depends on your goals and personal preferences. If weight loss is your primary goal, then doing cardio after your workout is generally the most effective. However, if you are training for a specific event, such as a marathon, doing cardio before your workout may be more beneficial for you. Remember to choose the right type of cardio for your goals and fitness level and to always listen to your body to avoid overtraining and injury.
