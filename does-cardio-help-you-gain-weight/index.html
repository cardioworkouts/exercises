Does Cardio Help You Gain Weight

Written by Aprilia Nov 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you trying to gain weight but wondering if your cardio workouts are hindering your progress? It's a common concern for those looking to bulk up, but the answer may surprise you. Let's take a closer look at whether cardio helps or hinders weight gain.

The fear of losing muscle mass and not gaining weight is a pain point for many fitness enthusiasts. They worry that cardio will burn off the calories needed to build muscle and increase weight. However, the truth is that cardio can actually aid in weight gain if done correctly.

The simple answer to whether cardio helps you gain weight is yes, but it depends on how you approach it. Cardiovascular exercise is known to increase metabolism and burn calories, but that doesn't mean it can't be used to your advantage. By adjusting your cardio routine and diet, you can use cardio to help you gain weight instead of hindering it.

To summarize, cardio can help you gain weight if you approach it correctly. By increasing your calorie intake and adjusting your cardio routine, you can use cardio to your advantage in your weight gain journey.

Increasing Calorie Intake

Personally, when I was trying to gain weight, I found it challenging to eat enough calories to see any significant progress. I would often feel full and have no appetite for more food. However, by incorporating cardio into my routine, I found that it increased my hunger and allowed me to consume more calories throughout the day.

Cardio can also help you burn off excess fat, allowing you to consume more calories without gaining unwanted weight. By consuming more calories than your body burns, you will gain weight. This is where cardio can help, as it increases the number of calories you can eat without gaining fat.

Cardio and Resistance Training

Cardio should not replace resistance training in your fitness routine. Resistance training is crucial for building muscle mass and should be the foundation of your weight gain journey. Cardio can be used as a supplement to resistance training, helping to increase your metabolism and aid in recovery.

Personally, I found that incorporating cardio after my resistance training sessions helped me recover faster and allowed me to increase the intensity of my workouts. This, in turn, led to more significant gains in muscle mass and weight.

The Importance of Rest

Rest is crucial for muscle recovery and growth. Overtraining can lead to muscle loss and hinder your weight gain progress. When incorporating cardio into your routine, it's essential to give your body enough rest to recover properly.

Personally, I found that incorporating one or two days of rest into my routine helped me recover faster and see more significant gains in muscle mass and weight.

Adjusting Your Cardio Routine

If you're worried about cardio hindering your weight gain progress, adjusting your cardio routine can help. Instead of long, steady-state cardio sessions, try incorporating high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or short, intense cardio sessions.

HIIT has been shown to increase metabolism and burn more calories than steady-state cardio. Short, intense cardio sessions can also increase your metabolism and burn calories without hindering muscle growth and weight gain.

Question and Answer

Q: Does cardio burn off muscle mass?

A: It depends on the intensity and duration of your cardio sessions. Long, steady-state cardio sessions can burn off muscle mass, but short, intense cardio sessions can aid in muscle recovery and growth.

Q: Can cardio help you gain weight?

A: Yes, by increasing your calorie intake and adjusting your cardio routine, you can use cardio to help you gain weight.

Q: Should cardio replace resistance training in my routine?

A: No, resistance training should be the foundation of your weight gain journey. Cardio can be used as a supplement to resistance training.

Q: Is rest important when incorporating cardio into my routine?

A: Yes, rest is crucial for muscle recovery and growth. Incorporate one or two days of rest into your routine to allow your body to recover properly.

Conclusion of Does Cardio Help You Gain Weight

Cardio can help you gain weight if done correctly. By increasing your calorie intake, incorporating cardio after resistance training, adjusting your cardio routine, and allowing for proper rest, you can use cardio to your advantage in your weight gain journey. Don't let the fear of losing muscle mass and hindering weight gain hold you back from incorporating cardio into your routine.
