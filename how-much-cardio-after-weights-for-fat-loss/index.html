How Much Cardio After Weights For Fat Loss A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you hitting the gym regularly, lifting weights, but still struggling to shed those stubborn pounds? You're not alone. Many people face the same problem, and the solution might lie in cardio after weights. In this post, we'll discuss how much cardio after weights for fat loss and related keywords to help you reach your fitness goals.

It's frustrating to put in the effort at the gym and not see the desired results. Sometimes, even after following a strict diet and lifting weights, the weight loss doesn't happen as expected. This is where cardio after weights come in. By incorporating cardio into your workout routine, you can increase your calorie burn and accelerate fat loss.

How Much Cardio After Weights for Fat Loss?

The amount of cardio you need after weights depends on factors such as your fitness level, weight loss goals, and time available. As a rule of thumb, aim to do at least 150 minutes of moderate cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio each week.

For beginners, start with low-intensity cardio for 20-30 minutes after your strength training. Gradually increase the time and intensity as your fitness level improves. You can also mix up your cardio routine by doing high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or plyometrics.

Remember, the key is to find a balance between weightlifting and cardio. Doing too much cardio can lead to muscle loss, which is not ideal if you're trying to build muscle and lose fat.

Personal Experience:

When I first started lifting weights, I was hesitant to do any cardio. However, after a few weeks, I realized that I wasn't losing as much weight as I wanted to. That's when I started incorporating 20 minutes of cardio after my strength training sessions. Gradually, I increased the time and intensity, and I noticed a significant difference in my weight loss progress.

The Benefits of Cardio After Weights

Cardio after weights has several benefits, including:

  • Increased calorie burn
  • Accelerated fat loss
  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Reduced stress and anxiety

Increased Calorie Burn

Adding cardio to your workout routine increases your calorie burn, helping you achieve a calorie deficit, which is essential for weight loss. Cardio after weights is especially effective because your body is already warmed up, making it easier to get your heart rate up.

Accelerated Fat Loss

By doing cardio after weights, you can accelerate fat loss because your body is already in fat-burning mode. Cardio helps to deplete glycogen stores, forcing your body to burn fat for energy.

Personal Experience:

After incorporating cardio into my workout routine, I noticed that my body fat percentage was decreasing faster than before. I also had more energy during the day, and my overall fitness level improved.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weights

If you're new to cardio, start with low-intensity workouts such as walking, cycling, or swimming. You can gradually increase the intensity by doing HIIT or plyometrics. Remember to stretch before and after your workout to prevent injury.

Here's an example of a workout routine that incorporates cardio after weights:

  1. Warm-up: 10 minutes of walking or light jogging
  2. Strength training: 30-45 minutes of weightlifting
  3. Cardio: 20-30 minutes of low-intensity cardio
  4. Cool-down: 10 minutes of stretching

Question and Answer:

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weights?

A: It's best to do weights before cardio because weightlifting requires more energy and focus. Doing cardio after weights can also help you burn more fat because your body is already in fat-burning mode.

Q: How many times a week should I do cardio after weights?

A: Aim to do cardio after weights at least three times a week. Gradually increase the time and intensity as your fitness level improves.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on rest days, but make sure to listen to your body and not overdo it. Low-intensity cardio such as walking or cycling can help to improve recovery and reduce muscle soreness.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after weights?

A: Wait at least 10-15 minutes after your strength training session to do cardio. This will give your body time to recover and prevent injury.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio After Weights for Fat Loss:

Incorporating cardio after weights is an effective way to accelerate fat loss and improve your overall fitness level. Remember, the key is to find a balance between weightlifting and cardio, and gradually increase the time and intensity as your fitness level improves. By following the tips and advice in this post, you'll be on your way to achieving your weight loss goals.
