Can I Do Cardio After My Workout

Written by Aprilia Jul 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who likes to hit the weights at the gym but also wants to get in some cardio? Perhaps you're wondering if it's okay to do cardio after your workout. This is a common question among fitness enthusiasts, and the answer may surprise you.

Many people believe that doing cardio after a workout can negatively impact their gains, but this isn't necessarily true. In fact, doing cardio after weight training can actually be beneficial for your overall fitness goals. However, there are a few things to keep in mind.

Firstly, it's important to understand the target of doing cardio after a workout. The purpose of cardio is to get your heart rate up and burn calories. This can help you to lose weight, improve your cardiovascular health, and even boost your mood. Meanwhile, weight training is focused on building muscle and strength. By combining the two, you can work towards a well-rounded fitness routine.

In summary, doing cardio after a workout can be an effective way to improve your overall fitness. However, it's important to keep a few things in mind. Here are some tips to help you make the most out of your post-workout cardio:

Target of Doing Cardio After A Workout

When I first started working out, I was focused solely on weight training. However, as I learned more about fitness, I realized the importance of incorporating cardio. I started doing a 15-20 minute cardio session after my weight training, and I noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness. Not only did I feel stronger, but I also had more endurance and stamina during my workouts.

When you do cardio after a workout, your body is already warmed up and ready to go. This means that you're able to jump right into your cardio routine without having to spend extra time warming up. Additionally, doing cardio after weight training can help to improve your recovery time. This is because cardio helps to increase blood flow to your muscles, which can help to reduce soreness and speed up the recovery process.

Tips for Doing Cardio After A Workout

While doing cardio after a workout can be beneficial, it's important to do it right. Here are some tips to help you make the most out of your post-workout cardio:

1. Keep it short and sweet

When doing cardio after weight training, it's best to keep your session short and sweet. Aim for 15-20 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio, such as jogging or cycling. This will help to get your heart rate up and burn calories without putting too much stress on your body.

2. Listen to your body

It's important to listen to your body when doing cardio after a workout. If you're feeling fatigued or sore, it's okay to skip the cardio session or to do a shorter session than usual. Additionally, if you're new to working out, it's important to start slow and gradually work your way up to longer cardio sessions.

3. Fuel your body properly

When doing cardio after weight training, it's important to fuel your body properly. This means eating a healthy meal or snack before your workout and drinking plenty of water during and after your workout. Additionally, it's important to replenish your body with protein and carbohydrates after your workout to help with muscle recovery.

4. Mix it up

Finally, it's important to mix up your cardio routine to prevent boredom and to challenge your body. Try different types of cardio, such as swimming or dancing, to keep things interesting.

Question and Answer

Q: Can doing too much cardio after weight training be detrimental to muscle growth?

A: If you're doing excessive amounts of cardio after weight training, it can potentially be detrimental to muscle growth. However, if you keep your cardio sessions short and sweet, it shouldn't have a significant impact on your gains.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's generally recommended to do weight training before cardio. This is because weight training requires more energy and focus, and doing it first can help to prevent injury and ensure that you have enough energy for your workout.

Q: Can doing cardio after weight training help with weight loss?

A: Yes, doing cardio after weight training can be an effective way to burn calories and aid in weight loss. However, it's important to maintain a healthy diet and to combine cardio with weight training for optimal results.

Q: How often should I do cardio after weight training?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and your current fitness level. If you're new to working out, start with one or two cardio sessions per week and gradually work your way up. If you're more experienced, you can do cardio after every weight training session.

Conclusion of Can I Do Cardio After My Workout

Overall, doing cardio after a workout can be a great way to improve your overall fitness. By combining weight training and cardio, you can work towards a well-rounded fitness routine that helps you to lose weight, build muscle, and improve your cardiovascular health. Just remember to keep your cardio sessions short and sweet, listen to your body, fuel your body properly, and mix up your routine to prevent boredom.
