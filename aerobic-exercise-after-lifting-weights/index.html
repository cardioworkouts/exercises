Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly and loves to lift weights? If yes, then you might be aware of the importance of recovery after weight lifting. One of the best ways to recover is to indulge in aerobic exercise after lifting weights. In this blog post, we will discuss the benefits, target, and how to perform aerobic exercise after lifting weights in detail.

What are the Pain Points Related to Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights?

Weight lifting is an intense exercise that puts a lot of stress on your muscles. After a weightlifting session, your muscles need time to recover and rebuild. However, sitting idle can make your muscles stiff and sore. This is where aerobic exercise comes into play. Many people avoid aerobic exercise after lifting weights because it can be tiring and challenging. However, it is crucial to indulge in aerobic exercise to improve your overall fitness level and get the most out of your weight lifting session.

What is the Target of Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights?

The primary goal of aerobic exercise after lifting weights is to improve your cardiovascular health and burn excess calories. It helps you recover faster by increasing blood flow and oxygen to your muscles. Aerobic exercise also helps reduce muscle soreness and stiffness, making you feel better after a weightlifting session.

Another target of aerobic exercise after lifting weights is to help you maintain a healthy weight. Weightlifting alone cannot help you lose weight. Aerobic exercise helps you burn calories and lose weight while improving your cardiovascular health.

Benefits of Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights

Aerobic exercise after lifting weights has numerous benefits. Some of these benefits include:

  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Burns excess calories
  • Reduces muscle soreness and stiffness
  • Increases blood flow and oxygen to muscles
  • Helps you maintain a healthy weight
  • Improves overall fitness level

My Personal Experience with Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights

As a fitness enthusiast, I always make sure to include aerobic exercise after my weightlifting session. Initially, it was challenging to indulge in aerobic exercise after weightlifting because I was exhausted. However, I gradually increased the intensity and duration of my aerobic exercise, which helped me improve my fitness level and recover faster. Nowadays, I prefer to do a 30-minute cardio session after weightlifting, and it has helped me feel better and improve my overall fitness level.

How to Perform Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights?

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to perform aerobic exercise after lifting weights:

  1. Begin with a 5-minute warm-up session to get your muscles ready for exercise.
  2. Choose an aerobic exercise that you enjoy, such as running, cycling, or swimming.
  3. Start with a low intensity and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your exercise.
  4. Monitor your heart rate to ensure that you are working out in the target heart rate zone.
  5. Finish with a 5-minute cool-down session to bring your heart rate back to normal.

Tips for Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights

Here are some tips that can help you get the most out of your aerobic exercise after weightlifting:

  • Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout.
  • Wear comfortable and breathable clothing.
  • Choose an exercise that you enjoy, so you are more likely to stick to your routine.
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workout.
  • Monitor your heart rate to ensure that you are working out in the target heart rate zone.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are some common mistakes that you should avoid while performing aerobic exercise after lifting weights:

  • Pushing yourself too hard can lead to injuries and muscle soreness.
  • Skipping the warm-up and cool-down sessions can increase the risk of injury.
  • Not drinking enough water can lead to dehydration and fatigue.
  • Choosing an exercise that you don't enjoy can lead to boredom and lack of motivation.

FAQs About Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights

Q. How long should I perform aerobic exercise after lifting weights?

A. It is recommended to perform aerobic exercise for at least 20-30 minutes after lifting weights.

Q. Can I perform aerobic exercise before lifting weights?

A. It is not recommended to perform aerobic exercise before lifting weights. This is because aerobic exercise can tire your muscles, making it difficult to lift weights with proper form.

Q. What is the best aerobic exercise to perform after lifting weights?

A. The best aerobic exercise to perform after lifting weights is the one that you enjoy the most. Running, cycling, swimming, and rowing are some popular options.

Q. How often should I perform aerobic exercise after lifting weights?

A. It is recommended to perform aerobic exercise after lifting weights at least 3-4 times a week.

Conclusion of Aerobic Exercise After Lifting Weights

Aerobic exercise after lifting weights is an excellent way to improve your cardiovascular health, burn excess calories, and recover faster. It is essential to choose an exercise that you enjoy and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workout. By following the tips mentioned in this blog post, you can get the most out of your aerobic exercise after lifting weights and improve your overall fitness level.
