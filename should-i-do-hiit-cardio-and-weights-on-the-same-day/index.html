Should I Do Hiit Cardio And Weights On The Same Day

Written by Oliver Nov 02, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’re someone who loves to workout, you’ve probably wondered if it’s a good idea to do HIIT cardio and weights on the same day. It’s a common question that many fitness enthusiasts ask themselves, and the answer isn’t always clear. In this blog post, we’ll explore the benefits and drawbacks of combining HIIT cardio and weights in the same workout, and help you decide whether or not it’s the right approach for you.

When it comes to working out, everyone has their own unique goals and challenges. Some people want to lose weight, while others want to build muscle. Some people have more time to dedicate to their workouts, while others have busy schedules and need to maximize their time. The decision to do HIIT cardio and weights on the same day largely depends on your personal goals and preferences.

If your goal is to burn fat and build muscle simultaneously, combining HIIT cardio and weights in the same workout can be an effective approach. HIIT cardio is a high-intensity workout that burns calories and gets your heart rate up, while weightlifting builds muscle and boosts your metabolism. Combining the two can help you burn fat and build muscle at the same time, which can lead to faster results.

However, if your goal is to build muscle and gain strength, doing HIIT cardio and weights on the same day might not be the best approach. This is because HIIT cardio can be taxing on your muscles and nervous system, which can make it harder to lift heavy weights and build muscle. If you’re trying to build muscle, it’s important to focus on weightlifting and give your muscles time to recover between workouts.

Personal Experience with Doing HIIT Cardio and Weights on the Same Day

As a fitness enthusiast, I’ve tried both approaches of doing HIIT cardio and weights on the same day and separating them into different workouts. I’ve found that when my goal is to burn fat and build muscle, combining the two in the same workout is effective. However, when my goal is to build muscle and gain strength, I prefer to separate them into different workouts to ensure that I’m lifting heavy weights with proper form and technique.

The Benefits of Doing HIIT Cardio and Weights on the Same Day

One of the main benefits of doing HIIT cardio and weights on the same day is that it saves time. If you have a busy schedule and can only dedicate a certain amount of time to your workouts, combining the two can be an efficient way to get the most out of your workout. Additionally, combining HIIT cardio and weights can help you burn more calories and build muscle faster, leading to faster results.

The Drawbacks of Doing HIIT Cardio and Weights on the Same Day

While there are benefits to combining HIIT cardio and weights in the same workout, there are also drawbacks to consider. Doing both in the same workout can be physically demanding and taxing on your body, especially if you’re not in good shape. Additionally, doing HIIT cardio before weightlifting can fatigue your muscles and make it harder to lift heavy weights with proper form and technique.

How to Incorporate HIIT Cardio and Weights into Your Workout

If you decide to incorporate HIIT cardio and weights into your workout, it’s important to do it safely and effectively. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start with a warm-up: Before you begin your workout, do a 5-10 minute warm-up to get your heart rate up and prepare your muscles for exercise.
  • Alternate between HIIT and weightlifting: To avoid overtaxing your muscles, alternate between HIIT cardio and weightlifting exercises throughout your workout.
  • Focus on proper form and technique: When weightlifting, focus on proper form and technique to avoid injury and get the most out of your workout.
  • Listen to your body: If you feel fatigued or overworked, take a break and rest before continuing your workout.

Conclusion of Should I Do HIIT Cardio and Weights on the Same Day

In conclusion, whether or not you should do HIIT cardio and weights on the same day largely depends on your personal goals and preferences. If your goal is to burn fat and build muscle simultaneously, combining the two can be an effective approach. However, if your goal is to build muscle and gain strength, separating them into different workouts might be a better approach. Ultimately, it’s important to listen to your body and do what works best for you.
