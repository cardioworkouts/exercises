Discover The Best Physical Therapy Exercise Apps For Your Recovery

Written by Aprilia Jul 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you in need of physical therapy but struggling to find the time or motivation to attend appointments? Do you want to supplement your in-person sessions with helpful exercises you can do at home? Look no further than the world of physical therapy exercise apps.

Physical therapy exercises can be daunting, tedious, and even painful. However, with the right guidance and tools, they can also be incredibly beneficial for your recovery. The best physical therapy exercise apps can provide you with a personalized plan, track your progress, and motivate you to stay on track towards healing.

Target of Physical Therapy Exercise Apps

The target of physical therapy exercise apps is to provide users with a convenient and accessible way to perform their prescribed exercises at home. These apps can help to keep users accountable, motivated, and on track towards their recovery goals. Here are some of the best physical therapy exercise apps on the market:

1. PhysiApp

PhysiApp is a popular physical therapy exercise app that allows users to access their prescribed exercises via video demonstration. Users can track their progress, communicate with their physical therapist, and receive reminders to complete their exercises. I have personally used this app and found it to be incredibly user-friendly and helpful for staying on track with my physical therapy routine.

2. Pelvic Health PT

Pelvic Health PT is an app specifically designed for those undergoing pelvic floor physical therapy. It offers a variety of exercises and educational resources to help users better understand and manage their pelvic health. This app can be incredibly helpful for those who are uncomfortable discussing these issues in person with their physical therapist.

3. Kaia Health

Kaia Health is a comprehensive digital health platform that offers a range of programs, including physical therapy exercises for back pain, knee pain, and more. It uses motion tracking technology to provide users with real-time feedback on their form and progress. This app can be a great option for those who want a more holistic approach to their physical therapy routine.

The Benefits of Using Physical Therapy Exercise Apps

There are numerous benefits to using physical therapy exercise apps. Firstly, they offer a convenient and accessible way to perform your exercises at home, without having to schedule additional in-person appointments. They can also provide you with personalized workouts, track your progress, and offer real-time feedback on your form.

How to Choose the Right Physical Therapy Exercise App for You

The right physical therapy exercise app for you will depend on your specific needs and goals. Consider what type of physical therapy you are undergoing, what features are most important to you (such as video demonstrations, progress tracking, or personalized workouts), and what your budget is. Don't be afraid to try out a few different apps until you find the one that works best for you.

4. Sworkit

Sworkit is a popular fitness app that also offers a range of physical therapy exercises. It allows users to choose from a variety of workouts, including those for strength, cardio, and flexibility. Sworkit also offers a personalized plan based on your fitness goals and abilities, making it a great option for those who want to supplement their physical therapy routine with additional exercises.

Question and Answer Section:

Q: Can physical therapy exercise apps replace in-person sessions with a physical therapist?

A: No, physical therapy exercise apps should not replace in-person sessions with a physical therapist. These apps can be used as a supplement to your in-person sessions, but they cannot provide the same level of personalized care and attention that a physical therapist can.

Q: Are physical therapy exercise apps suitable for all types of physical therapy?

A: No, not all physical therapy exercise apps are suitable for all types of physical therapy. Some apps are designed for specific types of physical therapy, such as pelvic floor therapy or back pain therapy. It is important to consult with your physical therapist before using any physical therapy exercise app to ensure that it is right for you.

Q: Are physical therapy exercise apps covered by insurance?

A: It depends on your insurance provider and plan. Some insurance plans may cover the cost of physical therapy exercise apps, while others may not. It is important to check with your insurance provider to see what is covered.

Q: Can physical therapy exercise apps be used by beginners?

A: Yes, physical therapy exercise apps can be used by beginners. Many apps offer a range of exercises for different skill levels, and some even offer personalized plans based on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of Best Physical Therapy Exercise Apps

Overall, physical therapy exercise apps can be a helpful tool for those undergoing physical therapy. They offer a convenient and accessible way to perform your exercises at home, track your progress, and stay motivated towards your recovery goals. Consider trying out one of the apps listed above or consulting with your physical therapist to find the app that works best for you.
