Best Free Exercise Apps For Seniors

Written by Luffy Oct 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a senior looking for an easy and convenient way to stay active and healthy? As we age, it can become more challenging to stay motivated to exercise, especially without the guidance of a personal trainer. Luckily, there are numerous free exercise apps available to seniors that can help them stay fit and healthy without the need for expensive gym memberships or personal trainers.

As we age, it becomes essential to maintain an active lifestyle to prevent various health conditions. However, seniors often face the challenge of limited mobility, flexibility, and strength, making it difficult to perform traditional workouts. Best free exercise apps for seniors are designed to provide low-impact exercises that are beneficial for seniors' health and well-being.

The best free exercise apps for seniors include SilverSneakers Go, FitOn, MyFitnessPal, and Daily Burn. SilverSneakers Go is a fantastic app that offers video workouts for seniors. FitOn is another app that provides a wide range of workouts, including yoga, strength training, and cardio. MyFitnessPal is an excellent app for tracking your daily calorie intake, and Daily Burn offers live streaming workouts that seniors can do at home.

In summary, seniors can benefit from using free exercise apps to maintain an active and healthy lifestyle. The best free exercise apps for seniors include SilverSneakers Go, FitOn, MyFitnessPal, and Daily Burn.

Question and Answer

Q: Are free exercise apps safe for seniors?

A: Yes, free exercise apps are safe for seniors. However, it is always recommended to consult with a doctor before starting any new exercise program.

Q: Can seniors perform high-impact exercises?

A: Seniors should avoid high-impact exercises as they can put excess strain on joints and muscles. Instead, they should focus on low-impact exercises that are beneficial for their health and well-being.

Q: Can seniors perform yoga?

A: Yes, seniors can perform yoga. Yoga is an excellent low-impact exercise that is beneficial for seniors' health and well-being.

Q: How often should seniors exercise?

A: Seniors should aim to exercise for at least 30 minutes a day, five days a week. However, it is always recommended to consult with a doctor before starting any new exercise program.
