Best High Intensity Cardio Workouts To Burn Fat And Stay Fit

Written by Eveline Oct 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of the same old cardio routine that doesn't give you the results you crave? Do you want to burn fat and stay fit but don't know where to start? Look no further than high intensity cardio workouts!

Many people struggle with finding the best high intensity cardio workouts that fit their lifestyle and fitness level. It can be overwhelming to sift through all the information available online and in magazines. Additionally, many people are intimidated by the idea of high intensity workouts and worry they won't be able to keep up.

But fear not! In this post, we will explore the best high intensity cardio workouts and explain how they can help you achieve your fitness goals. We will break down each workout and provide tips for beginners as well as those looking to take their workouts to the next level.

Overall, the goal of this post is to simplify the process of finding the best high intensity cardio workouts and provide you with the tools you need to succeed.

The Tabata Method

The Tabata Method is a popular high intensity workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by even shorter periods of rest. This workout can be done with almost any exercise, from running to burpees to jump squats.

Personally, I love doing Tabata sprints on the treadmill. I start with a 5-minute warm-up at a moderate pace, then alternate between 20 seconds of all-out sprinting and 10 seconds of rest for a total of 8 rounds. This workout takes me less than 20 minutes and leaves me feeling energized and accomplished.

The key to the Tabata Method is to go all-out during the 20 seconds of work. This means pushing yourself to your limit and really challenging your body. It's not easy, but the results are worth it!

HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training)

HIIT is another popular high intensity cardio workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest or active recovery. This workout can be done with almost any exercise, including running, cycling, and bodyweight exercises.

One of my favorite HIIT workouts is a 30-second sprint followed by 30 seconds of rest for a total of 10 rounds. I usually do this on a stationary bike, but it can be done with any cardio exercise. This workout takes me less than 15 minutes and leaves me feeling exhausted (in a good way!)

The key to HIIT is to push yourself during the work periods and use the rest periods to recover. This allows you to keep your heart rate elevated and burn more calories in less time.

Circuit Training

Circuit training is a high intensity workout that involves moving quickly between different exercises with little to no rest in between. This workout can be done with almost any exercise, from bodyweight exercises to weight lifting to cardio exercises.

One of my favorite circuit workouts is a full-body circuit that includes exercises like squats, push-ups, lunges, and jumping jacks. I do each exercise for 30 seconds with no rest in between, then rest for 1 minute before repeating the circuit again. This workout takes me about 30 minutes and leaves me feeling strong and energized.

The key to circuit training is to keep moving and challenge your body with each exercise. By moving quickly between exercises, you keep your heart rate elevated and burn more calories in less time.

Sprinting

Sprinting is a high intensity cardio workout that involves running at your maximum speed for short distances. This workout can be done on a track, treadmill, or even outside.

Personally, I love doing hill sprints. I find a steep hill and sprint up it as fast as I can, then walk back down and repeat for a total of 5-10 rounds. This workout takes me less than 20 minutes and leaves me feeling invigorated and accomplished.

The key to sprinting is to go all-out during each sprint and use the recovery time to catch your breath. This allows you to push yourself to your limit and burn more calories in less time.

Question and Answer

Q: Can high intensity cardio workouts be done at home?

A: Absolutely! Many high intensity workouts require little to no equipment and can be done in the comfort of your own home. Examples include bodyweight exercises, jump rope, and running up stairs.

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio workouts?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals, but most people can benefit from doing high intensity cardio workouts 2-4 times per week. Be sure to give your body time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: Can high intensity cardio workouts help me lose weight?

A: Yes! High intensity cardio workouts are great for burning calories and boosting your metabolism. When done consistently, they can help you lose weight and keep it off.

Q: What should I eat before and after a high intensity cardio workout?

A: It's important to fuel your body with the right foods before and after a high intensity workout. Before your workout, aim for a combination of carbs and protein, such as a banana with almond butter. After your workout, focus on replenishing your glycogen stores with carbs and repairing your muscles with protein, such as a protein shake with fruit.

Conclusion of Best High Intensity Cardio Workouts

High intensity cardio workouts are a great way to burn fat, boost your metabolism, and stay fit. By incorporating workouts like the Tabata Method, HIIT, circuit training, and sprinting into your routine, you can challenge your body and achieve your fitness goals.

Remember to listen to your body and start slowly if you're new to high intensity workouts. With consistency and dedication, you can see amazing results in no time!
