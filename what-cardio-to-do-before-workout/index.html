What Cardio To Do Before Workout A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 28, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you wondering what cardio to do before workout? Do you feel confused about which cardio exercises are best to get your heart rate up before hitting the weights? If you want to know the ideal cardio routine before your workout, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll explore the best cardio exercises to help you maximize your fitness goals.

When it comes to cardio before workout, it can be challenging to determine what exercises to do. This is because there are many options available, and it's essential to choose the ones that will best prepare your body for your workout. If you don't do the right cardio, you could end up feeling tired, sluggish, and unmotivated during your workout, which can negatively impact your progress.

The ideal cardio to do before a workout is low to moderate intensity exercises that will warm up your muscles and increase your heart rate gradually. This will help you get your body moving and improve blood flow, which will enhance your overall workout performance. Some of the best cardio exercises to do before a workout include brisk walking, cycling, jumping jacks, and dynamic stretching.

In summary, the best cardio to do before workout is low to moderate intensity exercises that will help warm up your muscles and increase your heart rate gradually. This will prepare your body for your workout and improve your overall performance. Now, let's take a closer look at some of the best cardio exercises to do before a workout.

Brisk Walking

Brisk walking is an excellent cardio exercise to do before your workout. It's low impact, easy to do, and can be done anywhere. Brisk walking helps to warm up your lower body and increases your heart rate gradually, making it an ideal choice before a leg or lower body workout. You can walk outside or use a treadmill, and aim for a pace that gets your heart rate up but still allows you to hold a conversation.

Cycling

Cycling is another great cardio exercise to do before a workout. It's low impact and can help warm up your lower body while also increasing your heart rate. You can use a stationary bike or cycle outdoors, and aim for a moderate pace that gets your heart rate up but still allows you to maintain control.

Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a simple yet effective cardio exercise that can get your heart rate up quickly. They help to warm up your entire body and can be done anywhere. Start by standing with your feet together, then jump and land with your feet shoulder-width apart while raising your arms above your head. Jump back to the starting position and repeat for several reps.

Dynamic Stretching

Dynamic stretching is a form of stretching that involves controlled movements to warm up your muscles and increase your heart rate. Some examples of dynamic stretching include leg swings, arm circles, and walking lunges. Dynamic stretching can help prepare your muscles for your workout while also providing cardiovascular benefits.

How Long Should You Do Cardio Before a Workout?

The amount of cardio you should do before a workout depends on several factors, including your fitness level, the type of workout you're doing, and your goals. As a general rule, aim for at least 5-10 minutes of cardio before your workout to warm up your body and get your heart rate up. If you're doing a high-intensity workout, you may want to do more cardio to prepare your body adequately.

Conclusion of What Cardio to Do Before Workout

Now that you know what cardio to do before workout, you can create a cardio routine that will help you reach your fitness goals. Remember, the best cardio exercises to do before a workout are low to moderate intensity exercises that will warm up your muscles and increase your heart rate gradually. By incorporating these exercises into your routine, you can improve your overall workout performance and achieve the results you desire.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio after my workout?

A: Yes, you can do cardio after your workout. However, it's essential to take into account your fitness goals and the type of workout you're doing. If you're doing a high-intensity workout, you may be too tired to do cardio afterward. It's also important to remember that doing too much cardio can interfere with muscle growth and recovery.

Q: How often should I do cardio before a workout?

A: The frequency of cardio before a workout depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, aim to do cardio before every workout to warm up your body and get your heart rate up. However, if you're doing a low-intensity workout, you may not need to do as much cardio before your workout.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weights?

A: It's generally recommended to do cardio before weights. This is because cardio can help warm up your muscles and prepare your body for your workout. However, if your goal is to focus on weightlifting, you may want to do your weightlifting first and then do cardio after your workout.

Q: How long should I rest between cardio and weights?

A: The rest time between cardio and weights depends on the type of workout you're doing and your fitness level. As a general rule, aim for at least 5-10 minutes of rest between cardio and weights to allow your body to recover and prepare for your workout.
