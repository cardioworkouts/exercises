What To Eat Before A Workout In The Morning To Build Muscle

Written by Robby Jul 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to build muscle and wondering what to eat before a workout in the morning? The right pre-workout meal can give you the energy and nutrients you need to maximize your gains. In this post, we'll explore the best foods to eat before a morning workout to help you build muscle.

When it comes to building muscle, nutrition is just as important as exercise. Eating the right foods before a workout can help fuel your body and provide the nutrients your muscles need to grow. However, figuring out what to eat before a workout in the morning can be challenging, especially if you're short on time or not sure what foods will give you the best results.

The goal of eating before a workout is to provide your body with the energy and nutrients it needs to perform at its best. This means consuming a meal that is high in protein and carbohydrates, as well as healthy fats and fiber. Ideally, you should aim to eat your pre-workout meal about 30 minutes to an hour before your workout.

My Personal Experience

As someone who loves to work out in the morning, I've experimented with different pre-workout meals over the years. Some of my favorite foods to eat before a morning workout include:

  • Whole grain toast with almond butter and banana
  • Greek yogurt with berries and granola
  • Oatmeal with protein powder and fruit
  • Egg white omelette with veggies and avocado

These meals provide a good balance of protein, carbs, and healthy fats, and give me the energy I need to power through my workout.

The Best Foods to Eat Before a Morning Workout

Now that we've covered the importance of eating before a workout and some examples of pre-workout meals, let's dive into the best foods to eat before a morning workout:

Protein

Protein is essential for muscle growth and repair, making it an important nutrient to consume before a workout. Aim to eat about 20-30 grams of protein before your workout. Good sources of protein include:

  • Eggs
  • Chicken or turkey breast
  • Salmon
  • Low-fat Greek yogurt
  • Protein powder

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates provide the energy your muscles need to power through a workout. Aim to eat about 30-50 grams of carbohydrates before your workout. Good sources of carbohydrates include:

  • Whole grain bread or crackers
  • Oatmeal
  • Fruit (banana, apple, berries, etc.)
  • Sweet potato

Hydration

Drinking enough water before a workout is just as important as eating the right foods. Aim to drink about 16-20 ounces of water about an hour before your workout. If you're doing a particularly intense workout, you may also want to consider a sports drink or electrolyte-infused water to help replenish lost fluids and minerals.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I work out on an empty stomach?

A: While it's possible to work out on an empty stomach, it's not recommended. Without fuel, your body won't have the energy it needs to perform at its best. Eating a small pre-workout meal can help improve your performance and prevent muscle breakdown.

Q: How long before a workout should I eat?

A: Aim to eat your pre-workout meal about 30 minutes to an hour before your workout. This will give your body enough time to digest the food and convert it into energy.

Q: What if I don't have time to eat before my workout?

A: If you're short on time, try to eat a small snack that is high in carbs and protein, such as a banana or a protein shake. This will give you some quick energy to power through your workout.

Q: Can I eat anything I want before a workout?

A: While you can technically eat anything you want before a workout, it's important to choose foods that will provide your body with the energy and nutrients it needs to perform at its best. Stick to foods that are high in protein and carbs, and avoid foods that are high in sugar or fat.

Conclusion of What to Eat Before a Workout in the Morning to Build Muscle

When it comes to building muscle, what you eat before a workout is just as important as the workout itself. By consuming a meal that is high in protein and carbohydrates, you can fuel your body and provide the nutrients your muscles need to grow. Remember to drink plenty of water and aim to eat your pre-workout meal about 30 minutes to an hour before your workout. With the right nutrition and exercise, you can achieve your muscle-building goals and feel your best.
