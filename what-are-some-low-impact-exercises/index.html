Low Impact Exercises A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for ways to exercise without putting too much strain on your body? Do you want to stay fit and healthy without experiencing any pain? If yes, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will discuss what are some low impact exercises that are perfect for beginners and those who want to take it easy on their joints.

As we age or recover from injuries, our bodies tend to become more delicate and prone to pain. High impact exercises like running or jumping can cause pain in the joints and muscles, making it hard to stay active. This is where low impact exercises come in – they are designed to be gentle on your body while still providing all the benefits of physical activity.

What are some low impact exercises? Here are some examples:

Walking

Walking is one of the easiest and most effective low impact exercises. It requires no equipment and can be done anywhere, anytime. Walking helps improve cardiovascular health, strengthens bones, and tones muscles.

Personally, I enjoy walking in the park early in the morning. It helps me clear my mind and jumpstarts my day. I usually walk for 30 minutes to an hour, depending on my schedule. I also make sure to wear comfortable shoes and bring a water bottle with me.

Swimming

Swimming is another excellent low impact exercise that works the whole body. It is especially beneficial for those with joint problems as it takes the pressure off your joints. Swimming helps improve cardiovascular health, builds endurance, and tones muscles.

I used to swim competitively in high school, but now I just swim for leisure. I usually go to the community pool on weekends and swim for an hour or so. It's a great way to cool off during hot summer days.

Yoga

Yoga is a low impact exercise that focuses on flexibility, balance, and strength. It is also a great way to reduce stress and improve mental health. Yoga can be done at home or in a studio, and all you need is a yoga mat.

Personally, I practice yoga every morning before work. I follow a 30-minute yoga routine that includes stretching and breathing exercises. It helps me feel more energized and focused throughout the day.

Cycling

Cycling is a low impact exercise that is perfect for those who want to work on their lower body. It helps improve cardiovascular health, strengthens leg muscles, and burns calories. Cycling can be done outdoors or indoors on a stationary bike.

I recently bought a bicycle and have been cycling around my neighborhood every day. It's a great way to explore the area and get some fresh air.

Question and Answer

Q: Can low impact exercises help with weight loss?

A: Yes, low impact exercises can help with weight loss as long as you do them consistently and pair them with a healthy diet.

Q: Are low impact exercises suitable for seniors?

A: Yes, low impact exercises are perfect for seniors as they are gentle on the joints and can help improve mobility.

Q: Do I need special equipment for low impact exercises?

A: No, most low impact exercises require little to no equipment. However, if you want to try cycling, you will need a bike.

Q: How often should I do low impact exercises?

A: It is recommended to do low impact exercises at least three times a week for 30 minutes each session.

Conclusion of what are some low impact exercises

Low impact exercises are an excellent way to stay active and healthy without causing pain or discomfort. Walking, swimming, yoga, and cycling are just a few examples of what are some low impact exercises that you can try. Remember to start slowly and listen to your body. With consistency and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals while keeping your body safe and healthy.
