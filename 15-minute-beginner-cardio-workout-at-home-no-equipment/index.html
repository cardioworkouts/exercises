15 Minute Beginner Cardio Workout At Home No Equipment

Written by Robby Aug 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a quick and easy way to get your heart pumping and burn some calories? Do you want to start a cardio routine but don't have any equipment or want to leave your home? Look no further than a 15 minute beginner cardio workout that requires no equipment and can be done in the comfort of your own home.

The thought of starting a new workout routine can be daunting, especially if you are a beginner. You may be worried about not having the proper equipment, not knowing where to start, or feeling intimidated by more advanced workouts. But with a 15 minute beginner cardio workout that requires no equipment, you can take the first step towards a healthier lifestyle without any of those worries.

The target of a 15 minute beginner cardio workout is to get your heart rate up and improve your cardiovascular health. It is a great way to burn calories, increase endurance, and improve overall fitness. This workout is designed for beginners, so no prior experience is necessary. You can modify the workout to your fitness level and gradually increase the intensity as you progress.

In summary, a 15 minute beginner cardio workout that requires no equipment is a great way for beginners to start a cardio routine from the comfort of their own home. It aims to improve cardiovascular health, burn calories, increase endurance, and improve overall fitness. You can modify the workout to your fitness level and gradually increase the intensity as you progress.

Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a classic cardio exercise that can get your heart rate up and burn calories. To do jumping jacks, stand with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Jump up and spread your feet apart while raising your arms above your head. Jump again and bring your feet back together while lowering your arms to your sides. Repeat for the desired amount of time.

Personally, I like to start my 15 minute beginner cardio workout with jumping jacks because they are a great warm-up exercise and get my heart pumping. I usually do jumping jacks for 30 seconds to a minute, and then move on to the next exercise.

High Knees

High knees are another great cardio exercise that can be done without any equipment. To do high knees, stand with your feet hip-width apart and your arms at your sides. Lift one knee up towards your chest while hopping on the other foot. Lower the lifted leg and repeat with the other leg. Continue alternating legs for the desired amount of time.

I like to do high knees for 30 seconds to a minute after jumping jacks. They are a great way to increase your heart rate and work your leg muscles at the same time.

Burpees

Burpees are a more advanced cardio exercise that can be modified for beginners. To do a burpee, start in a standing position. Drop down into a squat and place your hands on the ground. Jump your feet back into a plank position, and then jump them back to your hands. Stand up and jump into the air. To modify for beginners, you can skip the plank position and simply step your feet back and forth instead of jumping.

I like to do burpees for 30 seconds to a minute after high knees. They are a challenging exercise that work multiple muscle groups and get your heart rate up.

Mountain Climbers

Mountain climbers are a great cardio exercise that work your core and upper body as well. To do mountain climbers, start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart. Bring one knee up towards your chest, and then switch legs quickly. Continue alternating legs for the desired amount of time.

I like to finish my 15 minute beginner cardio workout with mountain climbers because they are a challenging exercise that work multiple muscle groups.
