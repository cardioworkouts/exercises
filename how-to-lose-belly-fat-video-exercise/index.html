How to Lose Belly Fat Video Exercise: Plank Variations

When it comes to losing belly fat, planks are one of the best exercises you can do. They work your entire core, including your abs, lower back, and obliques. Plus, they can be modified to fit your fitness level.

Personally, I struggled with belly fat for years before discovering the power of planks. After incorporating them into my workout routine, I saw a significant improvement in my midsection. Here's how to do them:

1. Forearm Plank

Start on your hands and knees. Lower your forearms to the ground, keeping your elbows directly under your shoulders. Step your feet back, one at a time, until your body forms a straight line from your head to your heels. Hold this position for 30 seconds to start, working your way up to 60 seconds or more.

2. Side Plank

Start on your side, with your elbow directly under your shoulder and your legs extended. Lift your hips off the ground, forming a straight line from your head to your heels. Hold for 30 seconds, then switch sides.

How to Lose Belly Fat Video Exercise: Cardio Workouts

While planks are great for toning your abs, cardio workouts are essential for burning fat. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) is a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories.

Personally, I love doing HIIT workouts because they're quick, effective, and fun. Here's an example of a HIIT workout you can do at home:

1. Jumping Jacks

Do jumping jacks for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

2. Burpees

Do burpees for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

3. Mountain Climbers

Do mountain climbers for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

4. High Knees

Do high knees for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do these video exercises?

A: Aim to do these exercises 3-4 times a week, along with other cardio and strength training exercises.

Q: How long will it take to see results?

A: Results will vary based on your current fitness level and lifestyle habits. Consistency is key, so aim to make these exercises a part of your regular routine.

Q: Can I do these exercises if I have back pain?

A: It's always best to consult with a medical professional before starting a new exercise routine, especially if you have any pre-existing conditions. However, planks can be a great way to strengthen your core and alleviate back pain.

Q: Do I need any equipment to do these exercises?

A: All you need is a yoga mat or a soft surface to do these exercises on.
