How Much Cardio Should You Do After Lifting Weights

Written by Oliver Jul 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly and wants to know how much cardio should you do after lifting weights? Are you confused about what to do and how much to do? If yes, then you have come to the right place. Many gym-goers feel confused when it comes to performing cardio after weight training. But don't worry, we will help you clear all your doubts.

After lifting weights, your body needs to recover and repair the muscles. At the same time, cardio exercises help to burn calories, improve cardiovascular health, and aid in weight loss. So, the question arises, how much cardio should you do after lifting weights?

The answer to this question varies from person to person, depending on their fitness goals, body type, and physical capacity. However, a general rule of thumb is to perform cardio for at least 15-20 minutes after lifting weights. This helps to improve blood circulation, aids in recovery, and burns additional calories. However, if you want to focus more on weight loss, you can increase the duration of your cardio exercises.

In summary, cardio after weight training is beneficial, but the amount of cardio will vary depending on what you are trying to achieve. So, you should consult with your trainer to create a workout plan tailored to your specific needs and goals.

My Personal Experience with Cardio After Lifting Weights

As someone who loves to do weight training, I was always skeptical about performing cardio after lifting weights. But after consulting with my trainer, I started incorporating cardio into my workout routine, and I must say that I have seen great results. I usually perform cardio for 20-30 minutes after weight training, and it helps me recover faster and burn extra calories. It has also improved my overall fitness and endurance.

How Much Cardio Should You Do After Lifting Weights for Weight Loss?

If your main goal is weight loss, you should perform cardio for at least 30-40 minutes after weight training. This helps to burn additional calories and aids in weight loss. However, you should consult with your trainer to create a workout plan that suits your fitness level and body type.

Benefits of Cardio After Lifting Weights

Performing cardio after weight training has several benefits, such as:

  • Improves blood circulation
  • Burns additional calories
  • Aids in recovery
  • Improves cardiovascular health

Tips for Performing Cardio After Lifting Weights

If you are new to performing cardio after weight training, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start slow and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your cardio exercises.
  • Choose a cardio exercise that you enjoy, such as running, cycling, or swimming.
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with nutritious food before and after your workout.
  • Consult with your trainer to create a workout plan tailored to your specific needs and goals.

My Personal Experience with Cardio After Lifting Weights for Weight Loss

As someone who wanted to lose weight, I started incorporating cardio into my workout routine after weight training. I usually perform cardio for 30-40 minutes, and it has helped me burn extra calories and lose weight. However, I made sure to consult with my trainer and create a workout plan that suited my fitness level and body type.

Question and Answer

Q. Can I perform cardio before lifting weights?
A. It is not recommended to perform cardio before lifting weights as it can tire out your muscles and affect your weight training performance.

Q. How many times a week should I perform cardio after lifting weights?
A. It depends on your fitness goals and physical capacity. However, performing cardio after lifting weights 2-3 times a week is a good starting point.

Q. Is it okay to skip cardio after lifting weights?
A. It is okay to skip cardio after lifting weights, but performing cardio has several benefits, such as improved cardiovascular health and weight loss.

Q. Can I perform cardio on the same day as weight training?
A. Yes, you can perform cardio on the same day as weight training, but it is recommended to perform weight training first and then cardio.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio Should You Do After Lifting Weights

Performing cardio after lifting weights is beneficial, but the amount of cardio will vary depending on your fitness goals, body type, and physical capacity. It is recommended to consult with your trainer to create a workout plan tailored to your specific needs and goals. Remember to start slow, stay hydrated, and fuel your body with nutritious food before and after your workout.
