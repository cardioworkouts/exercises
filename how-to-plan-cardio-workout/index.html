The How To Plan Cardio Workout Muscle Gain

Written by William Jul 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to plan a cardio workout but don't know where to start? Cardio workouts are an essential part of any fitness routine as they can help improve cardiovascular health, burn calories, and boost overall fitness. However, planning a cardio workout can be overwhelming, especially if you're new to exercise. In this tutorial, we'll guide you through the process of how to plan cardio workout in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

When it comes to planning a cardio workout, the most significant pain point is not knowing where to start. There are so many different types of cardio exercises, and it can be challenging to choose the right one for your fitness level and goals. Additionally, not having a plan can lead to boredom and a lack of motivation, making it harder to stick to your workout routine.

The first step in planning a cardio workout is to determine your fitness level and goals. Are you looking to improve your cardiovascular health, lose weight, or increase your overall fitness? Once you have a clear idea of your goals, you can choose the right type of cardio exercise to help you achieve them.

Here are some steps to follow when planning your cardio workout:

Step 1: Choose Your Cardio Exercise

The first step is to choose the type of cardio exercise you want to do. This could be anything from running, cycling, or swimming to jumping jacks, high knees, or burpees. Choose an exercise that you enjoy doing and that suits your fitness level.

For example, if you're new to exercise, start with a low-impact exercise like walking or cycling. If you're more advanced, you could try high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or running.

Step 2: Determine Your Workout Duration

Next, decide how long you want to work out for. The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. You could break this down into 30-minute sessions, five times a week or 25-minute sessions, three times a week.

Step 3: Set Your Intensity

Determine the intensity of your workout. This will depend on your fitness level and goals. If you're new to exercise, start with a lower intensity and gradually increase it over time. If you're more advanced, you could try high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or running at a faster pace.

Step 4: Plan Your Routine

Finally, plan your cardio workout routine. This could be a combination of different exercises, such as running for 20 minutes, followed by cycling for 10 minutes and then a cool-down walk for 5 minutes. Alternatively, you could do the same exercise for the entire workout, such as cycling for 30 minutes.

Make sure to include a warm-up and cool-down period in your routine to prevent injury and improve recovery time.

Benefits of Cardio Workout

For me, doing a cardio workout has been an excellent way to relieve stress and boost my energy levels. I usually start with a 5-minute warm-up on the treadmill, followed by 20 minutes of running, 10 minutes of cycling, and a cool-down period of 5 minutes. I find that this routine helps me stay motivated and focused during my workout.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake people make when planning a cardio workout is not allowing enough recovery time between workouts. It's essential to give your body time to recover and repair after a workout to prevent injury and improve overall fitness.

Another mistake is not varying your routine enough. Doing the same exercise every day can lead to boredom and a lack of motivation, making it harder to stick to your workout routine.

How Often Should You Do Cardio Workout?

According to the American Heart Association, adults should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. This could be broken down into 30-minute sessions, five times a week or 25-minute sessions, three times a week.

What Are Some Cardio Exercises You Can Do At Home?

There are many different types of cardio exercises you can do at home, including jumping jacks, high knees, burpees, mountain climbers, and jumping rope. You could also do a home workout video or follow a workout routine on an app.

How Can You Make Your Cardio Workout More Challenging?

You can make your cardio workout more challenging by increasing your intensity or adding more resistance. For example, you could increase the incline on the treadmill or add weights to your workout. Another way to challenge yourself is to try a new exercise or vary your routine.

What Are The Benefits Of Doing Cardio Workout?

Cardio workouts have many benefits, including improving cardiovascular health, burning calories, reducing stress, boosting energy levels, and improving overall fitness. Additionally, regular cardio exercise can reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and obesity.

Conclusion of How To Plan Cardio Workout

Planning a cardio workout doesn't have to be complicated. By following these simple steps, you can create a routine that suits your fitness level and goals. Remember to choose an exercise you enjoy doing, set your workout duration and intensity, and plan your routine. By incorporating cardio exercise into your fitness routine, you can improve your health, increase your energy levels, and feel great.
