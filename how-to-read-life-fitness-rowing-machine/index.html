How To Read Life Fitness Rowing Machine A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you new to using a rowing machine and having trouble understanding the display on your Life Fitness rowing machine? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many beginners find it overwhelming and confusing to read the data on the monitor, which can hinder their workout experience. In this tutorial, we will guide you through how to read Life Fitness rowing machine in a simplified manner.

The pain points of how to read Life Fitness rowing machine can range from not understanding the metrics displayed on the monitor to not knowing how to use the settings properly for an effective workout.

The first step in how to read Life Fitness rowing machine is to understand the display. The monitor displays various metrics such as time, distance, stroke rate, and resistance level. The display can also provide additional information such as calories burned and heart rate if you have a compatible heart rate monitor connected.

In summary, to read Life Fitness rowing machine, you need to understand the metrics displayed on the monitor, how to use the settings properly, and how to interpret the data to optimize your workout.

How to use the Monitor on Life Fitness Rowing Machine

When I first started using a Life Fitness rowing machine, I found the monitor confusing. It took some time to understand the different metrics and how to use them to my advantage. Here's what I learned:

The monitor on the Life Fitness rowing machine is divided into four main sections. The top section displays time, distance, and calories burned. The middle section displays stroke rate, which is the number of strokes per minute. The bottom section displays resistance level.

To start your workout, press the "on" button on the monitor. The monitor will display "Ready". Grab the handlebar and start rowing. The monitor will then display the metrics mentioned above. You can adjust the resistance level by pressing the "+" or "-" buttons on the monitor.

How to Interpret the Data on Life Fitness Rowing Machine

Interpreting the data on the monitor is crucial to optimize your workout. Here's what you need to know:

Time: This displays the duration of your workout. Use this to set goals and track your progress.

Distance: This displays the distance rowed during your workout. Use this to set distance goals and track your progress.

Stroke Rate: This displays the number of strokes per minute. Use this to maintain a consistent pace and increase your intensity.

Resistance Level: This displays the resistance level. Use this to adjust the intensity of your workout.

How to Set Goals on Life Fitness Rowing Machine

Setting goals on the Life Fitness rowing machine can help you stay motivated and track your progress. Here's how:

Press the "programs" button on the monitor. You can then choose from various preset programs such as distance, time, or calorie goals. You can also create custom programs by selecting "custom" and setting your own goals.

How to Use the Heart Rate Monitor on Life Fitness Rowing Machine

If you have a compatible heart rate monitor, you can connect it to the Life Fitness rowing machine to track your heart rate during your workout. Here's how:

Press the "heart rate" button on the monitor. The monitor will then display your heart rate. You can adjust the resistance level to maintain your target heart rate.

Conclusion of How to Read Life Fitness Rowing Machine

Reading the data on the Life Fitness rowing machine can be intimidating at first, but with practice and patience, you can learn to read it like a pro. Remember to start with the basics, understand the metrics, and use the settings to your advantage. Set goals and track your progress to stay motivated and achieve your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I connect my phone to the Life Fitness rowing machine?

A: Yes, you can connect your phone to the rowing machine via Bluetooth to play music or use fitness apps.

Q: How do I adjust the footrests on the rowing machine?

A: To adjust the footrests, pull the knob located under the seat and slide the footrests to the desired position. Tighten the knob to secure the footrests in place.

Q: What is the proper rowing technique?

A: The proper rowing technique involves sitting up straight, bending your knees, and holding the handlebar with an overhand grip. Push off with your legs, then lean back slightly and pull the handlebar towards your chest. Reverse the motion to return to the starting position.

Q: How often should I clean my rowing machine?

A: It's recommended to clean your rowing machine after every use to prevent the buildup of sweat, dirt, and bacteria. Use a damp cloth to wipe down the machine and a disinfectant spray to sanitize it.
