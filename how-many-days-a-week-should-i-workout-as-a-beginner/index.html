How Many Days A Week Should I Workout As A Beginner

Written by Eveline Jun 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Starting a workout routine can be overwhelming, especially for beginners who are unsure how many days a week they should be exercising. It's important to find a balance that works for you to avoid burnout, injuries, or giving up altogether.

The pain points of how many days a week should I workout as a beginner can include not knowing where to start, feeling pressured to exercise every day, or worrying about not seeing results quickly enough.

The general recommendation for beginners is to aim for at least three days of exercise per week. This allows your body to adjust to the new routine while still providing enough activity to see progress. It's also essential to include rest days to give your muscles time to recover.

In summary, the key is to start slow and build up gradually. Aim for three days of exercise per week, with a mix of strength training and cardio, and include rest days for recovery.

Personal Experience:

When I first started working out, I made the mistake of trying to exercise every day. I quickly became exhausted and didn't see any results, which made me feel discouraged. It wasn't until I scaled back and started a three-day-a-week routine that I started seeing progress and feeling more energized.

Importance of Variety:

It's also important to note that variety plays a significant role in any workout routine. Mixing up your exercises, such as switching between cardio and strength training, can help prevent boredom and keep your body challenged.

Additional Tips:

If you're still unsure how many days a week you should be working out, consider consulting with a personal trainer or a healthcare professional. They can help create a customized plan that fits your fitness goals and level of experience.

How to Avoid Burnout:

One way to avoid burnout is to schedule your workouts ahead of time and treat them like any other appointment. This not only helps you stay accountable but also ensures you don't overdo it by trying to cram too many workouts into one week.

Personal Experience:

I found that scheduling my workouts helped me stay consistent and made exercising feel like a regular part of my routine. It also gave me something to look forward to each week.

Question and Answer:

Q: Is it okay to work out every day as a beginner?

A: It's not recommended to work out every day as a beginner. Your body needs time to recover and adapt to the new routine. Aim for at least three days of exercise per week and include rest days.

Q: How long should each workout session be?

A: The duration of each workout session will depend on your fitness level and personal goals. However, a general guideline is to aim for 30 minutes to an hour of exercise per session.

Q: Can I do the same workout routine every day?

A: It's not recommended to do the same workout routine every day as it can lead to overuse injuries and plateauing. It's important to mix up your exercises and challenge your body in different ways.

Q: How can I track my progress?

A: Keeping a workout journal or using a fitness app can help track your progress, such as the number of reps, sets, or weight lifted. Taking progress pictures or measurements can also be a helpful visual tool.

Conclusion of How Many Days a Week Should I Workout as a Beginner:

Starting a workout routine can be intimidating, but it's important to find a balance that works for your fitness level and goals. Aim for at least three days of exercise per week, mix up your exercises, and include rest days for recovery. Remember to start slow and build up gradually to avoid burnout or injuries. And don't forget to track your progress to stay motivated and see the results of your hard work.
