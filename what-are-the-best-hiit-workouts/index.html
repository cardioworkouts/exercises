Get Fit Faster The Best Hiit Workouts You Need To Try

Written by Eveline Jun 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a workout that can get you in shape quickly and efficiently, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) might be just what you need. HIIT workouts are designed to burn a lot of calories in a short amount of time, making them perfect for busy people who want to get fit fast.

In this article, we'll answer the question of what are the best HIIT workouts and provide you with some great examples to try. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced athlete, you can benefit from adding HIIT to your workout routine.

In short, HIIT workouts are a type of exercise that combines short bursts of high-intensity activity with periods of rest or low-intensity activity. These workouts can be done with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, jumping jacks, and more.

The benefits of HIIT workouts are numerous. They can help you burn more calories in less time, improve your cardiovascular health, increase your endurance, and boost your metabolism. Additionally, HIIT workouts can be done with minimal equipment or even no equipment at all, making them a great option for those who prefer to exercise at home.

The Tabata Workout

One of the most popular HIIT workouts is the Tabata workout. In this workout, you perform eight rounds of 20 seconds of high-intensity activity followed by 10 seconds of rest. The total workout takes just four minutes, but it can be incredibly intense.

For example, you might do 20 seconds of burpees followed by 10 seconds of rest, then 20 seconds of mountain climbers followed by 10 seconds of rest, and so on for eight rounds. This workout can be done with bodyweight exercises or with weights, depending on your preference.

The EMOM Workout

Another great HIIT workout is the EMOM workout, which stands for "every minute on the minute." In this workout, you perform a set of exercises at the start of every minute and then rest for the remainder of the minute.

For example, you might do 10 squats and 10 push-ups at the start of every minute for 10 minutes. This workout can be easily modified to fit your fitness level by adjusting the number of reps or the exercises themselves.

The AMRAP Workout

The AMRAP workout, which stands for "as many rounds as possible," is another popular HIIT workout. In this workout, you perform a set of exercises for a set amount of time and try to complete as many rounds as possible within that time frame.

For example, you might do five pull-ups, 10 push-ups, and 15 squats for 20 minutes, trying to complete as many rounds as possible in that time. This workout can be incredibly challenging but is a great way to push yourself and improve your fitness.

The 10-20-30 Workout

The 10-20-30 workout is a HIIT workout that involves doing 30 seconds of low-intensity exercise, followed by 20 seconds of moderate-intensity exercise, and then 10 seconds of high-intensity exercise. You can repeat this cycle for as many rounds as you like.

For example, you might do 30 seconds of walking, followed by 20 seconds of jogging, and then 10 seconds of sprinting. You can repeat this cycle for 10 minutes or more, depending on your fitness level.

Question and Answer

Q: What are the best HIIT workouts for beginners?

A: The Tabata and 10-20-30 workouts are great options for beginners, as they can be easily modified to fit your fitness level.

Q: How often should I do HIIT workouts?

A: It's recommended to do HIIT workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

Q: Can I do HIIT workouts at home?

A: Absolutely! HIIT workouts can be done with little to no equipment, making them a great option for at-home workouts.

Q: Are HIIT workouts suitable for everyone?

A: While HIIT workouts can be modified to fit different fitness levels, they may not be suitable for everyone. It's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine.

Conclusion of what are the best HIIT workouts

HIIT workouts are a great way to get fit quickly and efficiently. With a variety of workouts to choose from and the ability to modify them to fit your fitness level, there's no reason not to give HIIT a try. Remember to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine and to listen to your body to prevent injury.
