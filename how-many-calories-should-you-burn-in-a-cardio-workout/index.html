How Many Calories Should You Burn In A Cardio Workout

Written by Eveline Jun 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to shed some extra pounds and get in shape? Cardio workouts are a great way to burn calories and get your heart pumping. However, if you're new to working out, you may be wondering how many calories you should be burning during a cardio session. In this post, we'll explore the topic of how many calories you should burn in a cardio workout and give you some tips to help you reach your fitness goals.

When it comes to cardio workouts, the number of calories you burn will depend on a few factors, including your age, weight, height, and the intensity of your workout. The American Heart Association recommends that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. This equates to burning 300-500 calories per workout session.

To determine how many calories you should be burning during a cardio workout, you can use an online calorie calculator that takes into account your age, weight, height, and activity level. This will give you an estimate of the number of calories you should aim to burn during your workout.

In summary, the number of calories you should burn in a cardio workout will depend on your personal factors, such as age, weight, and height, as well as the intensity of your workout. Aim to burn 300-500 calories per session to meet the recommended guidelines for cardio exercise.

How to Burn More Calories During a Cardio Workout

One effective way to burn more calories during a cardio workout is to increase the intensity of your exercise. This can be done by increasing the speed, resistance, or incline of your workout equipment. You can also try interval training, which involves alternating periods of high-intensity exercise with periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This can help you burn more calories and improve your overall fitness level.

The Importance of Rest and Recovery

While it's important to push yourself during a cardio workout to burn calories, it's also crucial to give your body time to rest and recover. Overtraining can lead to injury, fatigue, and burnout, which can ultimately hinder your progress. Make sure to give your body adequate rest between workouts, and incorporate stretching and foam rolling into your routine to prevent injury and improve flexibility.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Cardio Workouts

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your cardio workouts:

1. Mix up your workouts to prevent boredom and challenge your body.

2. Wear comfortable, supportive footwear to prevent injury and improve performance.

3. Stay hydrated before, during, and after your workout to maintain energy and prevent dehydration.

4. Fuel your body with healthy foods to provide energy for your workouts and promote recovery.

How to Incorporate Cardio Workouts into Your Fitness Routine

If you're new to working out, start by incorporating cardio workouts into your routine 2-3 times per week. Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts as your fitness level improves. You can also combine cardio with strength training and stretching for a well-rounded fitness routine.

Conclusion of How Many Calories Should You Burn in a Cardio Workout

Burning calories through a cardio workout can be an effective way to improve your fitness level and reach your weight loss goals. By using an online calorie calculator and aiming to burn 300-500 calories per session, you can ensure that you're meeting the recommended guidelines for cardio exercise. Remember to listen to your body, rest and recover adequately, and incorporate healthy habits into your routine to maximize your results.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should a cardio workout be?

A: The American Heart Association recommends getting at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. This can be broken down into 30-minute sessions 5 days a week for moderate-intensity exercise, or 25-minute sessions 3 days a week for vigorous-intensity exercise.

Q: What are some low-impact cardio workouts?

A: Low-impact cardio workouts include swimming, cycling, and the elliptical machine. These workouts are easier on the joints than high-impact activities like running or jumping.

Q: How many calories can you burn in a 30-minute cardio workout?

A: The number of calories you burn in a 30-minute cardio workout will depend on your personal factors and the intensity of your workout. However, on average, you can expect to burn between 150-300 calories.

Q: How often should you do cardio workouts?

A: The American Heart Association recommends getting at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. This equates to 2-3 sessions per week for moderate-intensity exercise, or 1-2 sessions per week for vigorous-intensity exercise.
