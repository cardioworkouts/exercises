Cardio Before Weight Training Or After Which Is Better

Written by Christine Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who is confused about the best time to do cardio? Do you wonder whether to do cardio before or after weight training? Well, you're not alone. This is a common question that many fitness enthusiasts ask. In this blog post, we will delve into the topic of cardio before weight training or after and help you understand the benefits and drawbacks of both.

What is the target of cardio before weight training or after?

The target of cardio before weight training or after is to understand the effects of doing cardio at different times of the day. While some people believe that cardio before weight training is the best way to burn more calories, others think that doing cardio after weight training leads to better muscle growth. Let's explore both these scenarios in detail.

My Personal Experience with Cardio Before Weight Training or After

Personally, I prefer doing cardio before weight training. This is because it helps me warm up my body and get my heart rate up. I usually start with a 10-minute jog on the treadmill or a 5-minute jump rope session. This helps me break a sweat and get my body ready for the weights. I've tried doing cardio after weight training, but I always feel too exhausted to do a proper cardio session. However, this is just my personal preference, and what works for me may not work for you.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weight Training

Doing cardio before weight training has several benefits. Firstly, it helps you warm up your body and get your heart rate up. This, in turn, helps you burn more calories during your weight training session. Secondly, it helps you improve your endurance and stamina, which is crucial for weight training. Thirdly, it helps you get into a better mental state, as cardio releases endorphins that make you feel good and happy.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Before Weight Training

While there are benefits to doing cardio before weight training, there are also some drawbacks. Firstly, doing cardio before weight training can be tiring, which may affect your weight training session. Secondly, it may reduce your strength and power during weight training, as your body may be fatigued from the cardio session. Lastly, it may increase the risk of injury, as your muscles may be tired and more susceptible to injury.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Weight Training

Doing cardio after weight training also has its benefits. Firstly, it helps you burn more fat, as your body has already depleted its glycogen stores during weight training. Secondly, it helps you improve your cardiovascular health and endurance. Lastly, it helps you cool down and recover from your weight training session.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio After Weight Training

While doing cardio after weight training has its benefits, there are also some drawbacks. Firstly, it may reduce your energy levels, as your body may be fatigued from the weight training session. Secondly, it may lead to overtraining, as your body may not be able to handle both weight training and cardio in one session. Lastly, it may not be suitable for people with time constraints, as doing cardio after weight training may increase the overall time spent in the gym.

Which is Better: Cardio Before Weight Training or After?

The answer to this question depends on your personal preferences and fitness goals. If you want to burn more calories and improve your stamina, then doing cardio before weight training may be the best option for you. On the other hand, if you want to burn more fat and improve your cardiovascular health, then doing cardio after weight training may be a better option. Ultimately, it's important to listen to your body and do what works best for you.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to do cardio before weight training or after?

A: No, it's not necessary to do cardio before or after weight training. It depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences.

Q: How long should I do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's recommended to do at least 20-30 minutes of cardio before or after weight training.

Q: Can doing cardio before weight training affect my muscle growth?

A: Yes, doing too much cardio before weight training may reduce your strength and power, which may affect your muscle growth.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day. However, it's important to space them out and not do them back-to-back.

Conclusion of Cardio Before Weight Training or After

In conclusion, doing cardio before weight training or after has its benefits and drawbacks. Ultimately, it depends on your personal preferences and fitness goals. It's important to listen to your body and do what works best for you. Remember to warm up properly before any exercise, and stay hydrated throughout your workout session.
