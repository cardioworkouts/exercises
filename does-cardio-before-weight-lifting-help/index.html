Does Cardio Before Weight Lifting Help

Written by Petter Jul 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who has been hitting the gym regularly but still not getting the desired results? Do you often find yourself confused about whether to do cardio before weight lifting or vice versa? If yes, then you have landed in the right place. In this blog post, we will discuss the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before weight lifting and if it can actually help in achieving your fitness goals.

One of the most common pain points related to does cardio before weight lifting help is that people often don't know the right way to do it. They are unsure about the order of exercises and whether it will affect their muscle building process or not. Moreover, some individuals also believe that doing cardio before weight lifting can be tiring and lead to a decrease in energy levels during weight lifting.

The simple answer to the question of whether cardio before weight lifting helps or not is that it depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to build muscle mass and strength, then weight lifting should be your priority. However, if your aim is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then doing cardio before weight lifting can be beneficial.

In summary, the main points related to does cardio before weight lifting help are that it can be beneficial for improving cardiovascular endurance, but it may not be the best approach for those who want to build muscle mass and strength. It is all about finding the right balance that works for your fitness goals.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weight Lifting

Personally, I have found doing cardio before weight lifting to be effective in boosting my energy levels and getting my heart rate up. It helps me warm up my body and prepare for the heavier lifting. Additionally, cardio before weight lifting can also help in burning more calories and reducing body fat, which is particularly helpful for those looking to lose weight.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Before Weight Lifting

One of the significant drawbacks of doing cardio before weight lifting is that it can lead to fatigue and affect your weight lifting performance. As a result, you may not be able to lift as heavy as you would if you were fresh. Moreover, if you do cardio before weight lifting for an extended period, it may also lead to muscle loss, which is not ideal for those who want to build muscle mass.

How to Do Cardio Before Weight Lifting

If you have decided to do cardio before weight lifting, then it is essential to keep a few things in mind. Firstly, make sure you warm up your body with some light cardio exercises such as jogging or cycling for 10-15 minutes. Secondly, keep the intensity low to moderate as you don't want to exhaust yourself before weight lifting. Lastly, ensure that you give your body enough time to recover between cardio and weight lifting, ideally around 10-15 minutes.

Tips for Maximizing Results

If you want to maximize the benefits of doing cardio before weight lifting, then you should consider the following tips:

  • Keep the intensity low to moderate
  • Focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups
  • Gradually increase your cardio duration and intensity over time
  • Ensure you are getting enough rest and recovery time

Personal Experience

Personally, I have tried both approaches of doing cardio before and after weight lifting, and I have found that doing cardio before weight lifting suits me best. It helps me warm up, improves my energy levels, and helps me burn more calories. However, I ensure that I don't over-exhaust myself and take enough rest between cardio and weight lifting.

Question and Answer

Q. Is it better to do cardio before or after weight lifting?
A. It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass and strength, then weight lifting should be your priority. However, if your aim is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then doing cardio before weight lifting can be beneficial.

Q. How long should you do cardio before weight lifting?
A. Ideally, you should do cardio for 10-15 minutes before weight lifting to warm up your body and get your heart rate up.

Q. Can doing cardio before weight lifting lead to muscle loss?
A. Doing cardio before weight lifting for an extended period may lead to muscle loss, particularly if you are not eating enough protein and not giving your body enough rest and recovery time.

Q. Can doing cardio before weight lifting affect your weight lifting performance?
A. Yes, doing cardio before weight lifting can lead to fatigue and affect your weight lifting performance. As a result, you may not be able to lift as heavy as you would if you were fresh.

Conclusion of Does Cardio Before Weight Lifting Help

To sum it up, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to whether cardio before weight lifting helps or not. It depends on your fitness goals, and you should choose the approach that works best for you. If you are looking to build muscle mass and strength, then weight lifting should be your priority. However, if your aim is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then doing cardio before weight lifting can be beneficial. The key is to find the right balance and be consistent with your workout routine.
