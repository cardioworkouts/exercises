How to Design a Full Body Workout Program at Gym?

Designing a full body workout program at gym might seem daunting at first, but it's actually quite simple. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step 1: Choose Your Exercises

The first step is to choose the exercises that you want to include in your workout. A full body workout should target all the major muscle groups of your body, including your chest, back, shoulders, arms, abs, and legs. You can choose compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press.

Step 2: Determine Your Sets and Reps

Once you have chosen your exercises, the next step is to determine the number of sets and reps for each exercise. The number of sets and reps will depend on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, beginners should start with 2-3 sets of 12-15 reps, while advanced fitness enthusiasts can do 3-4 sets of 8-10 reps.

Step 3: Choose Your Rest Periods

Rest periods are an important aspect of any workout program. They give your muscles time to recover between sets, which is essential for muscle growth and strength. The ideal rest period between sets is 60-90 seconds for beginners and 30-60 seconds for advanced fitness enthusiasts.

Step 4: Plan Your Workout Schedule

The final step is to plan your workout schedule. A full body workout program at gym should be done 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest between each workout. You can also alternate between different exercises to keep your workout interesting and challenging.
