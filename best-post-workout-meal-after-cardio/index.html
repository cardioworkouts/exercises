Best Post Workout Meal After Cardio What To Eat

Written by Robby Oct 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you trying to lose weight or improve your overall health? Cardio can be a great way to burn calories and improve your cardiovascular health. However, what you eat after your workout is just as important as the workout itself. In this post, we'll discuss the best post workout meal after cardio and why it matters.

After a cardio workout, your body needs nutrients to replenish its energy stores and repair any damage done during exercise. Without proper nutrition, you may experience fatigue, muscle soreness, and slower recovery times. That's why it's crucial to eat the right foods after your workout.

The best post workout meal after cardio should include protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats. Protein is essential for repairing and building muscle tissue, while carbohydrates provide energy to replenish glycogen stores in your muscles. Healthy fats help regulate hormones and reduce inflammation.

To summarize, the best post workout meal after cardio should include:

Protein:

Protein is crucial for muscle repair and growth. Aim for at least 20-30 grams of protein after your workout. Good sources of protein include:

- Chicken or turkey breast
- Fish
- Greek yogurt
- Eggs
- Protein powder (whey or plant-based)

Carbohydrates:

Carbohydrates provide energy and replenish glycogen stores in your muscles. Aim for 30-50 grams of carbohydrates after your workout. Good sources of carbohydrates include:

- Fruits (such as bananas, berries, and apples)
- Sweet potatoes
- Brown rice
- Whole grain bread
- Quinoa

Healthy Fats:

Healthy fats are important for hormone regulation and reducing inflammation. Aim for 10-15 grams of healthy fats after your workout. Good sources of healthy fats include:

- Avocado
- Nuts and seeds
- Olive oil
- Coconut oil
- Fatty fish (such as salmon or tuna)

Personally, I like to make a post workout smoothie with protein powder, banana, almond milk, and a tablespoon of almond butter. It's delicious and provides all the nutrients I need to recover from my workouts.

Hydration:

Hydration is also crucial after a workout. Drinking water or electrolyte-infused drinks can help replenish fluids lost during exercise and prevent dehydration.

Meal Timing:

It's also essential to eat your post workout meal within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout. This window of time is when your body is most receptive to nutrients, and eating during this time can help speed up recovery times.

Common Misconceptions:

There are a few common misconceptions about post workout nutrition. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Myth 1: You should only eat protein after your workout.

While protein is essential, you also need carbohydrates and healthy fats to properly recover from your workout. Make sure to include all three macronutrients in your post workout meal.

Myth 2: You need to eat a large meal after your workout.

You don't need to eat a huge meal after your workout. Instead, focus on getting the right balance of nutrients in a smaller meal or snack.

Myth 3: You can't eat carbs if you want to lose weight.

Carbohydrates are an essential part of a healthy diet and can help you lose weight when consumed in moderation. Just make sure to choose healthy sources of carbohydrates, such as fruits and whole grains.

Overall, the best post workout meal after cardio should include protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats. Don't forget to hydrate and eat within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout. With the right nutrition, you can speed up recovery times and see better results from your cardio workouts.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the best post workout meal after cardio is one that includes protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats. Make sure to hydrate and eat within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout. Don't fall for common misconceptions about post workout nutrition. With the right fuel, you can optimize your workouts and achieve your fitness goals.
