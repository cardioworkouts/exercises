How Long Should I Workout As A Beginner

Written by Christine Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you new to exercising and wondering how long you should workout for optimal results? It can be overwhelming to navigate the world of fitness, especially for beginners. You may be wondering how long to workout, what exercises to do, and how often to do them. In this article, we will focus on the question, "How long should I workout as a beginner?"

Starting a workout routine can be daunting, and finding the right amount of time to exercise can be a challenge. As a beginner, you may feel unsure about how much exercise is appropriate and how much is too much. You may also worry about not doing enough and not seeing results.

As a general rule of thumb, beginners should aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise per day, five days a week. You can gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts as you become more comfortable and fit. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard, too fast.

In summary, beginners should aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise per day, five days a week. Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts as you become more comfortable and fit.

Personal Experience

When I first started exercising, I was unsure of how long I should workout. I started with 20 minutes of moderate exercise, three days a week. As I became more comfortable, I increased my workout time to 30 minutes and added two more days to my routine. I found that this schedule worked well for me and helped me to see results without feeling overwhelmed.

Importance of Consistency

It's important to remember that consistency is key when it comes to exercise. You may not see results overnight, but with consistent effort, you will see progress over time. It's better to workout for a shorter amount of time consistently than to have long, sporadic workout sessions.

Factors to Consider

When determining how long to workout as a beginner, there are several factors to consider. These include your fitness level, age, health status, and goals. It's important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine, especially if you have any health concerns.

Tips for Getting Started

If you're new to exercising, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Choose activities you enjoy
  • Set realistic goals
  • Make exercise a habit
  • Find a workout buddy
  • Track your progress

Personal Experience

When I first started exercising, I struggled to find activities that I enjoyed. I tried running, but quickly realized it wasn't for me. I eventually found a love for yoga and strength training. It's important to find activities that you enjoy, as it will make it easier to stick with your routine.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to workout longer or harder?

A: It's better to workout harder for a shorter amount of time than to workout longer at a lower intensity. High-intensity workouts can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time.

Q: Can I workout every day as a beginner?

A: It's important to give your body time to rest and recover, especially as a beginner. Aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise, five days a week, and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts over time.

Q: How do I know if I'm overdoing it?

A: Listen to your body. If you experience pain, fatigue, or dizziness, it's a sign that you may be overdoing it. It's important to start slowly and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts.

Q: How long does it take to see results?

A: It can take several weeks to several months to see results from exercise. It's important to stay consistent and patient, as results will come with time.

Conclusion of How Long Should I Workout as a Beginner?

As a beginner, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts. Aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise, five days a week. Consistency is key when it comes to exercise, so find activities you enjoy and make exercise a habit. Consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine, especially if you have any health concerns.
