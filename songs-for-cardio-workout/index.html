The Best Songs For Cardio Workouts Pump Up Your Exercise Routine

Written by Eveline Oct 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Do you struggle to find the motivation to exercise? Do you find yourself getting bored during your cardio workouts? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to stay motivated and engaged during their workouts, and finding the right music can make all the difference. In this article, we'll explore the best songs for cardio workouts and how they can help you stay motivated and energized.

Whether you're running, cycling, or doing any other type of cardio exercise, it's important to have music that matches your pace and keeps you motivated. The right music can help you push through a tough workout and even make it enjoyable. But with so many different songs and genres to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know where to start.

The target of songs for cardio workout is to find music that matches the tempo of your exercise routine. This helps you stay on beat and maintain a consistent pace. The best songs for cardio workouts are those with a tempo of 120-140 beats per minute (BPM). This range matches the average person's heart rate during exercise and can help you maintain a steady rhythm.

In summary, when it comes to selecting the best songs for cardio workouts, you need to focus on the tempo of the music. The ideal range is between 120-140 BPM, which matches the average person's heart rate during exercise. With that in mind, let's explore some of the top songs for cardio workouts.

Songs for Cardio Workout: Pop and Dance Hits

When it comes to cardio workouts, pop and dance hits are always a great option. These upbeat songs are designed to get you moving and keep you energized throughout your workout. Some of the top pop and dance songs for cardio workouts include:

- "Blinding Lights" by The Weeknd

- "Dynamite" by BTS

- "Levitating" by Dua Lipa

- "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars

- "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake

Personally, I love listening to pop and dance hits during my cardio workouts. The infectious beats and catchy lyrics always help me power through a tough workout. Plus, these songs are easy to find on popular streaming services like Spotify and Apple Music.

Songs for Cardio Workout: Rock and Alternative

If pop and dance hits aren't your thing, don't worry. There are plenty of great rock and alternative songs that are perfect for cardio workouts. These songs have a higher BPM and can help you maintain a faster pace during your workout. Some of the top rock and alternative songs for cardio workouts include:

- "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

- "Enter Sandman" by Metallica

- "Smells Like Teen Spirit" by Nirvana

- "All These Things That I've Done" by The Killers

- "Seven Nation Army" by The White Stripes

Personally, I love listening to rock and alternative songs when I'm running or cycling. The high-energy beats and powerful lyrics always help me stay focused and motivated.

The Impact of Music on Cardio Workouts

Research has shown that music can have a significant impact on exercise performance. In one study, participants who listened to music during their workouts were able to exercise for longer periods of time and had a higher overall endurance than those who exercised without music. Another study found that listening to music can help reduce feelings of fatigue and increase motivation during exercise.

So why does music have such a powerful impact on exercise? One theory is that music distracts the brain from the physical sensations of exercise, such as fatigue and discomfort. It also triggers the release of endorphins, which are natural painkillers that can help reduce feelings of discomfort during exercise.

Tips for Creating the Perfect Cardio Workout Playlist

Creating the perfect playlist for your cardio workout can be a lot of fun. Here are some tips to help you get started:

- Start with a warm-up song that has a slower BPM to ease into your workout.

- Mix up the genres to keep things interesting.

- Include songs that have a variety of tempos to match the different stages of your workout.

- End with a cool-down song that has a slower BPM to help you relax and recover.

Personally, I like to start my playlist with a slower song like "Stuck with U" by Ariana Grande and Justin Bieber, and gradually increase the tempo with songs like "Circles" by Post Malone and "Mr. Brightside" by The Killers. I always end my playlist with a slower song like "Imagine" by John Lennon to help me cool down and relax.

Question and Answer

Q: Can listening to music during a cardio workout help you burn more calories?

A: While music can help you stay motivated and energized during your workout, it does not directly impact the number of calories you burn. However, if music helps you exercise for longer periods of time or at a higher intensity, you may burn more calories as a result.

Q: How can I find the BPM of a song?

A: There are several online resources that can help you find the BPM of a song, including SongBPM.com and GetSongBPM.com. You can also use a smartphone app like TempoPerfect to measure the BPM of a song.

Q: Is it better to listen to music with lyrics or without lyrics during a cardio workout?

A: There is no definitive answer to this question, as it ultimately comes down to personal preference. Some people prefer to listen to music with lyrics because it helps them stay motivated and engaged, while others prefer instrumental music because it is less distracting.

Q: Can listening to music during a cardio workout help reduce feelings of pain and discomfort?

A: Yes, research has shown that listening to music during exercise can help reduce feelings of pain and discomfort. This is due in part to the release of endorphins, which are natural painkillers that can help reduce feelings of discomfort during exercise.

Conclusion of Songs for Cardio Workout

In conclusion, the right music can make all the difference when it comes to cardio workouts. By selecting songs with a tempo of 120-140 BPM, you can maintain a consistent pace and stay motivated throughout your workout. Whether you prefer pop and dance hits or rock and alternative tunes, there are plenty of great options to choose from. So, put together your perfect workout playlist and get ready to pump up your exercise routine!
