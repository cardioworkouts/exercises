Cardio After Workout Everything You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Jun 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you wondering if you should do cardio after a workout? Maybe you've heard that it's beneficial, but you're not sure why. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of cardio after a workout and what you need to know about this exercise routine.

Many people struggle with post-workout fatigue and muscle soreness. Cardio after a workout can help alleviate these pains and promote recovery. Additionally, it can help you burn more calories and improve your overall fitness level.

Cardio after a workout involves performing aerobic exercises such as running, cycling, or swimming after completing a strength-training workout. The goal is to increase your heart rate and keep it elevated for a specific period of time.

In summary, cardio after a workout can help you recover faster, burn more calories, and improve your overall fitness level.

Benefits of Cardio After a Workout

When I first started incorporating cardio after a workout, I was skeptical of its benefits. However, after doing it consistently for a few weeks, I noticed a significant improvement in my recovery time and overall fitness level.

One of the main benefits of cardio after a workout is that it helps to flush out lactic acid buildup that causes muscle soreness. By increasing blood flow to the muscles, cardio can help to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

Additionally, cardio after a workout can help to boost your metabolism and burn more calories. This is because your body continues to burn calories at a higher rate for several hours after you've finished exercising.

How to Incorporate Cardio After a Workout

If you're interested in incorporating cardio after a workout, it's important to do it properly to avoid injury. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

1. Start slowly: If you're new to cardio after a workout, start with just a few minutes of low-intensity exercise and gradually increase the duration and intensity over time.

2. Choose the right exercises: Running, cycling, and swimming are all great options for cardio after a workout. Choose exercises that you enjoy and that work well with your fitness level.

3. Focus on recovery: Be sure to stretch and foam roll after your workout to help your muscles recover. Additionally, be sure to eat a healthy meal or snack within 30 minutes of completing your workout.

FAQs About Cardio After a Workout

Q: How long should I do cardio after a workout?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're new to cardio after a workout, start with just a few minutes and gradually increase the duration over time. Aim for at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after a workout for maximum benefits.

Q: Is cardio after a workout suitable for everyone?

A: While cardio after a workout can be beneficial for most people, it's important to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine.

Q: Can I do cardio before a workout?

A: It's generally recommended to do strength training before cardio. This is because strength training requires more energy and can help to warm up your muscles before cardio.

Q: How often should I do cardio after a workout?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. Aim for at least 2-3 times per week, but listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed.

Conclusion of Cardio After a Workout

Cardio after a workout can be a great way to improve your recovery time, burn more calories, and improve your overall fitness level. By following the tips outlined in this article and listening to your body, you can safely and effectively incorporate cardio after a workout into your exercise routine.
