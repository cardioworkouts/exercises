How Often Should I Do Cardio To Maintain Weight

Written by Robby Jun 18, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you trying to maintain your weight but unsure about how often you should do cardio? You’re not alone. Many people struggle with finding the perfect balance between cardio and weight maintenance.

The answer to the question of how often should I do cardio to maintain weight depends on many factors such as your age, weight, gender, and overall fitness level. However, there are some general guidelines you can follow.

To maintain your weight, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week. This can be broken down into 30 minutes of exercise for five days a week. Alternatively, you can aim for 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio exercise per week or a combination of moderate and vigorous exercise.

In summary, to maintain your weight, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week, which can be broken down into 30 minutes of exercise for five days a week.

Personal Experience

When I was trying to maintain my weight, I found that doing cardio for 30 minutes, five times a week worked best for me. I would usually go for a jog or a brisk walk around my neighborhood. It helped me maintain my weight and also improved my overall fitness level.

Factors Affecting How Often You Should Do Cardio

Several factors can affect how often you should do cardio to maintain your weight. These include:

Age and Gender

Your age and gender can affect how often you should do cardio to maintain your weight. Women tend to have a slower metabolism than men, which means they may need to do more cardio to burn the same amount of calories. Additionally, as you age, your metabolism slows down, which means you may need to do more cardio to maintain your weight.

Fitness Level

Your fitness level also plays a role in how often you should do cardio to maintain your weight. If you’re just starting out, you may need to do less cardio to avoid injury. As you become more fit, you may need to increase the amount of cardio you do to continue to see results.

Personal Experience

I started with just 15 minutes of cardio per day when I first started working out. As I got fitter, I slowly increased the amount of cardio I did until I was doing 30 minutes, five times a week. It took time, but it paid off in the end.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do too much cardio?

A: Yes, doing too much cardio can lead to overtraining and injury. It’s important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard.

Q: Can I do other types of exercise besides cardio to maintain my weight?

A: Yes, strength training and other forms of exercise can help you maintain your weight. However, cardio is an important component of any fitness routine.

Q: Can I do cardio every day?

A: It’s possible to do cardio every day, but it’s important to give your body time to rest and recover. Aim for at least one rest day per week.

Q: How long should I do cardio for?

A: Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per day, five times a week.

Conclusion of How Often Should I Do Cardio to Maintain Weight

Overall, to maintain your weight, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week, which can be broken down into 30 minutes of exercise for five days a week. However, the amount of cardio you need to do can depend on several factors such as your age, weight, gender, and fitness level. Always listen to your body and adjust your cardio routine accordingly.
