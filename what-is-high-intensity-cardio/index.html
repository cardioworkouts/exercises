What Is High Intensity Cardio And How To Do It

Written by Frank Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to get fit and burn fat quickly? High intensity cardio might be just what you need. It's a type of exercise that can help you lose weight, improve your cardiovascular health, and boost your energy levels. In this article, we'll explore what high intensity cardio is, how to do it, and its benefits.

If you're struggling to lose weight or want to improve your fitness level, high intensity cardio can be a great option. This type of exercise is designed to get your heart rate up and burn calories quickly. It can be challenging, but the rewards are well worth it. Plus, there are many different types of high intensity cardio workouts to choose from, so you're sure to find something that suits your preferences and fitness level.

What is High Intensity Cardio?

High intensity cardio is a type of exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. The goal is to get your heart rate up to a high level and keep it there for a short period of time. This type of workout can be done with many different types of exercises, including running, cycling, rowing, and even jumping jacks.

During a high intensity cardio workout, your body burns more calories than it would during a low-intensity workout. This is because high intensity exercise increases your metabolism and keeps it elevated for hours after you finish working out. This means you'll continue to burn calories even after you've finished exercising.

How to Do High Intensity Cardio

If you're new to high intensity cardio, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. You should also speak to your doctor before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have any health conditions or injuries.

One way to do high intensity cardio is with interval training. This involves alternating short bursts of high-intensity exercise with periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. For example, you might run at a fast pace for 30 seconds, then walk for 30 seconds, and repeat this cycle for 20-30 minutes.

Another way to do high intensity cardio is with circuit training. This involves doing a series of exercises in a specific order with little or no rest in between. For example, you might do a set of squats, followed by push-ups, lunges, and jumping jacks, and repeat this circuit for 20-30 minutes.

The Benefits of High Intensity Cardio

High intensity cardio has many benefits, including:

  • Burning more calories in less time
  • Improving cardiovascular health
  • Boosting metabolism
  • Reducing the risk of chronic diseases
  • Increasing energy levels

Tips for Doing High Intensity Cardio Safely

Here are some tips for doing high intensity cardio safely:

  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts
  • Speak to your doctor before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have any health conditions or injuries
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after your workout
  • Wear comfortable, supportive shoes and clothing
  • Warm up before starting your workout and cool down afterwards

Real Life Experience with High Intensity Cardio

Personally, I have found high intensity cardio to be a great way to get in shape and improve my fitness level. I started with interval training on the treadmill, and gradually worked my way up to doing circuit training with bodyweight exercises. I have noticed a significant improvement in my endurance, strength, and overall health since starting high intensity cardio.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio?

A: It's recommended to do high intensity cardio 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts.

Q: Can I do high intensity cardio if I have joint pain?

A: If you have joint pain, it's important to speak to your doctor before starting any new exercise program. They may recommend modifications or alternative exercises to reduce the impact on your joints.

Q: How long should a high intensity cardio workout be?

A: A high intensity cardio workout can be as short as 10-15 minutes or as long as 60 minutes, depending on your fitness level and the type of workout you're doing.

Q: Can I do high intensity cardio if I'm pregnant?

A: It's important to speak to your doctor before doing any exercise during pregnancy. They may recommend modifications or alternative exercises to ensure your safety and the safety of your baby.

Conclusion of What is High Intensity Cardio

High intensity cardio is a great way to improve your fitness level, burn fat, and boost your energy levels. It can be challenging, but the rewards are well worth it. By starting slowly and gradually increasing the intensity and duration of your workouts, you can safely enjoy the benefits of high intensity cardio. Remember to always speak to your doctor before starting any new exercise program, and to listen to your body to avoid injury.
