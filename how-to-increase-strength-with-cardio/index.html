How To Increase Strength With Cardio A Simple Guide

Written by Christine Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to boost your strength but don't know where to start? Most people think that lifting heavy weights is the only way to gain strength. But did you know that cardio can also help you increase your strength? In this post, we will discuss how to increase strength with cardio in a simple and straightforward way.

Why Cardio is Important for Strength Building

When we think of cardio, we usually associate it with weight loss or improving cardiovascular health. However, cardio can also help you build strength. By engaging in activities such as running, cycling, or swimming, you can improve your muscular endurance and power, which are both essential for building strength.

Cardio helps enhance your body's ability to use oxygen, which means that your muscles can work for longer periods without getting fatigued. This, in turn, leads to an increase in muscle mass and strength. Additionally, cardio helps improve blood flow and nutrient delivery to your muscles, allowing them to recover faster and grow stronger.

My Personal Experience with Cardio and Strength Building

When I first started my fitness journey, I was solely focused on lifting weights to build strength. However, I soon realized that I was neglecting my cardiovascular health. I decided to incorporate cardio into my routine, and to my surprise, I started seeing improvements in my strength as well.

Running became my go-to cardio activity, and I noticed that it helped me build endurance and power, which translated into better performance during my weightlifting sessions. I was able to lift heavier weights, and my recovery time between sets decreased significantly. Overall, incorporating cardio into my routine has helped me become a stronger and healthier version of myself.

The Best Cardio Activities for Strength Building

Not all cardio activities are created equal when it comes to building strength. Here are some of the best cardio activities you can incorporate into your routine:

Running

Running is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health and build strength in your legs. It engages multiple muscle groups and improves your muscular endurance, which translates into better performance during weightlifting sessions.

Cycling

Cycling is another excellent cardio activity that can help you build strength in your legs, glutes, and core. It is also low-impact, making it a great option for those with joint issues.

Swimming

Swimming is a full-body workout that engages all major muscle groups. It is also low-impact, making it an ideal option for those with joint issues. Swimming can improve your muscular endurance and power, which can help you become stronger overall.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

HIIT involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by periods of rest. It is an effective way to improve your cardiovascular health and build strength. HIIT workouts can be done with bodyweight exercises or with equipment such as kettlebells or dumbbells.

FAQs About How to Increase Strength with Cardio

Q: How often should I do cardio to build strength?

A: It is recommended to do cardio at least three times a week to see improvements in strength. However, it is essential to listen to your body and not overdo it, as recovery is just as important as exercise.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weightlifting?

A: It is generally recommended to do cardio after weightlifting, as lifting weights requires more energy and focus. However, some people prefer to do cardio before weightlifting to warm up their muscles and increase their heart rate.

Q: Can I build strength with low-intensity cardio?

A: While low-intensity cardio can improve your cardiovascular health, it is not as effective as high-intensity cardio when it comes to building strength. However, low-intensity cardio can still be beneficial and can be a good option for those starting on their fitness journey.

Q: Can I build strength with cardio alone?

A: While cardio can help you build strength, it is not the most effective way to do so. Strength training with weights or bodyweight exercises is still the best way to build strength and increase muscle mass.

Conclusion of How to Increase Strength with Cardio

Cardio is an essential component of any fitness routine, and it can help you build strength in addition to improving your cardiovascular health. By incorporating activities such as running, cycling, swimming, or HIIT into your routine, you can improve your muscular endurance and power, leading to better performance during weightlifting sessions. Remember to listen to your body and not overdo it, as recovery is just as important as exercise. With consistency and dedication, you can become a stronger and healthier version of yourself.
