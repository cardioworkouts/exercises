Best High Intensity Exercises A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Aug 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending hours doing low-intensity workouts with little results? Do you want to get in shape quickly and efficiently? Look no further than high intensity exercises. In this beginner's guide, we'll break down the best high intensity exercises and how to incorporate them into your routine.

Many people struggle with finding the right exercises that will give them the results they desire. They may feel unmotivated or unsure of where to start. High intensity exercises can be a great solution, but they can also be intimidating for beginners who are not familiar with them. Additionally, some people may experience physical limitations or injuries that can make certain high intensity exercises difficult.

The target of high intensity exercises is to maximize your heart rate and burn calories in a short amount of time. This creates an "afterburn effect" where your body continues to burn calories even after your workout is over. Some of the best high intensity exercises include: burpees, jump squats, mountain climbers, and plyometric lunges. These exercises can be done with no equipment and in a small space, making them perfect for at-home workouts.

To summarize, high intensity exercises are a great way to get in shape quickly and efficiently. They target your heart rate and burn calories in a short amount of time. Some of the best high intensity exercises include burpees, jump squats, mountain climbers, and plyometric lunges.

Burpees: The Ultimate Full-Body Exercise

Burpees are one of the most effective high intensity exercises as they work almost every muscle in your body. To do a burpee, start in a standing position, drop down into a push-up position, do a push-up, jump your legs back to your hands, and then jump up with your arms in the air. Repeat for a set amount of time or reps.

Personally, I used to hate burpees because they are so challenging. But once I started incorporating them into my routine, I noticed a significant increase in my overall fitness level. I was able to do more reps and felt stronger during my other workouts.

Jump Squats: Building Leg Strength and Cardio

Jump squats are another great high intensity exercise that target your leg muscles and cardio. To do a jump squat, start in a squat position, jump up explosively, and land back in a squat position. Repeat for a set amount of time or reps.

When I first tried jump squats, I struggled to jump high enough and land properly. But with practice, I was able to improve my form and see results in my leg strength and endurance.

Mountain Climbers: Core and Cardio Killer

Mountain climbers are a high intensity exercise that target your core muscles and cardio. To do mountain climbers, start in a plank position, bring one knee towards your chest, and then switch legs quickly. Repeat for a set amount of time or reps.

Mountain climbers are one of my favorite high intensity exercises because they are challenging but also fun. I feel my heart rate increase quickly and my core muscles engage strongly.

Plyometric Lunges: Building Leg Power and Speed

Plyometric lunges are a high intensity exercise that target your leg muscles and help build power and speed. To do plyometric lunges, start in a lunge position, jump up explosively, and switch legs mid-air to land in a lunge position on the other side. Repeat for a set amount of time or reps.

When I first tried plyometric lunges, I struggled with the coordination and balance. But with practice, I was able to improve my form and see results in my leg power and speed.

Question and Answer

Q: Can high intensity exercises be modified for physical limitations or injuries?

A: Yes, many high intensity exercises can be modified to fit individual needs. For example, burpees can be modified with a step instead of a jump, and mountain climbers can be done at a slower pace. It is important to listen to your body and consult with a doctor or trainer if you have any concerns.

Q: How often should I do high intensity exercises?

A: It is recommended to do high intensity exercises 1-3 times per week, depending on your fitness level and goals. It is important to also incorporate rest days and low-intensity workouts to prevent injury and aid in muscle recovery.

Q: Can high intensity exercises help with weight loss?

A: Yes, high intensity exercises can aid in weight loss by burning calories and increasing metabolism. However, it is important to also maintain a healthy diet and lifestyle to see results.

Q: What are some other high intensity exercises I can try?

A: Some other high intensity exercises to try include: burpee variations, jump rope, box jumps, and kettlebell swings.

Conclusion of Best High Intensity Exercises

High intensity exercises are a great way to get in shape quickly and efficiently. They target your heart rate and burn calories in a short amount of time. Some of the best high intensity exercises include burpees, jump squats, mountain climbers, and plyometric lunges. Remember to modify exercises for physical limitations, incorporate rest days, and maintain a healthy lifestyle to see results.
