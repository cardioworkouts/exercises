Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who likes to hit the gym frequently and is always on the lookout to maximize your workout routine? If yes, then one question that might have crossed your mind is whether you should do cardio after strength training or not. This is a common concern among fitness enthusiasts and can be confusing, especially for beginners. In this blog post, we will answer this question and provide you with a comprehensive guide on the subject.

Pain Points Related to Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training

There are several pain points related to the question of doing cardio after strength training. Some of the most common include:

  • Uncertainty about whether doing cardio after strength training is beneficial or not.
  • Fear of losing muscle mass or not building enough strength if cardio is done after strength training.
  • Confusion about the ideal amount of time to spend on cardio after strength training.

Answering the Target of Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training

The answer to whether you should do cardio after strength training is subjective and depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to build strength and gain muscle mass, then it might be better to focus on strength training first and do cardio later. This is because doing cardio before strength training can deplete your energy levels and make it difficult to lift weights optimally. However, if your goal is to burn fat and improve cardiovascular endurance, then doing cardio after strength training might be a good idea.

It's important to note that the order in which you do cardio and strength training doesn't matter as much as the intensity and duration of both workouts. If you're doing high-intensity cardio after strength training, it might have a negative impact on your muscle-building goals. However, if you're doing low-intensity cardio, it might not affect your strength training results at all.

Main Points Related to Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training

To summarize, here are the main points related to the question of doing cardio after strength training:

  • The answer is subjective and depends on your fitness goals.
  • The order doesn't matter as much as the intensity and duration of both workouts.
  • High-intensity cardio after strength training might negatively impact muscle-building goals.
  • Low-intensity cardio might not affect strength training results.

Personal Experience with Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training

As a fitness enthusiast, I have experimented with different workout routines to achieve my fitness goals. In my experience, doing cardio after strength training has helped me burn fat and improve my overall fitness. However, I made sure to keep the cardio intensity low and not overdo it, as I didn't want to negatively impact my strength training results.

How to Do Cardio After Strength Training

If you decide to do cardio after strength training, it's essential to keep a few things in mind:

  • Make sure to cool down and stretch after strength training before starting cardio.
  • Keep the cardio intensity low to avoid muscle depletion and fatigue.
  • Choose low-impact cardio exercises like walking, cycling or swimming.
  • Start with 10-15 minutes of cardio after strength training and gradually increase the duration over time.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Strength Training

There are several benefits of doing cardio after strength training:

  • Burns more calories and helps in weight loss.
  • Improves cardiovascular endurance and overall fitness.
  • Reduces the risk of heart disease and other health problems.
  • Helps in recovery by increasing blood flow to the muscles.

Question and Answer about Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training

Q: Can I do high-intensity cardio after strength training?

A: It's not recommended to do high-intensity cardio after strength training as it can negatively impact muscle-building goals. It's better to focus on low-intensity cardio for optimal results.

Q: How much time should I spend on cardio after strength training?

A: You can start with 10-15 minutes of low-intensity cardio after strength training and gradually increase the duration over time. Aim for 20-30 minutes of cardio after strength training for optimal results.

Q: Should I do cardio every day after strength training?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio every day after strength training. You can aim for 2-3 days of cardio after strength training per week for optimal results.

Q: Can I do strength training and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do strength training and cardio on the same day. However, it's recommended to do strength training first and cardio later to avoid muscle depletion and fatigue.

Conclusion of Should I Do Cardio After Strength Training

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio after strength training depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle mass and strength, it might be better to focus on strength training first and do cardio later. However, if your goal is to burn fat and improve cardiovascular endurance, doing cardio after strength training might be a good idea. Keep in mind that the intensity and duration of both workouts matter more than the order in which you do them.
