Best Fitness Band For Interval Training A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Sep 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for the best fitness band for interval training? Look no further because this guide has got you covered. Interval training is a great way to improve your fitness level and boost your metabolism. However, it can be challenging to keep track of your progress without the right fitness band. In this guide, we will explore the best fitness bands for interval training and how they can help you reach your fitness goals.

Interval training can be intense, and it requires a fitness band that can withstand the sweat and movement. You need a device that can accurately track your heart rate, distance, calories burned, and other essential metrics. With so many fitness bands on the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for your needs.

What is the Best Fitness Band for Interval Training?

The best fitness band for interval training is the one that fits your needs and budget. However, some fitness bands stand out from the rest when it comes to interval training. The top three fitness bands for interval training are:

  • Fitbit Charge 4: This fitness band has built-in GPS, heart rate monitor, and automatic exercise tracking. It also has a long battery life and is water-resistant up to 50 meters.
  • Garmin Vivosmart 4: This fitness band has a pulse oximeter sensor that can measure your oxygen levels. It also has a stress tracking feature and can track your sleep quality.
  • Samsung Galaxy Fit2: This fitness band has a large AMOLED display and can track up to five different exercises. It also has a long battery life and is water-resistant up to 50 meters.

Now that we have discussed the top three fitness bands for interval training let's dive deeper into their features and how they can help you improve your fitness level.

Fitbit Charge 4

I have been using the Fitbit Charge 4 for my interval training, and it has been a game-changer. The built-in GPS allows me to track my runs accurately, and the heart rate monitor ensures that I am staying within my target heart rate zone. The automatic exercise tracking feature saves me time, and I don't have to worry about starting or stopping the activity manually.

The Fitbit Charge 4 also has a feature called Active Zone Minutes, which tracks the time you spend in your target heart rate zone. This feature keeps me motivated to push myself harder during my workouts. The battery life lasts up to seven days, and it's water-resistant up to 50 meters, which means I can wear it while swimming.

Garmin Vivosmart 4

The Garmin Vivosmart 4 is another excellent fitness band for interval training. The pulse oximeter sensor is a unique feature that can measure your oxygen levels during your workouts. This feature can help you determine if you need to adjust your intensity level or take a break. The stress tracking feature can also help you manage your stress levels and improve your overall well-being.

The Garmin Vivosmart 4 also has a long battery life of up to seven days and is water-resistant up to 50 meters. The only downside is that it doesn't have built-in GPS, so you need to bring your phone with you to track your runs accurately.

Samsung Galaxy Fit2

The Samsung Galaxy Fit2 is a budget-friendly fitness band that has many features for interval training. The large AMOLED display makes it easy to read your metrics during your workouts. The fitness band can track up to five different exercises, including outdoor running, indoor cycling, and rowing.

The battery life lasts up to 21 days, and it's water-resistant up to 50 meters. However, it doesn't have a built-in GPS, so you need to bring your phone with you to track your runs accurately.

What Should You Look for in a Fitness Band for Interval Training?

When choosing a fitness band for interval training, there are a few things to keep in mind. Firstly, it should have a heart rate monitor to track your heart rate accurately. Secondly, it should have a built-in GPS to track your runs accurately. Thirdly, it should be water-resistant, so you can wear it while swimming. Fourthly, it should have a long battery life to last through your workouts. Lastly, it should be comfortable to wear and fit your wrist size.

How to Make the Most out of Your Fitness Band for Interval Training?

To get the most out of your fitness band for interval training, you should set goals and track your progress. Set a target heart rate zone and aim to stay within it during your workouts. Use the metrics to adjust your intensity level and push yourself harder. Also, use the automatic exercise tracking feature to save time and focus on your workouts.

Conclusion of Best Fitness Band for Interval Training

Choosing the best fitness band for interval training can be overwhelming, but with this guide, you now have the knowledge to make an informed decision. The Fitbit Charge 4, Garmin Vivosmart 4, and Samsung Galaxy Fit2 are all great options for interval training. Remember to look for a fitness band with a heart rate monitor, built-in GPS, water-resistant, long battery life, and comfortable to wear. Set goals and track your progress to reach your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q1. Is it necessary to have a fitness band for interval training?

A1. No, it's not necessary to have a fitness band for interval training, but it can help you track your progress and stay motivated.

Q2. Can I wear my fitness band while swimming?

A2. It depends on the fitness band. Look for a fitness band that is water-resistant up to 50 meters.

Q3. How often should I charge my fitness band?

A3. It depends on the fitness band. Look for a fitness band with a long battery life that can last through your workouts.

Q4. Can I use my fitness band for other activities besides interval training?

A4. Yes, most fitness bands can track various exercises, including running, cycling, and swimming.
