Basic Workouts Without Equipment A Beginner s Guide To Getting Fit Anywhere

Written by Bardi Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of feeling sluggish and out of shape? Do you want to start exercising but don't have access to a gym or equipment? Basic workouts without equipment can be the solution you're looking for.

Many people struggle with finding the time, money, or resources to start a fitness routine. Basic workouts without equipment can help you overcome these obstacles and get your body moving.

In this article, we'll cover the basics of basic workouts without equipment, including their target, benefits, and examples. By the end of this guide, you'll have all the information you need to start your fitness journey without stepping foot in a gym.

Basic workouts without equipment can be done anywhere, at any time, and by anyone. They are designed to use your body weight as resistance, making them a low-cost and effective way to get fit.

Some examples of basic workouts without equipment include push-ups, squats, lunges, planks, and jumping jacks. These exercises target different muscle groups and can be modified to fit your fitness level.

The Benefits of Basic Workouts Without Equipment

When you start a fitness routine, it's important to set yourself up for success. Basic workouts without equipment offer several benefits that can help you achieve your fitness goals:

Flexibility: Basic workouts without equipment can be done anywhere, making them a flexible option for people with busy schedules or limited resources.

Variety: With so many exercises to choose from, you can easily switch up your routine to prevent boredom and challenge your muscles.

Cost-Effective: Basic workouts without equipment require no gym membership or expensive equipment, making them a budget-friendly option for anyone.

Push-Ups

Push-ups are a classic exercise that target your chest, triceps, and shoulders. To perform a push-up, start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest touches the ground, then push back up to the starting position.

When you first start doing push-ups, you may need to modify the exercise by dropping to your knees or doing them against a wall. As you get stronger, you can increase the number of reps or the difficulty of the exercise.

Squats

Squats are a lower body exercise that target your glutes, hamstrings, and quads. To perform a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing forward. Lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground, then push back up to the starting position.

When you first start doing squats, you may need to modify the exercise by using a chair or wall for support. As you get stronger, you can increase the number of reps or the difficulty of the exercise.

Lunges

Lunges are a lower body exercise that target your glutes, hamstrings, and quads. To perform a lunge, start with your feet hip-width apart. Step forward with one foot, bending your front knee to a 90-degree angle. Your back knee should hover just above the ground. Push back up to the starting position and repeat on the other side.

When you first start doing lunges, you may need to modify the exercise by using a chair or wall for support. As you get stronger, you can increase the number of reps or the difficulty of the exercise.

Planks

Planks are a core exercise that target your abs, back, and shoulders. To perform a plank, start in a push-up position. Lower your forearms to the ground, keeping your elbows directly under your shoulders. Your body should form a straight line from your head to your heels. Hold for as long as you can.

When you first start doing planks, you may need to modify the exercise by holding for a shorter amount of time or doing them on your knees. As you get stronger, you can increase the duration of the exercise.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need any equipment for basic workouts without equipment?

A: No, basic workouts without equipment use your body weight as resistance, so no equipment is needed.

Q: Can I do basic workouts without equipment if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, basic workouts without equipment can be modified to fit any fitness level.

Q: How often should I do basic workouts without equipment?

A: It's recommended to do at least 30 minutes of exercise per day, so aim for a few days a week with basic workouts without equipment.

Q: Are basic workouts without equipment effective for weight loss?

A: Yes, any form of exercise can help with weight loss when combined with a healthy diet.

Conclusion of Basic Workouts Without Equipment

Basic workouts without equipment offer a low-cost and flexible option for anyone looking to get fit. With a variety of exercises to choose from, you can easily switch up your routine and challenge your muscles. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced athlete, basic workouts without equipment can help you achieve your fitness goals.
