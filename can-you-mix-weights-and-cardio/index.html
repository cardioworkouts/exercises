Can You Mix Weights And Cardio The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you someone who loves working out but is unsure whether it's okay to mix weights and cardio in your routine? If so, you're not alone. Many fitness enthusiasts have a similar question, and it's time to put the debate to rest.

Pain Points of Mixing Weights and Cardio

Most people have a few concerns when it comes to mixing weights and cardio. The most common ones are:

  • Will it hurt my gains?
  • Will it decrease my endurance?
  • Will it take longer to see results?

These are valid concerns, but the good news is that mixing weights and cardio won't affect your gains or endurance, and it can actually lead to better results.

Can You Mix Weights and Cardio?

The short answer is yes, you can mix weights and cardio. In fact, it's recommended to do so because it provides a full-body workout and helps you burn more calories. Both weights and cardio have their benefits, and combining them can lead to a well-rounded fitness routine.

Benefits of Mixing Weights and Cardio

Mixing weights and cardio has numerous benefits, including:

  • Better cardiovascular health
  • Improved muscle strength and endurance
  • Increased calorie burn
  • Reduced risk of injury
  • Improved overall fitness

My Personal Experience

As a personal trainer, I always recommend my clients to mix weights and cardio for optimal results. In my experience, it has helped people lose weight, gain muscle, and improve their overall fitness. Personally, I mix weights and cardio in my routine and have seen great results.

How to Mix Weights and Cardio?

There are many ways to mix weights and cardio, and it depends on your fitness goals. Here are a few ways to get started:

  • Do a cardiovascular warm-up before lifting weights
  • Alternate between weight lifting and cardio exercises during your workout
  • Do a high-intensity interval training (HIIT) workout that combines both weights and cardio
  • Do a circuit training workout that combines both weights and cardio

FAQs

Q. Can I do weights and cardio on the same day?

A. Yes, you can do weights and cardio on the same day. In fact, it's recommended to do so to get the most benefits out of your workout.

Q. How much cardio should I do if I'm lifting weights?

A. It depends on your fitness goals, but a general recommendation is to do 20-30 minutes of cardio after your weight lifting session.

Q. Is it better to do cardio or weights first?

A. It's recommended to do cardio first to warm up your body and get your heart rate up. However, it ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preference.

Q. Will mixing weights and cardio bulk me up?

A. No, mixing weights and cardio won't bulk you up unless you're eating a high-calorie diet and intentionally trying to gain muscle mass.

Conclusion of Can You Mix Weights and Cardio

Mixing weights and cardio is not only okay but also recommended for a well-rounded fitness routine. It can help you achieve your fitness goals faster and improve your overall health. So, don't be afraid to mix things up and enjoy the benefits of a full-body workout.
