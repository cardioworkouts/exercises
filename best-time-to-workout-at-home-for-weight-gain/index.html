The Best Time To Workout At Home For Weight Gain

Written by Christine Jun 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to gain weight and looking for the best time to workout at home? Do you feel confused about the best time of day to exercise for maximum weight gain? If so, you're not alone. Many people face this same challenge, but don't worry, we've got you covered.

The best time to workout at home for weight gain is in the morning, preferably between 6 am to 10 am. This is because your body is in a fasting state after a long night's sleep, and your glycogen stores are depleted. When you exercise in the morning, your body will burn more fat to fuel your workout, which can lead to greater weight gain over time.

In summary, the best time to workout at home for weight gain is in the morning. This is because your body is in a fasting state, and you'll burn more fat to fuel your workout. Additionally, exercising in the morning helps you kickstart your metabolism and energizes you for the day ahead.

The Benefits of Morning Workouts for Weight Gain

Personally, I've found that working out in the morning is the best time for me to gain weight. When I exercise in the morning, I feel more energized and productive throughout the day. Plus, I've noticed that my appetite increases after a morning workout, which helps me consume more calories to support my weight gain goals.

Research also supports the benefits of morning workouts for weight gain. In a study published in the Journal of Physiology, researchers found that exercising in the morning leads to greater fat loss and increases in muscle mass compared to working out in the evening.

How to Optimize Your Morning Workouts for Weight Gain

If you want to get the most out of your morning workouts for weight gain, there are a few things you can do to optimize your routine:

Eat a Protein-Rich Breakfast Before Your Workout

Before your morning workout, make sure to eat a protein-rich breakfast. This will help fuel your workout and provide your body with the nutrients it needs to build muscle. Good breakfast options include eggs, Greek yogurt, oats, and protein shakes.

Do Compound Exercises

During your morning workout, focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once. This will help you build more muscle mass and burn more fat. Good compound exercises include squats, deadlifts, bench press, and pull-ups.

What About Evening Workouts?

You may be wondering if evening workouts are still effective for weight gain. While morning workouts are generally recommended, you can still gain weight by working out in the evening. The key is to make sure you're consuming enough calories throughout the day to support your weight gain goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I workout on an empty stomach in the morning?

A: It's generally not recommended to workout on an empty stomach. Try to eat a small snack before your morning workout, such as a banana or a handful of almonds, to give your body the energy it needs to perform at its best.

Q: How long should my morning workout be?

A: Your morning workout should be around 45-60 minutes long. This is enough time to get a good workout in without overtaxing your body.

Q: Can I do cardio in the morning for weight gain?

A: While cardio is important for overall health, it's not the best type of exercise for weight gain. Instead, focus on strength training exercises that build muscle mass.

Q: What should I eat after my morning workout?

A: After your morning workout, make sure to eat a protein-rich meal to help your muscles recover and grow. Good options include chicken, fish, tofu, and quinoa.

Conclusion of Best Time to Workout at Home for Weight Gain

In conclusion, the best time to workout at home for weight gain is in the morning, between 6 am to 10 am. This is because your body is in a fasting state, and you'll burn more fat to fuel your workout. Additionally, morning workouts have been shown to lead to greater fat loss and muscle gain compared to evening workouts. Remember to eat a protein-rich breakfast before your workout, focus on compound exercises, and consume enough calories throughout the day to support your weight gain goals.
