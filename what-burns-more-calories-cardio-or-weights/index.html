What Burns More Calories Cardio Or Weights

Written by Christine Nov 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to shed some extra pounds, but not sure if you should focus on cardio or weights? The debate over what burns more calories, cardio or weights, has been ongoing for years, but the answer may not be as straightforward as you think. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of both cardio and weight training and which one may be more effective in burning calories.

Many people struggle with weight loss and are often looking for ways to maximize their calorie burn during exercise. Whether you're trying to shed a few pounds or maintain a healthy weight, knowing which type of exercise is most effective can make a big difference in your results.

So, what burns more calories: cardio or weights? The answer is not so simple. While cardio exercises, such as running and cycling, are known for their ability to burn calories quickly, weight training can also be an effective way to shed pounds.

Both cardio and weight training can help you burn calories, but in different ways. Cardio exercises work to increase your heart rate and get your blood pumping, which can lead to a high calorie burn during and after your workout. Weight training, on the other hand, helps to build lean muscle mass, which can increase your metabolism and help you burn more calories even when you're at rest.

Personal Experience with Cardio and Weight Training

Personally, I have found that a combination of cardio and weight training is the most effective way for me to burn calories and maintain a healthy weight. I typically start my workout with 30 minutes of cardio, such as running or cycling, to get my heart rate up and burn calories quickly. Then, I move on to weight training, focusing on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, such as squats and deadlifts. This helps me to build lean muscle mass while still burning calories.

The Benefits of Cardio for Calorie Burn

Cardio exercises are known for their ability to burn calories quickly. This is because these exercises work to increase your heart rate and get your blood pumping, which can lead to a high calorie burn during and after your workout. In fact, a 30-minute run can burn anywhere from 200-400 calories depending on your weight and intensity level.

Cardio exercises are also great for improving your cardiovascular health and increasing your endurance. Regular cardio workouts can help to lower your risk of heart disease, stroke, and other chronic illnesses.

How to Maximize Your Calorie Burn During Cardio

If you want to maximize your calorie burn during cardio, there are a few things you can do. First, increase the intensity of your workout by adding intervals or incline to your run or cycling route. This will help to boost your heart rate and calorie burn. Second, try to incorporate a variety of cardio exercises into your routine, such as running, cycling, and swimming, to keep your body challenged and prevent boredom.

The Benefits of Weight Training for Calorie Burn

While cardio exercises are great for burning calories quickly, weight training can also be an effective way to shed pounds. This is because weight training helps to build lean muscle mass, which can increase your metabolism and help you burn more calories even when you're at rest.

Weight training is also great for improving your overall strength and muscle tone. Regular weight training workouts can help to increase bone density, reduce your risk of injury, and improve your posture.

Personal Experience with Weight Training

I have found that weight training has been essential in helping me build lean muscle mass and improve my overall strength. I typically focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press. This helps me to build muscle while still burning calories.

Cardio vs. Weight Training: Which is Better for Calorie Burn?

The Verdict

So, which is better for calorie burn: cardio or weight training? The answer is that it depends on your goals and preferences. If you're looking to burn calories quickly, cardio exercises such as running, cycling, and swimming may be more effective. However, if you're looking to build lean muscle mass and increase your metabolism, weight training may be the better option.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you lose weight without cardio?

A: Yes, you can still lose weight without cardio. Weight training can be an effective way to shed pounds by building lean muscle mass and increasing your metabolism.

Q: How many calories does weight training burn?

A: The number of calories burned during weight training varies depending on your weight, intensity level, and the exercises you're performing. On average, weight training can burn anywhere from 100-300 calories per hour.

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weights first?

A: It's generally recommended to do cardio first, as it can help to warm up your muscles and get your heart rate up before moving on to weight training.

Q: How often should I do cardio and weight training?

A: It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week, along with at least two days of weight training per week.

Conclusion of what burns more calories cardio or weights

In conclusion, both cardio and weight training can be effective ways to burn calories and achieve your weight loss goals. The key is to find a balance between the two that works for you and your lifestyle. Incorporating a variety of cardio exercises and compound weight training exercises into your routine can help you maximize your calorie burn and achieve the results you're looking for.
