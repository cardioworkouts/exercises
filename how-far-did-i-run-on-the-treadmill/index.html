How Far Did I Run On The Treadmill

Written by Frank Jun 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering how far you've run on the treadmill? Maybe you're trying to track your progress or just curious about your workout. Whatever your reason, figuring out your distance can be a bit tricky. In this post, we'll explore how to determine how far you've run on the treadmill and provide some helpful tips along the way.

The first pain point is trying to figure out how to calculate your distance when running on a treadmill. Many people assume that the distance on the treadmill screen is accurate, but this is not always the case. Another pain point is trying to keep track of your distance manually, which can be tedious and time-consuming.

So, how far did you actually run on the treadmill? The answer depends on a few factors. First, you need to determine if your treadmill is calibrated correctly. This can be done by comparing the distance on the treadmill screen to the distance you run using a GPS watch or phone app. If there is a discrepancy, you may need to adjust the calibration of your treadmill.

Another factor to consider is your stride length. This can vary depending on your height and running style. To calculate your stride length, measure the distance between the heel prints of one foot to the heel prints of the other foot when you take a normal step. Multiply this measurement by two to get your stride length. Then, multiply your stride length by the number of steps you take per mile to determine your distance.

In summary, determining how far you've run on the treadmill requires a bit of math and calibration. It's important to make sure your treadmill is accurate and take into account your stride length.

My Personal Experience

When I first started running on the treadmill, I assumed that the distance on the screen was accurate. However, I soon realized that this was not the case. I compared the distance on the treadmill screen to the distance on my GPS watch and found that the treadmill was overestimating my distance by about 0.2 miles per mile. After adjusting the calibration of my treadmill, I was able to get a more accurate distance reading.

Helpful Tips

One helpful tip is to use a GPS watch or phone app to track your distance. This can provide a more accurate reading than relying solely on the treadmill screen. Another tip is to make sure your treadmill is on a level surface and not near any walls or objects that could interfere with the calibration. Finally, consider investing in a foot pod or other device that can measure your stride length and help you track your distance more accurately.

Calibrating Your Treadmill

To calibrate your treadmill, start by setting the speed to 6 mph and walking on the treadmill for 5 minutes. Then, measure the distance you've walked using a GPS watch or phone app. Compare this distance to the distance on the treadmill screen. If there is a discrepancy, adjust the calibration of your treadmill until the two distances match.

Tracking Your Distance

One way to track your distance is to count your steps per mile. To do this, run on the treadmill for exactly one mile while counting your steps. Then, divide the number of steps by 5280 (the number of feet in a mile) to determine your steps per mile. Multiply this number by your stride length to determine your distance.

Conclusion

Determining how far you've run on the treadmill requires a bit of math and calibration, but it's worth it to track your progress and reach your fitness goals. By following these tips, you can get a more accurate reading and make the most of your workout.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I trust the distance on the treadmill screen?
A: Not always. It's important to calibrate your treadmill to ensure accuracy.

Q: How do I calculate my stride length?
A: Measure the distance between the heel prints of one foot to the heel prints of the other foot when you take a normal step. Multiply this measurement by two to get your stride length.

Q: Can a foot pod help me track my distance more accurately?
A: Yes, a foot pod can measure your stride length and help you track your distance more accurately.

Q: Is it worth the effort to track my distance on the treadmill?
A: Yes, tracking your distance can help you reach your fitness goals and make the most of your workout.
