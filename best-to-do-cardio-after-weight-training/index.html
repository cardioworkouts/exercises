Best Way To Do Cardio After Weight Training A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Nov 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking to maximize the benefits of your workout routine by combining weight training with cardio? If so, you may be wondering what the best way to do cardio after weight training is. In this guide, we'll explore the benefits of combining these two types of exercise and provide tips for optimizing your workout for maximum results.

Understanding the Benefits of Cardio After Weight Training

Before we dive into the specifics of how to do cardio after weight training, it's important to understand why this combination is so effective. Weight training builds muscle, which is essential for increasing metabolism and burning calories even when you're not working out. Adding cardio to your routine can help you burn even more calories, improve cardiovascular health, and boost endurance.

However, it's important to approach cardio after weight training strategically in order to avoid overtraining, injury, or diminishing returns. Here are some tips for optimizing your routine:

Tip #1: Start with a Warm-Up

Before diving into your weight training routine, start with a 5-10 minute cardio warm-up such as jogging or cycling. This will get your blood flowing and prepare your body for the workout to come.

Tip #2: Focus on Intensity Over Duration

When it comes to cardio after weight training, short bursts of high-intensity exercise are more effective than long, steady-state cardio sessions. Aim for 20-30 minutes of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) rather than an hour of moderate-intensity cardio.

Tip #3: Choose the Right Type of Cardio

The best type of cardio after weight training is one that complements your weight training routine. For example, if you're working on building lower body strength, focus on cardio exercises that work the same muscle groups, such as jump squats or box jumps.

Tip #4: Listen to Your Body

It's important to pay attention to your body and adjust your routine as needed. If you're feeling fatigued or sore, take a break or reduce the intensity of your cardio workout. Overtraining can lead to injury and diminished results.

My Personal Experience

As a fitness enthusiast, I have experimented with various workout routines over the years. When I first started incorporating cardio after weight training, I noticed a significant increase in my endurance and overall fitness level. However, I also experienced some setbacks due to overtraining and not listening to my body. Through trial and error, I've learned to approach cardio after weight training strategically and have seen great results.

FAQs About Cardio After Weight Training

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after weight training?

A: It's generally recommended to wait at least 30 minutes before starting your cardio session. This allows your muscles to recover and reduces the risk of injury.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's best to do weight training first, as this will help you build muscle and increase metabolism. Cardio after weight training can help you burn even more calories and improve cardiovascular health.

Q: How often should I do cardio after weight training?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and overall workout routine. It's generally recommended to incorporate cardio after weight training 2-3 times per week.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on rest days, but be sure to listen to your body and avoid overtraining.

Conclusion of Best Way to Do Cardio After Weight Training

When done strategically, cardio after weight training can help you achieve your fitness goals and improve overall health. Remember to start with a warm-up, focus on intensity over duration, choose the right type of cardio, and listen to your body. By following these tips and incorporating cardio after weight training into your routine, you can maximize the benefits of your workout and achieve optimal results.
