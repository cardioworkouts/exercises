Is It Better To Do Cardio Or Weights First For Fat Loss

Written by Luffy Oct 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you trying to lose weight but unsure whether you should start with cardio or weights? This topic has been debated among fitness enthusiasts for years, and it can be confusing to know which one is better for fat loss. In this post, we will explore both options and provide you with an answer to this age-old question.

Pain Points

Many people struggle with losing weight, and the process can be frustrating. It can be challenging to know what exercises to do to achieve your weight loss goals. Moreover, you may be unsure of the right order in which to do them to maximize your weight loss. This confusion can make it difficult to stick to a consistent exercise routine.

Is it Better to Do Cardio or Weights First for Fat Loss?

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is weight loss, it is best to start with cardio first. Cardio exercises, such as running, cycling, or swimming, get your heart rate up and help you burn calories. Doing cardio first will help you burn more calories during your workout, which can lead to weight loss. Additionally, cardio exercises increase your endurance, which can help you perform better during your weightlifting exercises.

However, if your goal is to build muscle and increase strength, it is best to start with weightlifting first. Weightlifting exercises such as squats, bench presses, and deadlifts help build muscle, which increases your metabolism. This means that you will continue to burn calories even after your workout is complete. Moreover, starting with weightlifting exercises will help you build strength, which can help you perform better during your cardio exercises.

Main Points

In summary, if your primary goal is fat loss, it is best to start with cardio first. Doing cardio exercises first will help you burn more calories during your workout and increase your endurance for weightlifting exercises. However, if you want to build muscle and increase strength, starting with weightlifting exercises first is best. Weightlifting exercises help build muscle, which increases your metabolism, and improves your performance during cardio exercises.

Personal Experience with Cardio and Weights

When I first started my weight loss journey, I was unsure of the best way to approach my exercise routine. I tried doing weightlifting first, but I found that I did not have enough energy to complete my cardio exercises. However, when I started with cardio exercises first, I found that I had more energy and could perform better during my weightlifting exercises. I was also able to burn more calories during my workout, which helped me reach my weight loss goals faster.

How to Incorporate Cardio and Weights in Your Workout Routine

If you are unsure of the best way to incorporate cardio and weightlifting exercises into your workout routine, try alternating between the two. For example, you can start with cardio exercises on Monday and Wednesday and weightlifting exercises on Tuesday and Thursday. You can also mix the two exercises in one workout, such as doing a circuit of weightlifting exercises followed by a cardio exercise.

Benefits of Cardio and Weightlifting Exercises

Both cardio and weightlifting exercises offer various benefits for your health. Cardio exercises help improve your cardiovascular health, increase your endurance, and reduce your risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and diabetes. Weightlifting exercises, on the other hand, help build muscle, increase your metabolism, and improve your bone density.

How to Get the Most Out of Your Cardio and Weightlifting Exercises

To get the most out of your cardio and weightlifting exercises, it is essential to challenge yourself. This means increasing the intensity, duration, or weight of your exercises over time. Additionally, it is crucial to have a balanced diet to support your weight loss or muscle-building goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting exercises in the same workout?

A: Yes, you can mix cardio and weightlifting exercises in the same workout. This is called a circuit workout, and it can be an effective way to burn more calories and build muscle at the same time.

Q: How long should I do cardio and weightlifting exercises?

A: The duration of your cardio and weightlifting exercises depends on your fitness level and goals. It is recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio exercise per week. For weightlifting exercises, it is recommended to do at least two days per week, targeting all major muscle groups.

Q: What is the best time to do cardio and weightlifting exercises?

A: The best time to do your cardio and weightlifting exercises is when it works best for you. Some people prefer to do their exercises in the morning, while others prefer to do them in the evening. It is essential to find a time that fits your schedule and stick to it consistently.

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting exercises every day?

A: It is recommended to have at least one rest day per week to allow your muscles to recover. However, you can do cardio and weightlifting exercises on the same day as long as you give your muscles time to recover.

Conclusion of Is it Better to Do Cardio or Weights First for Fat Loss

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio or weightlifting exercises first depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is fat loss, it is best to start with cardio exercises first. However, if you want to build muscle and increase strength, starting with weightlifting exercises first is best. It is essential to have a balanced exercise routine that includes both cardio and weightlifting exercises to achieve optimal health and fitness.
