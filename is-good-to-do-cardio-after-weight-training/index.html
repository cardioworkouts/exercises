Why Is It Good To Do Cardio After Weight Training

Written by Eveline Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym and work up a sweat? If you're already incorporating weight training into your workout routine, you may be wondering if you should also be doing cardio after weight training. While it may seem counterintuitive to do cardio after a strenuous weightlifting session, it can actually have many benefits for your body.

Many people struggle with finding the right balance between weight training and cardio. Some may even avoid cardio altogether, while others may overdo it and neglect weight training. However, incorporating both into your routine can have a positive impact on your overall health and fitness goals.

So, why is it good to do cardio after weight training? Simply put, it helps your body burn more calories and fat. When you do weight training, your body relies on stored glycogen for energy. However, when you do cardio after weight training, your body switches to burning fat for energy, which can help you achieve your weight loss goals.

To summarize, here are some of the main benefits of doing cardio after weight training:

The Cardio After Weight Training Target

Personally, I found that doing cardio after weight training really helped me with my weight loss goals. I used to do 30 minutes of cardio before weightlifting, but I switched it up and started doing 30 minutes of cardio after weightlifting instead. Not only did I notice a significant increase in my energy levels during my workouts, but I also saw better results in terms of fat loss.

The Importance of Cardio After Weight Training

Another benefit of doing cardio after weight training is that it can improve your cardiovascular health. By raising your heart rate and engaging in cardiovascular exercise, you can strengthen your heart and lungs, reduce your risk of heart disease, and improve your overall fitness level.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Training

If you're new to incorporating cardio after weight training, it's important to start slow and gradually build up your endurance. Start with 10-15 minutes of low-intensity cardio, such as walking or cycling, and gradually increase your time and intensity as your fitness level improves. You can also mix up your cardio routine by trying different forms of exercise, such as running, swimming, or dancing.

Tips for Success with Cardio After Weight Training

One important thing to keep in mind when doing cardio after weight training is to stay hydrated. Be sure to drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to avoid dehydration. You should also fuel your body with healthy foods, such as protein and complex carbohydrates, to help your muscles recover and refuel.

My Experience with Cardio After Weight Training

Personally, I've found that doing cardio after weight training has helped me see better results in terms of fat loss and overall fitness. I typically do 30-45 minutes of low-intensity cardio after my weightlifting session, and I've noticed a significant increase in my energy levels and endurance. It's also a great way to clear my mind and de-stress after a tough workout.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it okay to do cardio before weight training?

A: Yes, it's okay to do cardio before weight training. However, it's important to start with low-intensity cardio and gradually increase your intensity to avoid burning out before your weight training session.

Q: How often should I do cardio after weight training?

A: It's best to do cardio after weight training at least 2-3 times a week, but you can do it more frequently if your body can handle it.

Q: Can I do high-intensity cardio after weight training?

A: Yes, you can do high-intensity cardio after weight training, but it's important to listen to your body and not overdo it. Start with low-intensity cardio and gradually increase your intensity as your fitness level improves.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after weight training?

A: It's best to wait at least 30 minutes after weight training to do cardio, to allow your body time to recover and refuel.

Conclusion of Is Good to Do Cardio After Weight Training

Incorporating cardio after weight training can have many benefits for your body, including increased calorie and fat burning, improved cardiovascular health, and overall fitness. However, it's important to start slow and gradually build up your endurance, and to listen to your body to avoid overexertion. By finding the right balance between weight training and cardio, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being.
