Workouts That Combine Cardio And Strength A Beginner s Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a workout that can help you burn fat, build muscles, and boost your cardiovascular endurance? If so, then workouts that combine cardio and strength may be the perfect solution for you. By combining both types of exercises, you can get the best of both worlds and achieve your fitness goals faster.

However, many people are intimidated by the idea of combining cardio and strength exercises in one workout. They worry that it might be too challenging or that they might not be able to keep up. But the truth is that with the right approach, anyone can benefit from this type of workout.

In this article, we'll explain what workouts that combine cardio and strength are, what their benefits are, and how you can get started with them. We'll also provide you with some examples of exercises that you can include in your workouts.

So, let's get started!

Cardio and Strength Workouts: What Are They?

Cardio and strength workouts are a type of exercise that combines cardiovascular exercises (such as running, cycling, or jumping jacks) with strength-training exercises (such as push-ups, squats, or lunges). The goal of this type of workout is to challenge your body in multiple ways, by improving your cardiovascular health while also building muscle strength and endurance.

Here's an example of a cardio and strength workout:

Warm-up: 5 minutes of jogging or jumping jacks

Cardio: 20 minutes of cycling or running

Strength: 3 sets of 10 push-ups, 3 sets of 10 squats, and 3 sets of 10 lunges

Cooldown: 5 minutes of stretching

As you can see, this workout combines both types of exercises in one session, allowing you to work on your cardiovascular endurance and your strength at the same time.

The Benefits of Cardio and Strength Workouts

There are many benefits to incorporating cardio and strength exercises into your workout routine. Here are just a few:

  • Improved cardiovascular health: Cardio exercises help to strengthen your heart and lungs, reducing your risk of heart disease and other cardiovascular conditions.
  • Increased muscle strength: Strength exercises help to build and tone your muscles, making you stronger and more resilient.
  • Weight loss: Combining cardio and strength exercises can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster than doing either type of exercise alone.
  • Better endurance: Cardio exercises can help to improve your endurance, making it easier for you to perform everyday activities and sports.

Cardio and Strength Workouts: Exercise Examples

Here are some examples of exercises that you can include in your cardio and strength workouts:

1. Burpees

Burpees are a full-body exercise that combines cardio and strength training. To perform a burpee, start in a standing position, then drop down into a squat position and place your hands on the ground. Kick your feet back into a plank position, then jump your feet forward and stand up. Repeat for 10-15 reps.

2. Jump Rope

Jumping rope is a great cardio exercise that also works your arms and legs. Try jumping rope for 1-2 minutes at a time, then taking a short break before starting again.

3. Push-ups

Push-ups are a classic strength exercise that work your chest, arms, and core. Try doing 3 sets of 10-15 push-ups, taking short breaks in between each set.

4. Squats

Squats are a lower-body strength exercise that work your glutes, quads, and hamstrings. Try doing 3 sets of 10-15 squats, taking short breaks in between each set.

5. Lunges

Lunges are another lower-body strength exercise that work your glutes, quads, and hamstrings. Try doing 3 sets of 10-15 lunges on each leg, taking short breaks in between each set.

Cardio and Strength Workouts: Tips for Beginners

If you're new to cardio and strength workouts, here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start slow: Don't try to do too much too soon. Start with a few basic exercises and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.
  • Use proper form: Make sure you're using proper form for each exercise to avoid injury and get the most out of your workout.
  • Take breaks: It's okay to take short breaks in between exercises or sets if you need to catch your breath or rest your muscles.
  • Stay hydrated: Make sure you drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated.

Question and Answer

Q:

What are some other examples of cardio and strength exercises?

A:

Other examples include jumping jacks, mountain climbers, kettlebell swings, and bench presses.

Q:

How often should I do cardio and strength workouts?

A:

It's recommended to do cardio and strength workouts at least three times a week, with rest days in between.

Q:

Can I do cardio and strength exercises on the same day?

A:

Yes, you can do cardio and strength exercises on the same day. Just make sure you warm up properly and don't overdo it.

Q:

Do I need any special equipment for cardio and strength workouts?

A:

No, you don't need any special equipment. You can do many cardio and strength exercises with just your bodyweight. However, some exercises may require dumbbells or resistance bands.

Conclusion of Workouts That Combine Cardio and Strength

Workouts that combine cardio and strength exercises are a great way to challenge your body and achieve your fitness goals faster. By incorporating both types of exercises into your routine, you can improve your cardiovascular health, build muscle strength, and burn fat. Remember to start slow, use proper form, and stay hydrated. With consistency and dedication, you'll see progress in no time!
