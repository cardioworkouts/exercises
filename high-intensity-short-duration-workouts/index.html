High Intensity Short Duration Workouts A Quick Guide

Written by Christine Jun 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym with little results to show for it? Or maybe you simply don't have the time to commit to long, drawn-out workouts? If so, high intensity short duration workouts may be just what you need to achieve your fitness goals. In this guide, we'll explore the benefits of this type of training and give you tips on how to incorporate it into your routine.

The Pain Points of Traditional Workouts

For many people, the thought of spending hours at the gym each week is daunting. Between work, family obligations, and other commitments, finding the time to exercise can be a challenge. Even those who make the effort to hit the gym regularly may find that their efforts don't translate into the results they're looking for. This can be frustrating and demotivating, leading many to give up on their fitness goals altogether.

What Are High Intensity Short Duration Workouts?

High intensity short duration workouts, also known as HIIT, are a type of exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. The goal is to get your heart rate up quickly and keep it there for a short period of time, typically less than 30 minutes. These workouts can be done with a variety of exercises, including bodyweight movements, weightlifting, and cardio activities like running or cycling.

The benefits of HIIT are numerous. Not only does it burn more calories in less time than traditional workouts, but it also increases your metabolism and improves cardiovascular health. Plus, because the workouts are so short, they're a great option for those who are short on time.

How to Incorporate High Intensity Short Duration Workouts

If you're new to HIIT, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. Begin with short intervals of high-intensity exercise followed by longer periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. As you become more comfortable with the movements and your fitness level improves, you can increase the duration and intensity of your workouts.

One great way to incorporate HIIT into your routine is to alternate it with more traditional workouts. For example, you might do a HIIT workout on Monday, weightlifting on Wednesday, and cardio on Friday. This allows you to get the benefits of both types of exercise while keeping your routine interesting and varied.

The Science Behind High Intensity Short Duration Workouts

HIIT has been shown to be an effective way to improve cardiovascular health, burn fat, and increase metabolism. This type of training can also improve insulin sensitivity and reduce the risk of chronic diseases like diabetes and heart disease.

One study found that just one minute of high-intensity exercise was as effective at improving health and fitness as 45 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise. This means that even short, intense workouts can have a big impact on your overall health and well-being.

Tips for Getting Started with HIIT

If you're new to HIIT, it's important to start slowly and build up your fitness level gradually. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

  • Choose exercises that you enjoy and that you're comfortable performing
  • Start with short intervals of high-intensity exercise followed by longer periods of rest or low-intensity exercise
  • Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts as your fitness level improves
  • Be sure to warm up properly before beginning your workout to prevent injury
  • Stay hydrated and listen to your body - if you feel pain or discomfort, stop and rest

Personal Experience with High Intensity Short Duration Workouts

As someone who struggles to find the time to exercise regularly, HIIT has been a game changer for me. I love being able to get in a quick, intense workout that leaves me feeling energized and accomplished. Plus, the variety of exercises and movements keeps things interesting and challenging.

One of my favorite HIIT workouts involves a combination of bodyweight movements like squats and lunges, followed by short bursts of cardio like jumping jacks or high knees. I'll do each movement for 30 seconds, followed by 10 seconds of rest, and repeat for a total of 4-5 rounds. It's a quick and effective way to get my heart rate up and burn some calories.

Questions and Answers

Q: Can anyone do high intensity short duration workouts?

A: While HIIT can be a great option for many people, it's important to consult with a doctor or fitness professional before starting any new exercise routine. Those with certain medical conditions or injuries may need to modify their workouts or avoid certain movements altogether.

Q: How often should I do HIIT workouts?

A: This will depend on your fitness level and goals, but most people find that 2-3 HIIT workouts per week is sufficient. It's important to give your body time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: Do I need any special equipment for HIIT?

A: No, HIIT can be done with just your bodyweight or simple equipment like dumbbells or resistance bands. However, some people choose to use equipment like rowing machines or stationary bikes to add variety to their workouts.

Q: How long should a typical HIIT workout be?

A: HIIT workouts can vary in length, but most are less than 30 minutes. The goal is to keep your heart rate up for a short period of time, so the focus is on intensity rather than duration.

Conclusion of High Intensity Short Duration Workouts

High intensity short duration workouts are a great option for those who are short on time or looking to switch up their routine. With the potential to burn more calories, increase metabolism, and improve cardiovascular health, HIIT is a smart choice for anyone looking to improve their fitness level. By starting slowly and building up gradually, you can reap the benefits of this type of training and achieve your fitness goals in less time than you ever thought possible.
