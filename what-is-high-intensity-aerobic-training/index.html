What Is High Intensity Aerobic Training

Written by Eveline Aug 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of the same old workouts that seem to drag on forever with little to no results? Do you want a more efficient way to burn calories and improve your overall fitness? If so, high intensity aerobic training may be just what you need.

High intensity aerobic training, also known as HIIT, is a form of exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or lower intensity activity. This type of training can be done with a variety of aerobic exercises, such as running, cycling, or jumping rope.

The goal of high intensity aerobic training is to push your body to its limits for short periods of time, allowing you to burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular fitness in less time than traditional steady-state cardio workouts.

In this article, we will explore what high intensity aerobic training is, how it works, and the benefits it can provide for your health and fitness goals.

What is High Intensity Aerobic Training?

High intensity aerobic training is a type of exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or lower intensity activity. This type of training can be done with a variety of aerobic exercises, such as running, cycling, or jumping rope.

During high intensity aerobic training, your heart rate and breathing rate will increase significantly, and you may feel like you're pushing yourself to your limits. However, the rest periods in between the intense activity allow your body to recover and prepare for the next burst of activity.

One of the main benefits of high intensity aerobic training is that it allows you to burn more calories in less time than traditional steady-state cardio workouts. This is because the short bursts of intense activity increase your heart rate and metabolism, causing your body to continue burning calories even after your workout is over.

Another benefit of high intensity aerobic training is that it can improve your cardiovascular fitness, helping you to build endurance and stamina. This can be especially helpful if you participate in sports or other activities that require a lot of physical exertion.

My Personal Experience with High Intensity Aerobic Training

Once I started incorporating high intensity aerobic training into my workouts, I noticed a significant change in my fitness level. I was able to push myself harder and burn more calories in less time than ever before. Plus, the variety of exercises I could do made my workouts more interesting and enjoyable.

How Does High Intensity Aerobic Training Work?

High intensity aerobic training works by pushing your body to its limits for short periods of time, followed by periods of rest or lower intensity activity. This type of training can be done with a variety of aerobic exercises, but the key is to work at a high intensity level for a short period of time.

During the intense activity periods, your heart rate and breathing rate will increase significantly, causing your body to burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular fitness. The rest periods in between the intense activity allow your body to recover and prepare for the next burst of activity.

Over time, high intensity aerobic training can help you build endurance and stamina, allowing you to perform better during sports or other activities that require a lot of physical exertion.

The Benefits of High Intensity Aerobic Training

One of the main benefits of high intensity aerobic training is that it allows you to burn more calories in less time than traditional steady-state cardio workouts. This is because the short bursts of intense activity increase your heart rate and metabolism, causing your body to continue burning calories even after your workout is over.

Another benefit of high intensity aerobic training is that it can improve your cardiovascular fitness, helping you to build endurance and stamina. This can be especially helpful if you participate in sports or other activities that require a lot of physical exertion.

High intensity aerobic training can also help to improve your overall fitness level, including your strength, speed, and agility. This can translate into improved performance in a variety of sports and activities.

How to Incorporate High Intensity Aerobic Training Into Your Workouts

If you're interested in incorporating high intensity aerobic training into your workouts, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time.

You should also make sure to warm up before each workout and cool down afterwards to prevent injury and promote recovery. Finally, it's important to listen to your body and adjust your workouts as needed to avoid overtraining or burnout.

Conclusion of What is High Intensity Aerobic Training

High intensity aerobic training is a highly effective form of exercise that can help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular fitness in less time than traditional steady-state cardio workouts. By incorporating this type of training into your workouts, you can build endurance and stamina, improve your overall fitness level, and achieve your health and fitness goals faster than ever before.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do high intensity aerobic training?

A: It's generally recommended to do high intensity aerobic training 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts.

Q: Is high intensity aerobic training safe for beginners?

A: Yes, high intensity aerobic training can be safe for beginners as long as you start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time.

Q: What are some good exercises for high intensity aerobic training?

A: Running, cycling, jumping rope, and rowing are all good exercises for high intensity aerobic training. You can also incorporate bodyweight exercises like burpees, jumping jacks, and mountain climbers into your workouts.

Q: How long should the intense activity periods be during high intensity aerobic training?

A: The intense activity periods during high intensity aerobic training should be anywhere from 20-60 seconds, depending on your fitness level and the specific exercise you're doing.
