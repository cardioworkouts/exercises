Cardio Intervals On Treadmill A Beginner s Guide

Written by William Oct 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending hours on the treadmill without seeing results? Do you want to take your cardio workouts to the next level? If so, it's time to incorporate cardio intervals on treadmill into your routine. Not only will this help you burn more calories in less time, but it will also improve your overall cardiovascular fitness.

Many people struggle with traditional steady-state cardio workouts because they can quickly become boring and repetitive. Additionally, spending long periods of time at the same pace can lead to a plateau in your fitness progress. This is where cardio intervals on treadmill come in. By alternating between high-intensity intervals and periods of active recovery, you can challenge your body in new ways and avoid hitting a plateau.

The primary target of cardio intervals on treadmill is to increase your heart rate and challenge your cardiovascular system. This type of workout helps to improve your endurance, burn calories, and boost your metabolism. By incorporating intervals into your treadmill routine, you'll also improve your body's ability to use oxygen efficiently, which can positively impact your overall health and fitness levels.

In summary, cardio intervals on treadmill are a great way to improve your cardiovascular fitness, burn more calories, and avoid hitting a plateau in your workouts. By incorporating high-intensity intervals and periods of active recovery, you can challenge your body in new ways and see results faster.

Why Cardio Intervals on Treadmill Work

Cardio intervals on treadmill work by challenging your body to work harder than it would during a steady-state workout. During high-intensity intervals, your heart rate increases, and your body burns more calories. Additionally, active recovery periods give your body time to recover before the next interval, allowing you to maintain a higher level of intensity for a more extended period of time. This combination of high-intensity intervals and active recovery is what makes cardio intervals on treadmill so effective.

Personally, I've found that incorporating cardio intervals on treadmill into my routine has helped me break through a plateau in my fitness progress. I've also noticed that I feel more energized and focused throughout the day when I start with a high-intensity workout.

How to Do Cardio Intervals on Treadmill

Before starting any new workout routine, it's essential to warm up properly. Spend 5-10 minutes walking at a moderate pace to get your heart rate up and your muscles warmed up. Once you're ready to start your intervals, follow these steps:

Step 1: High-Intensity Interval

Begin by increasing the speed and incline on your treadmill to a challenging level. Run or jog at this pace for 30 seconds to 2 minutes, depending on your fitness level. The goal is to work at a high intensity, so you should feel out of breath and unable to carry on a conversation.

Step 2: Active Recovery

After your high-intensity interval, slow down your pace and decrease the incline on your treadmill. Walk or jog at a comfortable pace for 1-2 minutes. The goal of this active recovery period is to allow your heart rate to come down before the next high-intensity interval.

Step 3: Repeat

Repeat steps 1 and 2 for a total of 20-30 minutes. As you become more comfortable with this routine, you can increase the duration of your high-intensity intervals and decrease the duration of your active recovery periods.

The Benefits of Cardio Intervals on Treadmill

Cardio intervals on treadmill offer numerous benefits beyond just improving your cardiovascular fitness. Here are some of the primary benefits:

1. Burn More Calories

By working at a high intensity, you'll burn more calories in less time. Additionally, the afterburn effect of high-intensity exercise means that your body will continue to burn calories even after you've finished your workout.

2. Improve Your Endurance

Cardio intervals on treadmill challenge your body to work harder than it does during steady-state cardio. This, in turn, improves your endurance and allows you to work out for longer periods of time.

3. Boost Your Metabolism

High-intensity exercise has been shown to increase your metabolism, which means that you'll burn more calories throughout the day, even when you're not working out.

4. Improve Your Overall Health

Cardiovascular exercise is essential for maintaining good health. Regular cardio intervals on treadmill can improve your heart health, lower your blood pressure, and reduce your risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes and obesity.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio intervals on treadmill?

A: It's recommended that you do cardio intervals on treadmill 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between. This will allow your body time to recover and adapt to the new challenge.

Q: Can I do cardio intervals on treadmill if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes! Cardio intervals on treadmill can be adapted to any fitness level. If you're new to exercise, start with shorter intervals and longer active recovery periods. As you become more comfortable, you can increase the duration of your intervals and decrease the duration of your active recovery periods.

Q: What's the best time of day to do cardio intervals on treadmill?

A: The best time of day to do cardio intervals on treadmill is whenever you can fit it into your schedule! That being said, many people find that working out in the morning helps to energize them for the day ahead.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to do cardio intervals on treadmill?

A: The only equipment you need to do cardio intervals on treadmill is a treadmill! If you want to track your heart rate, you can use a heart rate monitor, but this is not necessary.

Conclusion of Cardio Intervals on Treadmill

Cardio intervals on treadmill are a great way to improve your cardiovascular fitness, burn more calories, and avoid hitting a plateau in your workouts. By incorporating high-intensity intervals and periods of active recovery, you can challenge your body in new ways and see results faster. Remember to warm up properly before starting your intervals, and start with shorter intervals if you're a beginner. With consistency and dedication, cardio intervals on treadmill can help you reach your fitness goals.
