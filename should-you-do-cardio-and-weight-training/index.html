Should You Do Cardio And Weight Training The Ultimate Guide

Written by William Jun 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering whether you should do cardio and weight training? You're not alone. Many people are confused about which workout is best for them. Some think cardio is the key to losing weight, while others believe weightlifting is the only way to build muscle. So, what should you do? The answer is both. In this ultimate guide, we'll explore the benefits of cardio and weight training, how to combine them effectively, and everything you need to know to get started.

Why People Struggle with Cardio and Weight Training?

Many people struggle with cardio and weight training because they believe they have to choose one or the other. Some people think that cardio is only for weight loss, while weightlifting is only for muscle gain. However, this is a common misconception. The truth is that both cardio and weight training are essential for overall health and fitness. Cardio helps improve heart health, burn calories, and reduce stress, while weight training helps build muscle, increase strength, and boost metabolism.

What is Cardio and Weight Training?

Cardio, short for cardiovascular exercise, is any form of exercise that raises your heart rate and works your cardiovascular system. Examples of cardio include running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. Weight training, on the other hand, is any form of exercise that uses resistance to build muscle strength and size. Examples of weight training include lifting weights, using resistance bands, and bodyweight exercises like push-ups and squats.

How to Combine Cardio and Weight Training?

The best way to combine cardio and weight training is to alternate between the two. For example, you could do weight training on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and cardio on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday. This will give your body time to rest and recover between workouts while still allowing you to get the benefits of both cardio and weight training. Another way to combine cardio and weight training is to do both in the same workout. This is known as circuit training, where you alternate between cardio and weight training exercises with little to no rest in between.

The Benefits of Cardio and Weight Training

Cardio and weight training offer many benefits for your overall health and fitness. Cardio helps improve heart health, increase endurance, and burn calories. It also reduces stress and improves mood. Weight training, on the other hand, helps build muscle, increase strength, and boost metabolism. It also helps prevent osteoporosis and improves bone density.

My Personal Experience with Cardio and Weight Training

When I first started working out, I focused mainly on cardio because I wanted to lose weight. However, I didn't see the results I was looking for. It wasn't until I started incorporating weight training into my routine that I noticed a significant change in my body. I started to see muscle definition and felt stronger overall. Combining cardio and weight training has helped me achieve my fitness goals and maintain a healthy weight.

The Best Cardio and Weight Training Exercises

The best cardio exercises include running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. These exercises raise your heart rate and work your cardiovascular system. The best weight training exercises include lifting weights, using resistance bands, and bodyweight exercises like push-ups and squats. These exercises use resistance to build muscle strength and size.

How Often Should You Do Cardio and Weight Training?

The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week. They also recommend weight training at least two days per week. However, the frequency of your workouts will depend on your fitness goals and schedule.

How to Get Started with Cardio and Weight Training?

If you're new to cardio and weight training, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and frequency of your workouts. You should also consult with a trainer or healthcare professional to ensure you're performing exercises correctly and safely. Start with basic exercises and focus on proper form before increasing weight or intensity. As you progress, you can add more advanced exercises and increase the weight or intensity of your workouts.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day. This is known as circuit training, where you alternate between cardio and weight training exercises with little to no rest in between.

Q: How long should a cardio and weight training workout be?

A: The duration of your workout will depend on your fitness goals and schedule. However, the American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week and weight training at least two days per week.

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weight training first?

A: It's best to alternate between cardio and weight training or do them in the same workout. However, if you have to choose one, it's generally better to do weight training first because it requires more energy and focus.

Q: How long does it take to see results from cardio and weight training?

A: The time it takes to see results from cardio and weight training will depend on your fitness goals and starting point. However, you can expect to see some changes in your body within a few weeks to a few months of consistent exercise.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio and Weight Training

Cardio and weight training are both essential for overall health and fitness. They offer many benefits and can help you achieve your fitness goals. The best way to combine cardio and weight training is to alternate between the two or do both in the same workout. If you're new to cardio and weight training, start slowly and consult with a trainer or healthcare professional. Remember, consistency is key, and with time and effort, you can achieve your fitness goals.
