Can I Do Cardio After Workout The Ultimate Guide To Post Workout Cardio

Written by Christine Nov 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who has just finished a workout and is wondering whether it's okay to do some cardio? You're not alone! Many people have the same question. In this article, we will explore the topic of whether you can do cardio after a workout and provide you with some helpful tips and advice.

The concern of whether or not to do cardio after a workout is a common one. Many people worry that doing cardio after a workout will undo all their hard work, lead to muscle loss, or cause injury. However, this is not necessarily true. In fact, incorporating cardio into your post-workout routine can have many benefits.

So, can you do cardio after a workout? The answer is yes! In fact, doing cardio after a workout can help to improve your cardiovascular endurance, burn more calories, and aid in muscle recovery. However, it is important to keep in mind that the type, duration, and intensity of the cardio should be appropriate for your fitness level and goals.

In summary, doing cardio after a workout can be beneficial as long as it is done correctly. Here are some key points to keep in mind when considering post-workout cardio:

What Type of Cardio Should You Do?

When it comes to post-workout cardio, the type of exercise you choose should be dependent on your fitness level and goals. For example, if you are a beginner, you may want to start with low-impact exercises like walking, cycling, or swimming. If you are more advanced, you may opt for high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or running. It's important to choose a type of cardio that you enjoy and that will help you achieve your fitness goals.

Personally, I like to do some light cycling after a weightlifting session. It helps me to cool down and flush out any lactic acid build-up in my muscles. I find that it also helps me to mentally relax and unwind after a tough workout.

How Long Should Your Post-Workout Cardio Session Be?

The duration of your post-workout cardio session should also be dependent on your fitness level and goals. Generally, it's recommended to do at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after a workout. However, if you are a beginner, you may want to start with shorter sessions and gradually increase the duration as your fitness level improves.

I usually do around 20-30 minutes of light cycling after a workout. However, if I'm feeling particularly energetic, I may do a longer session of up to an hour.

What Should the Intensity of Your Post-Workout Cardio Session Be?

The intensity of your post-workout cardio session should also be appropriate for your fitness level and goals. If you are a beginner, you may want to start with a low to moderate intensity. This could be as simple as going for a brisk walk or a light jog. If you are more advanced, you may opt for a high-intensity interval training (HIIT) session or a more intense form of cardio like running or cycling.

Personally, I like to keep my post-workout cardio session at a moderate intensity. I find that it helps me to burn some extra calories without putting too much strain on my body.

How to Incorporate Post-Workout Cardio into Your Routine

If you're new to post-workout cardio, it can be helpful to start with short, low-intensity sessions and gradually increase the duration and intensity as your fitness level improves. It's also important to listen to your body and not overdo it. If you feel tired or sore, take a rest day or do a lighter form of cardio like walking or yoga.

Personally, I like to do cardio after a weightlifting session. I find that it helps me to cool down and relax after a tough workout. I usually start with 10-15 minutes of light cycling and gradually increase the duration as my body feels more energized.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Will doing cardio after a workout cause muscle loss?

A: No, doing cardio after a workout will not cause muscle loss as long as it is done appropriately. In fact, it can aid in muscle recovery and help to improve your cardiovascular endurance.

Q: Should I do high-intensity cardio after a weightlifting session?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. If you are more advanced and looking to improve your cardiovascular endurance, high-intensity cardio may be appropriate. However, if you are a beginner, it may be better to start with low to moderate intensity cardio.

Q: How long should I wait after a workout before doing cardio?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after a workout before doing cardio. This allows your body to properly cool down and recover.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on rest days as long as it doesn't interfere with your recovery. It's important to listen to your body and not overdo it.

Conclusion of Can I Do Cardio After Workout

In conclusion, doing cardio after a workout can be beneficial as long as it is done appropriately. The type, duration, and intensity of the cardio should be dependent on your fitness level and goals. Remember to listen to your body and not overdo it. If you feel tired or sore, take a rest day or do a lighter form of cardio like walking or yoga. With these tips in mind, you can incorporate post-workout cardio into your routine and achieve your fitness goals.
