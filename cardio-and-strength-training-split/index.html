Cardio And Strength Training Split A Beginner s Guide

Written by Robby Jun 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to improve your fitness level but not sure where to start? Cardio and strength training split could be the answer you're looking for. This approach combines two essential forms of exercise to help you achieve a healthier body, boost your metabolism, and enhance your overall fitness level. In this article, we'll cover the basics of cardio and strength training split, its benefits, and how to get started.

The Pain Points of Cardio and Strength Training Split

Many people struggle to find the right exercise routine that suits their needs and fitness goals. Some individuals focus solely on cardio or strength training, missing out on the benefits of both. Others may not have the time or knowledge to create a balanced workout plan that includes both types of exercise. This is where cardio and strength training split comes in, providing a structured and efficient way to include both forms of exercise in your routine.

What is Cardio and Strength Training Split?

Cardio and Strength Training Split is a workout routine that alternates between cardiovascular exercises and strength training activities. Instead of doing both forms of exercise in one session, you split them into different days, allowing your body to rest and recover between workouts. This approach helps you avoid overtraining and allows you to focus on specific muscle groups in each session.

Cardio exercises aim to improve your cardiovascular endurance, burn calories, and increase your heart rate. Examples of cardio exercises include running, cycling, jumping jacks, and swimming. Strength training, on the other hand, focuses on building muscle mass, increasing strength, and improving bone density. Examples of strength training exercises include weightlifting, resistance band exercises, and bodyweight exercises.

Benefits of Cardio and Strength Training Split

There are several benefits to incorporating cardio and strength training split into your workout routine:

  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased muscle mass and strength
  • Enhanced metabolism and calorie burning
  • Better bone density and reduced risk of osteoporosis
  • Increased energy levels and improved mood
  • Reduced stress and anxiety levels

My Experience with Cardio and Strength Training Split

When I started working out, I would focus solely on cardio exercises, such as running and cycling. While I enjoyed these activities, I felt like I was missing out on the benefits of strength training. I decided to try cardio and strength training split, and I immediately noticed a difference in my fitness level. I felt stronger, had more energy, and noticed a change in my body composition. I also enjoyed the variety of exercises and the challenge of trying new workouts.

One of my favorite cardio exercises is cycling, and I usually do this activity on my cardio days. For strength training, I like to use resistance bands and bodyweight exercises, focusing on my legs and core. I also incorporate weightlifting into my routine, targeting different muscle groups each session.

How to Get Started with Cardio and Strength Training Split

If you're new to cardio and strength training split, it's essential to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. Here are some tips to get started:

  • Consult with a fitness professional to create a balanced workout plan that suits your fitness level and goals.
  • Start with two or three days of cardio and strength training split per week, alternating between the two types of exercise.
  • Focus on proper form and technique to avoid injury.
  • Incorporate a variety of exercises to keep your workouts fun and challenging.
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts as your fitness level improves.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: While it's possible to do both forms of exercise in one session, it's not recommended for beginners. Splitting your workouts into different days allows your body to rest and recover between sessions, reducing the risk of injury and overtraining.

Q: How long should my cardio and strength training split workouts be?

A: The duration of your workouts will depend on your fitness level and goals. As a beginner, aim for 30-60 minutes per session, gradually increasing the duration as your fitness level improves.

Q: Do I need any equipment for cardio and strength training split?

A: While some exercises may require equipment, such as weights or resistance bands, many can be done with just your bodyweight. Consult with a fitness professional to determine what equipment you need for your workout plan.

Q: How long will it take to see results with cardio and strength training split?

A: The time it takes to see results will depend on your fitness level, goals, and consistency with your workouts. With regular exercise and a balanced diet, you can expect to see improvements in your fitness level within a few weeks.

Conclusion of Cardio and Strength Training Split

Cardio and strength training split is a great way to improve your fitness level, increase your metabolism, and enhance your overall health. By alternating between cardiovascular exercises and strength training activities, you can achieve a balanced workout routine that targets all areas of your body. Remember to start slow, focus on proper form and technique, and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. With dedication and consistency, you can reach your fitness goals and enjoy the numerous benefits of cardio and strength training split.
