Can I Do Cardio Before Or After Workout A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Jul 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you new to fitness and wondering whether you should do cardio before or after your workout? Or have you been working out for a while but still unsure about the best approach? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this question, and it's an important one to answer because the timing of your cardio can affect your results.

Some pain points related to can I do cardio before or after workout include not knowing how to structure a workout, not understanding the benefits of cardio, and not knowing how to balance cardio and weightlifting.

The answer to can I do cardio before or after workout is that it depends on your goals. If your primary goal is to build muscle and strength, it's best to do weightlifting first and cardio second. This is because weightlifting requires more energy and focus, and doing cardio first can tire you out and reduce the quality of your weightlifting session. However, if your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness, doing cardio first is a good option.

In summary, the main points related to can I do cardio before or after workout are that the timing depends on your goals, weightlifting should come first if building muscle is your priority, and cardio is a good option if you want to improve your cardiovascular fitness.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Your Workout

Personally, I like to start my workouts with cardio because it helps me warm up and get my heart rate up. I find that doing 10-15 minutes of cardio before my weightlifting session helps me feel more energized and focused. Additionally, doing cardio first can help you burn more calories overall because your body will continue to burn calories even after you finish your workout.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Your Workout

On the other hand, some people prefer to do cardio after their weightlifting session because it can help them cool down and stretch out their muscles. Additionally, doing cardio after weightlifting can help you burn more fat because your body will have depleted its glycogen stores during weightlifting, forcing it to use fat for energy during cardio.

How to Balance Cardio and Weightlifting

If you want to do both cardio and weightlifting, it's important to balance the two in a way that works for you. One approach is to do weightlifting and cardio on different days. For example, you could do weightlifting on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and cardio on Tuesday and Thursday. Another approach is to do both weightlifting and cardio on the same day, but in separate sessions. For example, you could do weightlifting in the morning and cardio in the evening.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before or After Your Workout

Whether you decide to do cardio before or after your workout, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First, make sure to warm up properly before starting any exercise to reduce the risk of injury. Second, listen to your body and adjust your workout accordingly. If you're feeling tired or sore, it's okay to take a break or reduce the intensity of your workout. Finally, don't forget to stretch after your workout to help your muscles recover and prevent stiffness.

Conclusion of Can I Do Cardio Before or After Workout

In conclusion, the answer to can I do cardio before or after workout is that it depends on your goals. If building muscle is your priority, it's best to do weightlifting first and cardio second. However, if improving your cardiovascular fitness is your goal, doing cardio first is a good option. Ultimately, the best approach is the one that works for you and your body.

Question and Answer:

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weightlifting if I want to lose weight?

A: If weight loss is your goal, doing cardio after weightlifting is a good option because it can help you burn more fat.

Q: How long should I do cardio before or after my weightlifting session?

A: It's best to do 10-15 minutes of cardio before or after your weightlifting session to warm up or cool down.

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both cardio and weightlifting on the same day, but it's important to balance the two in a way that works for you.

Q: Should I do high-intensity or low-intensity cardio before or after my weightlifting session?

A: The intensity of your cardio depends on your fitness level and goals. Low-intensity cardio is good for warming up or cooling down, while high-intensity cardio can help you burn more calories overall.
