How To Choose Between Two A Days Cardio Or Weights First

Written by Oliver Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you wondering whether to start your two a days with cardio or weights? This question has been a source of confusion for most gym-goers, and it's easy to see why. Both cardio and weights have numerous benefits to the body. So, how do you choose between the two? In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of each to help you make an informed decision.

The Target of Two A Days Cardio or Weights First

The target of two a days is to maximize the benefits of exercising while minimizing the time spent in the gym. By working out twice a day, you can split your routine into two shorter sessions, allowing you to focus more on specific muscle groups and avoid overtraining. Two a days also help to boost your metabolism, burn more calories, and increase your overall fitness level.

When it comes to choosing between cardio and weights, the answer depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to lose weight, cardio should be your priority. If you want to build muscle, lifting weights should come first. However, there are a few factors to consider.

Cardio First or Weights First?

When starting your two a days routine, it's crucial to decide whether to do cardio or weights first. Here are some pros and cons of both:

Cardio First

Starting with cardio can help to warm up your muscles, increase your heart rate, and boost your endurance. It also burns calories, which can help to create a calorie deficit necessary for weight loss. However, if you're lifting weights after cardio, you may feel fatigued and not perform at your best.

How to Do Cardio First?

Begin your workout with a 20-30 minute cardio session such as running, cycling, or rowing. Make sure to keep the intensity moderate to high, depending on your fitness level. After finishing cardio, take a few minutes to stretch and hydrate before moving onto weights.

Weights First

Starting with weights can help to build muscle, increase strength, and burn more calories during and after exercise. It also allows you to lift heavier weights when you're not fatigued, improving your overall performance. However, lifting weights first may decrease your endurance and make cardio harder to perform.

How to Do Weights First?

Begin your workout with compound exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press, which work multiple muscle groups at once. Lift heavy weights with proper form and take adequate rest between sets. After finishing weights, cool down with a few minutes of light cardio and stretching.

The Bottom Line

So, which should you choose between cardio and weights first? The answer depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. If you're unsure, try both and see which works best for you. Remember, the most important thing is to be consistent and challenge yourself with every workout.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do two a days every day?

A: It's not recommended to do two a days every day as it can lead to overtraining and increase your risk of injury. Limit your two a days routine to 2-3 times a week and allow your body to rest and recover on other days.

Q: Should I eat before or after two a days?

A: It's recommended to eat a small meal or snack before exercising to provide your body with the necessary energy. After working out, consume a protein-rich meal or shake to help with muscle recovery and growth.

Q: Can I do two a days at home?

A: Yes, you can do two a days at home with minimal equipment. Incorporate bodyweight exercises, resistance bands, and cardio exercises such as jumping jacks, burpees, and mountain climbers into your routine.

Q: How long should my two a days workout be?

A: Your two a days workout should be 30-45 minutes each, with a 4-6 hour break in between. This allows your body to recover and prepare for the next session.

Conclusion of Two A Days Cardio or Weights First

Choosing between cardio and weights first can be a difficult decision, but it ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. By incorporating two a days into your routine, you can optimize your workout and achieve your fitness goals faster. Remember to listen to your body, be consistent, and have fun!
