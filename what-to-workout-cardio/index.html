What To Workout Cardio A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Jun 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you new to working out and wondering what type of cardio exercises are best for you? Do you feel overwhelmed by the amount of information available online? You're not alone. Starting a fitness journey can be intimidating, but with the right guidance, you can achieve your goals and improve your overall health and well-being.

Cardio, short for cardiovascular exercise, is any type of exercise that gets your heart rate up and increases blood flow throughout your body. It's an essential part of any fitness routine and has numerous health benefits, including improved cardiovascular health, increased endurance, and weight loss.

So, what type of cardio should you do? The answer depends on your fitness level, goals, and personal preferences. Here are some of the most popular cardio exercises:

Running

Running is a popular form of cardio that can be done both indoors and outdoors. It's a great way to burn calories, improve cardiovascular health, and strengthen your lower body. If you're new to running, start with short distances and gradually increase your speed and distance over time. Don't forget to stretch before and after your run to prevent injury.

Cycling

Cycling is a low-impact cardio exercise that's easy on your joints. It's a great way to improve cardiovascular health, burn calories, and strengthen your lower body. If you're new to cycling, start with a stationary bike at the gym or a low-resistance bike outdoors. As you build endurance, you can increase the resistance and try longer distances.

Swimming

Swimming is a full-body workout that's easy on the joints and great for people of all fitness levels. It's an excellent way to improve cardiovascular health, build endurance, and strengthen your entire body. If you're new to swimming, start with short distances and gradually increase your speed and distance over time.

Jumping Rope

Jumping rope is a fun and challenging cardio exercise that can be done anywhere. It's a great way to improve cardiovascular health, burn calories, and improve coordination. If you're new to jumping rope, start with a slow and steady pace and gradually increase your speed and intensity over time.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

HIIT is a type of cardio exercise that involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. It's a great way to burn calories, improve cardiovascular health, and increase endurance. If you're new to HIIT, start with short intervals and gradually increase the intensity and duration over time.

Dance Cardio

Dance cardio is a fun and energetic way to get your heart rate up and burn calories. It's a great way to improve cardiovascular health, coordination, and flexibility. If you're new to dance cardio, start with a beginner's class and gradually work your way up to more advanced classes.

Now that you know what type of cardio exercises are best for you, it's essential to create a workout plan that includes a variety of exercises to prevent boredom and ensure that you're working your entire body. Remember to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time. With dedication and consistency, you'll achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio?

A: It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week. You can split this up into shorter workouts throughout the week.

Q: Can I do cardio every day?

A: It's safe to do moderate-intensity cardio every day, but high-intensity cardio should be done no more than 3-4 times per week to prevent injury and burnout.

Q: Do I need any equipment to do cardio?

A: No, you don't need any equipment to do cardio. Running, cycling, and jumping rope can be done with minimal equipment. However, if you have access to a gym, there are many cardio machines available, such as treadmills, stationary bikes, and ellipticals.

Q: Can I lose weight by doing cardio alone?

A: Cardio can help you lose weight by burning calories, but it's essential to combine it with a healthy diet and strength training to achieve optimal results.

Conclusion of what to workout cardio

Cardio exercise is an essential part of any fitness routine and has numerous health benefits. Whether you prefer running, cycling, swimming, jumping rope, HIIT, or dance cardio, it's essential to create a workout plan that includes a variety of exercises to prevent boredom and ensure that you're working your entire body. Remember to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time. With dedication and consistency, you'll achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being.
