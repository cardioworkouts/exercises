Can I Do Hiit And Strength Training On The Same Day

Written by Bardi Nov 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering if you can do HIIT and strength training on the same day? This is a common question for those who want to optimize their workouts but also want to avoid overtraining or injury. In this blog post, we will explore the answer to this question and provide tips on how to safely incorporate both types of workouts into your routine.

Combining HIIT and strength training on the same day can be challenging because both workouts place a significant amount of stress on your body. HIIT workouts are high-intensity and require a lot of energy, while strength training requires your muscles to work against resistance. Doing both on the same day can lead to fatigue and increased risk of injury if not done properly.

The short answer is yes, you can do HIIT and strength training on the same day. However, there are some things to consider before doing so.

First, it's important to alternate between the two types of workouts to give your body enough rest and recovery time. For example, you could do strength training on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and HIIT on Tuesday and Thursday. This will give your muscles enough time to recover between workouts.

Personal Experience

I used to do both HIIT and strength training on the same day, and I found that my workouts suffered as a result. I was always tired and felt like I wasn't able to give 100% effort during my workouts. However, once I started alternating between the two types of workouts, I noticed a significant improvement in my performance and recovery time.

Tips for Combining HIIT and Strength Training

Here are some tips for safely combining HIIT and strength training on the same day:

1. Warm-up and cool down properly

Make sure to warm up your muscles before starting any type of workout. This can include a light jog, jumping jacks, or dynamic stretching. Cooling down after your workout is also important to prevent injury and reduce muscle soreness.

Example:

Before starting your workout, spend 5-10 minutes doing light cardio, such as jogging or jumping jacks. After your workout, cool down by stretching your muscles and doing some light yoga poses.

2. Alternate between the two types of workouts

As mentioned earlier, alternating between HIIT and strength training on different days will give your body enough rest and recovery time. If you want to do both on the same day, make sure to do one type of workout first and then the other.

Example:

You could start your workout with 30 minutes of strength training, followed by 20 minutes of HIIT. Alternatively, you could do 20 minutes of HIIT first, followed by 30 minutes of strength training.

3. Listen to your body

It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. If you feel overly fatigued or experience pain during your workout, take a break or stop altogether. Overtraining can lead to injury and long-term health problems.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do HIIT and strength training every day?

A: It's not recommended to do both types of workouts every day. Your body needs time to rest and recover between workouts. It's best to alternate between the two types of workouts or do them on different days.

Q: Should I do HIIT or strength training first?

A: It's up to personal preference, but it's generally recommended to do strength training first. This is because HIIT requires a lot of energy and doing it first may deplete your energy reserves for strength training.

Q: How long should I rest between HIIT and strength training?

A: It's recommended to rest for at least 30 minutes between HIIT and strength training to give your body enough time to recover.

Q: Can I do both types of workouts in one session?

A: Yes, you can do both types of workouts in one session, but make sure to alternate between the two and listen to your body. It's important not to overdo it and risk injury.

Conclusion of Can I Do HIIT and Strength Training on the Same Day

Combining HIIT and strength training on the same day is possible, but it requires careful planning and attention to your body's needs. Alternating between the two types of workouts or doing them on different days is recommended to prevent overtraining and injury. Remember to warm up and cool down properly, listen to your body, and rest and recover between workouts.
