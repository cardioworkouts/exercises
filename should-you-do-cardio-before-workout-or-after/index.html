Should You Do Cardio Before Workout Or After Find Out Here

Written by Eveline Sep 26, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a fitness enthusiast who wants to maximize their workout routine? One question that often comes up is whether to do cardio before or after a workout. It's a common dilemma that gym-goers face, and the answer isn't always clear-cut. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of both options so that you can make an informed decision.

When it comes to deciding whether to do cardio before or after a workout, there are several factors to consider. These include your fitness goals, workout intensity, and time constraints. For example, if you're training for a marathon, doing cardio before your strength training session might be more beneficial. On the other hand, if your main goal is to build muscle, then doing cardio after your strength training session might be better.

The short answer to the question of whether you should do cardio before or after a workout is that it depends on your goals. There is no one-size-fits-all answer that will work for everyone. However, we'll explore the advantages and disadvantages of both options so that you can make an informed decision.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before a Workout

Personally, I prefer doing cardio before my workout. It helps me get my heart rate up, and I feel like I have more energy during my strength training session. Doing cardio before your workout can also help you burn more calories. When you do cardio first, your body burns stored glycogen for fuel. Once that glycogen is depleted, your body starts burning fat for fuel, which can help you lose weight.

However, there are some downsides to doing cardio before your workout. For one, it can be tiring, which can lead to a less intense strength training session. Additionally, if you do too much cardio before your workout, you might not have enough energy to complete your strength training session.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After a Workout

The main benefit of doing cardio after your workout is that you'll have more energy for strength training. When you do cardio first, you might be too tired to lift weights with proper form or lift as much weight as you would like. Doing cardio after your strength training session can also help you burn more fat. When you do strength training first, your body burns stored glycogen for fuel. Once that glycogen is depleted, your body starts burning fat for fuel, which can help you lose weight.

However, there are also some downsides to doing cardio after your workout. For one, it can be time-consuming, especially if you're already pressed for time. Additionally, if you're too tired after your strength training session, you might not feel like doing cardio at all.

How to Incorporate Cardio Into Your Workout Routine

One way to incorporate cardio into your workout routine is to do it on separate days from your strength training. This allows you to focus on each type of exercise without compromising the quality of your workout. Another option is to do cardio and strength training on the same day, but at different times. For example, you could do cardio in the morning and strength training in the evening. This gives your body time to recover between workouts.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before or After Your Workout

Whether you decide to do cardio before or after your workout, there are some tips you should keep in mind. First, make sure to warm up properly before your cardio session. This will help prevent injury and prepare your body for the workout. Second, choose a type of cardio that you enjoy. If you hate running on the treadmill, try cycling or swimming instead. Finally, listen to your body. If you feel too tired to complete your workout after doing cardio, take a break and come back another day.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before Workout or After

Ultimately, whether you should do cardio before or after your workout depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. There are advantages and disadvantages to both options, so it's important to choose what works best for you. Remember to warm up properly, choose a type of cardio that you enjoy, and listen to your body. With these tips in mind, you can create a workout routine that helps you achieve your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weightlifting?

A: It depends on your goals. If you're training for a marathon or trying to improve your endurance, doing cardio before weightlifting might be better. However, if you're trying to build muscle, doing cardio after weightlifting might be more beneficial.

Q: How long should I do cardio before or after a workout?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per session, but you can do more if you feel up to it.

Q: What type of cardio is best before or after a workout?

A: The best type of cardio is one that you enjoy and that challenges you. Some good options include running, cycling, swimming, and rowing.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and strength training on the same day. However, it's important to give your body time to recover between workouts.
