Songs For Aerobic Exercise A Guide To Keeping You Moving

Written by Robby Jul 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your workout routine feeling stale? Do you struggle to find the motivation to get moving? Music can be a powerful tool for boosting your energy and helping you power through your workouts. In this guide, we'll explore the benefits of using songs for aerobic exercise and provide some examples of great songs to add to your workout playlist.

Why You Need Songs for Aerobic Exercise

Let's face it - working out can be tough. Whether you're running on a treadmill or lifting weights, it's easy to get bored and lose motivation. That's where music comes in. Studies have shown that listening to music during exercise can improve your endurance, boost your mood, and even distract you from feelings of fatigue or discomfort.

So, what makes a great aerobic exercise song? Ideally, you want to choose songs that have a strong beat and a fast tempo. This will help you stay motivated and keep your heart rate up throughout your workout. Additionally, songs with positive lyrics or a catchy melody can help keep your mind engaged and focused on your goals.

Now that you understand the benefits of using songs for aerobic exercise, let's dive into some specific examples.

Top Songs for Aerobic Exercise

Here are five great songs to add to your workout playlist:

1. "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake

This upbeat pop song has a tempo of 113 beats per minute (BPM) and a catchy melody that will have you singing along while you sweat. The positive lyrics will help you stay motivated and focused on your goals.

2. "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson feat. Bruno Mars

With a tempo of 115 BPM, this funky tune is perfect for getting your heart rate up. The funky bassline and catchy chorus will keep you moving and grooving throughout your workout.

3. "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

This classic rock anthem has a tempo of 109 BPM and a powerful, motivating message that will help you push through even the toughest workouts. Plus, who doesn't love a good Rocky reference?

4. "Sorry" by Justin Bieber

This pop hit has a tempo of 100 BPM and a catchy chorus that will keep you moving. The upbeat melody and positive lyrics will help you stay focused and motivated throughout your workout.

5. "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott feat. Ciara & Fatman Scoop

This high-energy hip hop track has a tempo of 125 BPM and a driving beat that will keep you moving from start to finish. The catchy chorus and fast-paced verses will help you power through even the toughest workouts.

Personal Experience

As someone who struggles to stay motivated during workouts, I can attest to the power of music. Whenever I'm feeling sluggish or unmotivated, I turn on my favorite workout playlist and let the music do the rest. Whether it's an upbeat pop song or a high-energy hip hop track, the right music has the ability to transform my workout from a chore to a joy.

The Benefits of Using Songs for Aerobic Exercise

There are countless benefits to using songs for aerobic exercise. In addition to improving endurance and boosting mood, music can also help you stay focused and motivated throughout your workout. Plus, with so many great songs to choose from, you're sure to find a playlist that suits your personal taste and workout style.

Choosing the Right Songs for Aerobic Exercise

When it comes to choosing songs for aerobic exercise, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First, make sure the tempo is appropriate for your workout. A slower tempo may be better for strength training or yoga, while a faster tempo is ideal for cardio or high-intensity interval training (HIIT).

Additionally, pay attention to the lyrics and melody of each song. Choose songs with positive messages and catchy melodies that will keep you focused and motivated throughout your workout.

Tips for Creating the Perfect Workout Playlist

If you're looking to create the ultimate workout playlist, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose songs with a consistent tempo to help you maintain a steady pace throughout your workout.
  • Include a mix of genres to keep things interesting and prevent boredom.
  • Pay attention to the lyrics and melody of each song to ensure they're motivating and engaging.
  • Update your playlist regularly to keep things fresh and prevent burnout.

Question and Answer

Q: Can listening to music really improve my workout?

A: Yes! Numerous studies have shown that listening to music during exercise can improve endurance, boost mood, and distract from feelings of fatigue or discomfort.

Q: Do I need to choose specific songs for different types of workouts?

A: Ideally, yes. Different types of workouts may require different tempos and styles of music. For example, a slower tempo may be better for yoga or stretching, while a faster tempo is ideal for cardio or HIIT workouts.

Q: Can I use songs with explicit lyrics in my workout playlist?

A: It's up to you, but keep in mind that explicit lyrics may not be appropriate for all settings (such as a gym or group fitness class). Additionally, some people may find explicit lyrics distracting or offensive.

Q: How often should I update my workout playlist?

A: It's a good idea to update your playlist regularly to keep things fresh and prevent burnout. Aim to add new songs every few weeks or whenever you start to feel bored with your current playlist.

Conclusion of Songs for Aerobic Exercise

Adding songs for aerobic exercise to your workout routine can be a game-changer. Not only does it improve endurance and boost mood, but it can also make your workout feel more enjoyable and less like a chore. By choosing the right songs for your workout style and personal taste, you can transform your workout from a dreaded task to a fun and energizing experience.
