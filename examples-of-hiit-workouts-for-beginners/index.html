Examples Of Hiit Workouts For Beginners

Written by Luffy Sep 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a workout routine that will help you burn fat, build muscle, and improve your overall fitness, but you're not sure where to start? High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) may be the answer you've been looking for. HIIT workouts are short, intense bursts of exercise that can help you get fit in a fraction of the time it takes with traditional cardio workouts. In this post, we'll explore examples of HIIT workouts for beginners and how they can help you achieve your fitness goals.

If you're new to working out, starting with an intense HIIT routine can be intimidating. You might worry that you're not fit enough or that you'll injure yourself. However, HIIT workouts can be tailored to any fitness level, and with the right guidance, you'll be able to safely and effectively complete these workouts.

The goal of HIIT workouts is to alternate between periods of high-intensity exercise and periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of training helps you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time and helps improve your cardiovascular health. Examples of HIIT workouts for beginners include:

1. Jumping Jacks and Squats

Start by doing jumping jacks for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds. Next, do squats for 30 seconds, followed by a 10-second rest. Repeat this pattern for 10-20 minutes, depending on your fitness level.

I started with this HIIT workout routine after a long period of inactivity. It was challenging at first, but I gradually increased my repetitions and eventually was able to complete the entire workout without any breaks. This routine helped me lose weight and build muscle, and I felt more energized throughout the day.

2. Sprint Intervals

Find a flat surface, like a track or a treadmill, and sprint for 30 seconds, then rest for 30 seconds. Repeat this pattern for 10-20 minutes. If you're not comfortable sprinting, you can jog or walk instead.

I was hesitant to try sprint intervals at first because I wasn't used to running, but I quickly realized that the rest periods allowed me to catch my breath and recharge for the next sprint. This routine helped me improve my speed and endurance, and I felt more confident in my ability to tackle other fitness challenges.

3. Bodyweight Circuits

Select five to six bodyweight exercises, like push-ups, lunges, and burpees, and perform each exercise for 30 seconds, followed by a 10-second rest. Repeat the circuit for 10-20 minutes.

Bodyweight circuits were my go-to HIIT workout routine when I didn't have access to a gym or equipment. They were challenging, but I felt a sense of accomplishment after completing each circuit. This routine helped me build strength and endurance, and I felt more confident in my ability to perform other exercises.

4. Tabata Intervals

Perform one exercise, like jumping jacks or squats, for 20 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds. Repeat this pattern for eight rounds, for a total of four minutes. Rest for one minute, then repeat the process with a different exercise.

Tabata intervals are a great way to mix up your workout routine and challenge your body in new ways. I found that the short, intense bursts of exercise helped me burn more calories and improve my cardiovascular health.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should a HIIT workout last for beginners?
A: A HIIT workout should last between 10-20 minutes for beginners.

Q: Can I do a HIIT workout every day?
A: It's best to give your body time to rest and recover between HIIT workouts. Aim for two to three sessions per week.

Q: What are the benefits of HIIT workouts?
A: HIIT workouts can help you burn fat, improve your cardiovascular health, and build muscle.

Q: Do I need equipment for HIIT workouts?
A: No, HIIT workouts can be done with just your bodyweight, or you can use equipment like dumbbells or resistance bands to add more resistance.

Conclusion of Examples of HIIT Workouts for Beginners

HIIT workouts are a great way to get fit, burn fat, and improve your overall health in a fraction of the time it takes with traditional cardio workouts. With the examples of HIIT workouts for beginners we've discussed in this post, you can start your fitness journey and achieve your fitness goals. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. With time and consistency, you'll see the results you're looking for.
