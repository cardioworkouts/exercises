The Best High Intensity Cardio Machine A Tutorial Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for the best high intensity cardio machine for your workout routine? You've come to the right place. In this tutorial guide, we'll explore the top options for high intensity cardio machines, and help you find the best one for your needs.

When it comes to high intensity cardio, many people struggle to find the right machine or workout routine. Whether you're trying to improve your fitness, lose weight, or simply challenge yourself, it's important to choose a machine that will help you achieve your goals.

So, what exactly is high intensity cardio, and what are the best machines for this type of workout? High intensity cardio involves short bursts of intense activity, followed by periods of rest or recovery. The goal is to increase your heart rate and push your body to its limits. The best machines for high intensity cardio are those that allow you to work at a high intensity level, while also providing options for rest or recovery.

In this guide, we'll explore four of the top high intensity cardio machines on the market: the treadmill, the stationary bike, the rowing machine, and the elliptical trainer. We'll discuss the benefits and drawbacks of each machine, and help you decide which one is best for your needs.

The Treadmill

As one of the most popular cardio machines in the gym, the treadmill is a great option for high intensity workouts. It allows you to run or walk at a variety of speeds and inclines, and can be customized to fit your fitness level. Personally, I love using the treadmill for high intensity interval training (HIIT) workouts. I'll start with a five-minute warm-up, and then alternate between 30-second sprints and 30-second rests for 20 minutes. It's a tough workout, but it really gets my heart pumping!

The Stationary Bike

If you're looking for a lower-impact option for high intensity cardio, the stationary bike is a great choice. It's easy on your joints, but still allows you to work at a high intensity level. I like to use the stationary bike for longer, endurance-based workouts. I'll start with a five-minute warm-up, and then cycle at a steady pace for 30-60 minutes. I'll occasionally switch things up by adding in a 30-second sprint every few minutes.

The Rowing Machine

The rowing machine is a great option for full-body workouts that really get your heart rate up. It works your arms, legs, and core, and can be customized to fit your fitness level. Personally, I love to use the rowing machine for interval workouts. I'll row at a moderate pace for three minutes, and then row as fast as I can for 30 seconds. I'll repeat this pattern for 20-30 minutes.

The Elliptical Trainer

The elliptical trainer is another low-impact option for high intensity cardio. It's great for those who have joint pain, but still want to get a challenging workout in. I like to use the elliptical for longer, endurance-based workouts. I'll start with a five-minute warm-up, and then use the pre-programmed workouts on the machine to vary my intensity level.

Question and Answer

Q: What are the benefits of high intensity cardio?

A: High intensity cardio can help improve your cardiovascular health, increase your endurance, and burn calories more efficiently. It's also a great way to challenge yourself and push your limits.

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio?

A: It's recommended that you do high intensity cardio 1-2 times per week, in addition to regular moderate-intensity cardio and strength training.

Q: Do I need any special equipment for high intensity cardio?

A: While you don't necessarily need any special equipment, having access to a high intensity cardio machine like the ones we've discussed in this guide can make your workouts more challenging and effective.

Q: Can beginners do high intensity cardio?

A: Yes, beginners can do high intensity cardio, but it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity level. It's also important to listen to your body and rest when needed.

Conclusion

When it comes to high intensity cardio, there are many great machines to choose from. Whether you prefer the treadmill, stationary bike, rowing machine, or elliptical trainer, it's important to find a machine that works for your fitness level and goals. By incorporating high intensity cardio into your workout routine, you can improve your cardiovascular health, increase your endurance, and challenge yourself like never before.
