Diagnosis and Treatment of Dilated Cardiomyopathy

Diagnosis of dilated cardiomyopathy often involves a physical examination, a review of medical history, and diagnostic tests such as an electrocardiogram, echocardiogram, or cardiac MRI. Treatment for dilated cardiomyopathy depends on the severity of the condition and may include medications, lifestyle changes, and medical procedures such as implantable devices or heart transplant.

Medications

Medications such as ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, and diuretics can help manage symptoms and improve heart function in people with dilated cardiomyopathy. These medications work by reducing blood pressure, slowing the heart rate, and removing excess fluid from the body.

Lifestyle Changes

Lifestyle changes such as maintaining a healthy diet, exercising regularly, quitting smoking, and reducing alcohol consumption can also help manage dilated cardiomyopathy. These lifestyle changes can help reduce stress on the heart and improve overall health.
