Best Gym Music 2020 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Jul 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of listening to the same old gym music? Do you find yourself losing motivation during your workout? If you answered yes to either of these questions, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll be discussing the best gym music of 2020 to keep you energized and motivated throughout your workout.

When it comes to gym music, it's important to find something that matches your workout style and personal taste. Having the right music can increase your endurance, make you feel less fatigued, and even improve your mood. On the other hand, listening to the wrong music can negatively impact your workout and make you feel less motivated.

What is the target of Best Gym Music 2020?

The target of Best Gym Music 2020 is to provide you with a list of songs that are perfect for your workout. These songs have been carefully selected based on their beats per minute (BPM), lyrics, and overall energy. Whether you're into hip-hop, rock, or pop, there's something on this list for everyone.

So without further ado, let's dive into the best gym music of 2020.

Cardio Workout Music

For those who love a good cardio workout, we recommend songs with a BPM of 120-140. Some great options include "Blinding Lights" by The Weeknd, "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars, and "Dance Monkey" by Tones and I. These songs have a fast-paced beat that will keep you moving and motivated throughout your workout.

Weightlifting Music

If you're hitting the weights, then you'll want to listen to songs with a BPM of 80-100. Our top picks include "Lose Yourself" by Eminem, "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor, and "Welcome to the Jungle" by Guns N' Roses. These songs have a powerful energy that will help you push through those heavy lifts.

Top 3 Songs for Yoga and Stretching

While yoga and stretching may not require high-energy music, it's still important to find songs that help you relax and focus. Our top picks for yoga and stretching are "Weightless" by Marconi Union, "Breathe Me" by Sia, and "Clair de Lune" by Claude Debussy. These songs have a calming effect that will help you stay centered during your practice.

How to Create Your Own Playlist

If you prefer to create your own playlist, then consider the BPM of each song and the overall energy. You can use websites like jog.fm to find songs that match your workout style and BPM preferences. Don't be afraid to mix and match different genres to create a unique playlist that you'll love.

Conclusion of Best Gym Music 2020

Listening to the right music can make all the difference in your workout. Whether you're into cardio, weightlifting, or yoga, there's a song out there that's perfect for you. Use our list of the best gym music of 2020 as a starting point and create your own playlist that will keep you energized and motivated throughout your workout.
