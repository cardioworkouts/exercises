What Type Of Training Is Best For Weight Loss

Written by Aprilia Aug 01, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you struggling with weight loss and wondering what type of training is best for you? Losing weight can be a daunting task, especially when you don't know which type of exercise is best for you. You might have tried several types of exercise, but none seems to work. Well, you are not alone. Many people struggle with weight loss, and finding the best type of training can be overwhelming. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we will discuss what type of training is best for weight loss and why it works.

Weight loss is more than just looking good. It's about improving your overall health and reducing the risk of chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers. However, many people find it challenging to lose weight because they don't know where to start or what type of training to do. Some people might even feel like they are wasting their time and effort on exercises that don't work. But the truth is, not all exercises are created equal when it comes to weight loss.

The best type of training for weight loss is a combination of cardio and strength training. Cardio exercises like running, cycling, and swimming are great for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health. On the other hand, strength training exercises like weightlifting and resistance training help to build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and burns more calories even when you are resting.

So, why is a combination of cardio and strength training the best for weight loss? Well, it's simple. Cardio exercises help to burn calories during the workout, while strength training exercises help to build muscle, which burns more calories even when you are not working out. The more lean muscle mass you have, the more calories you burn at rest. This means that you will be burning more calories throughout the day, even when you are not exercising.

In summary, the best type of training for weight loss is a combination of cardio and strength training. Cardio exercises help to burn calories during the workout, while strength training exercises help to build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and burns more calories even when you are resting.

Cardio Training

When it comes to cardio training, there are many options to choose from. You can choose from running, cycling, swimming, dancing, or any other type of exercise that gets your heart rate up. Personally, I prefer running because it's a simple and effective way to burn calories. Running also helps to improve cardiovascular health and reduce the risk of chronic diseases like diabetes and heart disease.

Strength Training

Strength training is equally important when it comes to weight loss. Building lean muscle mass helps to increase your metabolism and burn more calories even when you are resting. Personally, I prefer weightlifting because it's a great way to build strength and muscle. However, you don't have to lift weights to strength train. You can use resistance bands or bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, and lunges.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is a type of training that combines cardio and strength training. HIIT involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by periods of rest. This type of training is great for burning calories and building lean muscle mass. Personally, I love doing HIIT workouts because they are challenging and effective.

Circuit Training

Circuit training is another type of training that combines cardio and strength training. Circuit training involves performing a series of exercises with little or no rest in between. This type of training is great for burning calories and building lean muscle mass. Personally, I enjoy doing circuit training because it keeps me engaged and motivated.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio and strength training?

A: It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio exercise per week. You should also do strength training exercises at least two days per week.

Q: Can I lose weight by doing cardio only?

A: Yes, you can lose weight by doing cardio only. However, you will get better results if you combine cardio and strength training.

Q: How long does it take to see results from weight loss training?

A: It depends on various factors like your diet, exercise routine, and metabolism. However, you should start seeing results within a few weeks if you are consistent with your training and diet.

Q: Do I need to join a gym to do weight loss training?

A: No, you don't need to join a gym to do weight loss training. You can do bodyweight exercises at home or go for a run outside. However, joining a gym can give you access to equipment and classes that can help you achieve your weight loss goals.

Conclusion of What Type of Training is Best for Weight Loss

Weight loss can be challenging, but finding the best type of training can make a significant difference. A combination of cardio and strength training is the best type of training for weight loss because it helps to burn calories during the workout and build lean muscle mass, which burns more calories even when you are not working out. You can choose from various types of cardio and strength training exercises like running, weightlifting, HIIT, and circuit training. Whatever type of training you choose, make sure you are consistent and enjoy what you do. Remember, weight loss is a journey, not a destination.
