Does Weight Lifting Count As Aerobic Exercise

Written by Petter Nov 27, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who is looking to get fit but is confused about whether weight lifting is a good form of aerobic exercise? Do you want to know if weight lifting can help you lose weight and improve your cardiovascular health? If so, then this article is for you!

Weight lifting is a popular form of exercise that is known for its ability to build muscle and increase strength. However, many people wonder if weight lifting can also count as aerobic exercise. Aerobic exercise, also known as cardio, is any type of activity that increases your heart rate and improves your respiratory system. It is an important element of a well-rounded exercise routine, as it can help you burn calories, improve your endurance, and reduce your risk of chronic diseases.

The short answer to whether weight lifting counts as aerobic exercise is no. Weight lifting is generally considered an anaerobic exercise, which means it does not rely on oxygen to produce energy. While weight lifting can still provide some cardiovascular benefits, it is not as effective at improving your aerobic fitness as other forms of exercise like running, cycling, or swimming.

In conclusion, while weight lifting is a great way to build muscle and increase strength, it is not the best form of aerobic exercise. If you are looking to improve your cardiovascular health, it is best to incorporate some form of aerobic exercise into your routine. However, that doesn't mean you should avoid weight lifting altogether. In fact, weight lifting can be a great complement to your aerobic workout, helping you build lean muscle mass and burn more calories overall.

Why is Aerobic Exercise Important?

Before we dive into why weight lifting isn't considered aerobic exercise, let's take a moment to understand why aerobic exercise is important. Aerobic exercise has been shown to have numerous health benefits, including:

  • Reducing the risk of heart disease, stroke, and diabetes
  • Improving lung function and respiratory health
  • Boosting mood and reducing stress levels
  • Helping you lose weight and maintain a healthy body weight
  • Increasing your energy levels and improving sleep quality

As you can see, aerobic exercise is an essential component of a healthy lifestyle. While weight lifting can provide some of these benefits, it is not as effective at improving your cardiovascular health as other forms of exercise.

How to Incorporate Aerobic Exercise into Your Routine

If you are looking to incorporate aerobic exercise into your routine, there are many forms of exercise to choose from. Some popular options include:

  • Running or jogging
  • Cycling or spinning
  • Swimming or water aerobics
  • Dancing or aerobics classes
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)

When incorporating aerobic exercise into your routine, it is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise most days of the week for optimal health benefits.

The Bottom Line

In conclusion, weight lifting does not count as aerobic exercise. While weight lifting can provide some cardiovascular benefits, it is not as effective at improving your aerobic fitness as other forms of exercise. Question and Answer

Q: Can weight lifting help me lose weight?

A: Yes, weight lifting can help you lose weight by building lean muscle mass and increasing your metabolism.

Q: How often should I incorporate weight lifting into my routine?

A: It is recommended to incorporate weight lifting at least 2-3 times per week for optimal results.

Q: Can I do weight lifting and aerobic exercise on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do weight lifting and aerobic exercise on the same day. Just be sure to prioritize your aerobic workout if you are looking to improve your cardiovascular health.

Q: Can I do weight lifting and aerobic exercise if I have a chronic condition?

A: It is best to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine, especially if you have a chronic condition. Your doctor can help you determine what types of exercise are safe and appropriate for you.

Conclusion of Does Weight Lifting Count as Aerobic Exercise

Weight lifting is a great form of exercise that can help you build muscle and increase strength. That being said, weight lifting can still be a great complement to your aerobic workout, helping you build lean muscle mass and burn more calories overall. Remember to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts for optimal health benefits.
