Best Cardio Hiit Moves For A Killer Workout

Written by Robby Jun 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Looking for a way to burn calories and get your heart pumping? There's no better way to do it than with a high-intensity interval training (HIIT) workout. HIIT is a type of workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. Not only is it a great way to burn fat, but it also improves your cardiovascular health and boosts your metabolism. But with so many HIIT moves out there, which ones are the best for a killer workout? In this post, we'll share some of the top cardio HIIT moves to help you get the most out of your workout.

Do you feel like you've hit a plateau in your workout routine? Or maybe you're struggling to find the right moves that will challenge you and keep you motivated. Whatever your pain points are, incorporating cardio HIIT moves into your routine can help you break through those barriers and take your fitness to the next level.

1. Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a classic cardio move that never goes out of style. They're a great way to warm up your body and get your heart rate up. To do a jumping jack, start with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Jump up and spread your legs out wide while raising your arms over your head. Then jump back to the starting position. Repeat for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

2. Burpees

Burpees are a full-body exercise that will get your heart pumping and your muscles working. To do a burpee, start in a standing position. Drop down into a plank position, do a push-up, jump your feet back up to your hands, and then jump up into the air. Repeat for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

3. Mountain Climbers

Mountain climbers are a great way to target your core while getting your heart rate up. To do a mountain climber, start in a plank position. Bring one knee up to your chest, then switch and bring the other knee up to your chest. Repeat as fast as you can for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

4. High Knees

High knees are a great way to work your legs and get your heart rate up. To do high knees, stand in place and bring your knees up to your chest one at a time, as if you're running in place. Repeat for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

5. Jump Squats

Jump squats are a great way to work your legs and get your heart rate up. To do a jump squat, start in a squat position. Jump up in the air as high as you can, then land back in the squat position. Repeat for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

6. Jump Rope

Jumping rope is a classic cardio move that can be done anywhere. It's a great way to get your heart rate up and work your entire body. To jump rope, start with the rope behind your feet. Swing the rope over your head and jump over it as it comes back around. Repeat for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Why are these moves effective for cardio HIIT?

These moves are effective for cardio HIIT because they all involve short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. This type of training has been shown to be more effective at burning fat and improving cardiovascular health than steady-state cardio. By incorporating these moves into your routine, you can challenge your body and get the most out of your workout.

How can I incorporate these moves into my routine?

You can incorporate these moves into your routine by doing them in a circuit. Do each move for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then rest for 30 seconds to 1 minute. Repeat the circuit 3-5 times for a killer cardio HIIT workout.

7. Plyometric Lunges

Plyometric lunges are a great way to work your legs and get your heart rate up. To do a plyometric lunge, start in a lunge position. Jump up in the air and switch legs, landing in a lunge position with the other leg forward. Repeat for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do a cardio HIIT workout?

A: You should aim to do a cardio HIIT workout 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between.

Q: Can I modify these moves if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes! You can modify these moves by doing them at a slower pace or by doing a modified version of the move. For example, you can do a modified push-up instead of a full push-up.

Q: Do I need any equipment to do these moves?

A: No! These moves can all be done with just your bodyweight. However, if you want to challenge yourself even more, you can add weights or resistance bands.

Q: Are there any precautions I should take before doing these moves?

A: As with any exercise program, it's important to consult with your doctor before starting. You should also make sure to warm up properly before doing any intense exercise.

Conclusion of Best Cardio HIIT Moves

Cardio HIIT moves are a great way to challenge your body and improve your fitness. By incorporating these moves into your routine, you can burn fat, improve your cardiovascular health, and boost your metabolism. Try doing these moves in a circuit for a killer cardio HIIT workout that will leave you feeling energized and accomplished.
