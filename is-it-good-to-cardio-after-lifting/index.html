Is It Good To Do Cardio After Lifting

Written by Eveline Jul 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly to build muscles or lose weight? If yes, you might have heard different opinions about whether to do cardio after lifting. Some say it's a perfect way to burn more calories while others believe it can harm your gains. So, what should you do? Let's find out!

Pain Points Related to Doing Cardio After Lifting

There are several pain points that people face when it comes to doing cardio after lifting. For instance, some people feel exhausted and drained out after lifting, which makes it challenging for them to do cardio. Others worry that cardio might affect their muscle gains and hinder their progress. Some also believe that doing cardio after lifting can lead to injuries.

Answering the Question: Is it Good to Do Cardio After Lifting?

The answer to this question is a bit complicated. Doing cardio after lifting can be good or bad, depending on your fitness goals and preferences. If your primary goal is to lose weight or improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio after lifting can be beneficial. It can help you burn more calories and improve your heart health.

However, if your goal is to build muscles and gain strength, doing cardio after lifting might not be the best idea. Cardio can deplete your energy levels and affect your muscle recovery, which can hinder your progress. In such cases, it's best to do cardio on separate days or at least a few hours apart from lifting.

Summary of Main Points

So, to sum up, whether or not you should do cardio after lifting depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight or improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio after lifting can be beneficial. However, if your goal is to build muscles and gain strength, doing cardio after lifting might not be the best idea as it can affect your muscle recovery and hinder your progress.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Lifting

Personally, I find doing cardio after lifting to be beneficial for my fitness goals. As someone who wants to maintain a healthy weight and improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio after lifting helps me burn more calories and improve my heart health. I usually do 20-30 minutes of cardio after lifting weights, and it leaves me feeling energized and refreshed.

Moreover, doing cardio after lifting can also help me relieve stress and boost my mood. It's a great way to unwind after a strenuous lifting session and feel good about myself.

Possible Drawbacks of Doing Cardio After Lifting

While I find doing cardio after lifting to be beneficial, it's not always the case for everyone. Some people might experience fatigue, exhaustion, or even injuries when they do cardio after lifting. Moreover, doing cardio after lifting can also affect your muscle recovery and hinder your progress if you're trying to build muscles.

Factors to Consider Before Doing Cardio After Lifting

If you're considering doing cardio after lifting, there are a few factors you should keep in mind. Firstly, it's essential to listen to your body and not push yourself beyond your limits. If you feel exhausted or drained out after lifting, it's best to skip cardio or do it on a separate day.

Secondly, it's essential to consider your fitness goals and preferences. If your goal is to build muscles and gain strength, doing cardio after lifting might not be the best idea. However, if you want to lose weight or improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio after lifting can be beneficial.

Tips for Doing Cardio After Lifting

If you decide to do cardio after lifting, here are a few tips that can help you get the most out of your workout:

  • Start with low-intensity cardio and gradually increase your intensity and duration over time.
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with the right nutrients before and after your workout.
  • Stretch and warm up your muscles before lifting and cardio to prevent injuries.
  • Listen to your body and take rest days when you need them.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can doing cardio after lifting help me lose weight?

A: Yes, doing cardio after lifting can help you burn more calories and lose weight.

Q: Will doing cardio after lifting affect my muscle gains?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscles and gain strength, doing cardio after lifting might not be the best idea as it can affect your muscle recovery and hinder your progress.

Q: Should I do cardio on the same day as lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and preferences. If your goal is to lose weight or improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio on the same day as lifting can be beneficial. However, if your goal is to build muscles and gain strength, it's best to do cardio on separate days or at least a few hours apart from lifting.

Q: How long should I do cardio after lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're a beginner, start with 10-15 minutes of low-intensity cardio and gradually increase your duration over time. If you're more advanced, you can do 20-30 minutes of moderate to high-intensity cardio after lifting.

Conclusion of Is it Good to Do Cardio After Lifting

In conclusion, whether or not you should do cardio after lifting depends on your fitness goals and preferences. If your goal is to lose weight or improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio after lifting can be beneficial. However, if your goal is to build muscles and gain strength, doing cardio after lifting might not be the best idea as it can affect your muscle recovery and hinder your progress. Ultimately, it's essential to listen to your body, consider your goals, and make the best decision for your fitness journey.
